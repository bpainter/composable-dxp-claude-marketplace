# CX canon reading list

The books this plugin treats as authoritative. Each entry maps the book to the skills that should reach for it first.

## Service design

**Stickdorn, Hormess, Lawrence, Schneider — *This Is Service Design Doing* (2018).** The handbook. Process, methods, facilitation, implementation. → `cx-service-designer`, `cx-journey-mapper`, supporting reads for `cx-customer-research`, `cx-customer-feedback-synthesizer`.

**Stickdorn & Schneider — *This Is Service Design Thinking* (2011).** The earlier theoretical companion. Five principles (user-centred, co-creative, sequencing, evidencing, holistic), tool inventory. → Theoretical grounding for `cx-service-designer` and `cx-journey-mapper`.

## Jobs-to-be-Done

**Ulwick — *Jobs to be Done: Theory to Practice* (2016).** Outcome-Driven Innovation. The needs framework, the universal job map, desired-outcome statement format, the opportunity algorithm. → `cx-jobs-to-be-done` (canonical methodology), `cx-customer-research` (interview design for ODI).

**Kalbach — *The Jobs To Be Done Playbook* (2020).** Practical plays — Switch interviews, Four Forces, job maps, job stories, JTBD-driven roadmaps. The pluralist counterpart to Ulwick. → `cx-jobs-to-be-done` (techniques), `cx-customer-research` (Switch method), `cx-journey-mapper` (job map vs. journey map distinction).

## Experience orchestration

**Risdon & Quattlebaum — *Orchestrating Experiences* (2018).** Channels, touchpoints, ecosystems, journeys, experience maps, experience principles, opportunities. The most useful single book for crossing the gap from research to design vision. → `cx-journey-mapper`, `cx-service-designer`, `cx-information-architect`. Shared reading.

## Information architecture

**Covert — *How to Make Sense of Any Mess* (2014).** Sensemaking as a discipline. Identify the mess, state intent, face reality, choose direction, measure distance, play with structure, prepare to adjust. Best primer in the field. → `cx-information-architect` (canonical), `cx-service-designer` (sensemaking on complex services).

## Complexity and design

**Norman — *Living with Complexity* (2010).** Complexity is necessary; bad design makes it complicated. Conceptual models, social signifiers, system design, the design of waits, managing complexity as a partnership between designer and user. → `cx-service-designer`, `cx-journey-mapper`, `cx-information-architect`.

## Sense and respond

**Gothelf & Seiden — *Sense and Respond* (2017).** Continuous two-way conversation with the market. Outcomes over outputs, hypothesis-driven discovery, OKR-based steering, organizing for learning. → `cx-customer-feedback-synthesizer`, `cx-customer-research`, paired with `cx-personalization-strategist` for measurement loops.

## Shared with product-management plugin

These two books span CX and product-management. The canonical digests live in the product-management plugin so both plugins point to one source of truth. Reach for them when CX work bleeds into product strategy or roadmap craft.

**Banfield, Eriksson, Walkingshaw — *Product Leadership* (2017).** Leadership cascade (principles → vision → strategy → roadmap), three stages of product leadership style, partner ecology, hiring timing. → CX cross-references: journey-mapper (themes-on-roadmap pairing), JTBD (vision-then-jobs sequencing), customer-research (avoiding sanitized data at enterprise scale), service-designer (partner ecology). Full digest: [[book-product-leadership]].

**Lombardo, McCarthy, Ryan, Connors — *Product Roadmaps Relaunched* (2017).** Theme-driven roadmap structure, components (vision, objectives, themes, timeframes, disclaimer), three-question stakeholder filter, prioritization frameworks (Kano, DFV, ROI scorecard, MoSCoW, Cost of Delay). → CX cross-references: themes ARE jobs at the roadmap level (JTBD), journey-map opportunities feed candidate themes (journey-mapper), feedback synthesis enables the stakeholder filter (feedback-synthesizer). Full digest: [[book-product-roadmaps-relaunched]].

## How to use this canon

- **Don't paraphrase a book into the vault.** Capture the concept, framework, or template in a focused reference; cite the source.
- **One book is rarely enough.** JTBD work uses Ulwick *and* Kalbach. Service design uses Stickdorn *and* Norman. Pair them.
- **Book references live next to the skill they inform.** Plugin-shared reads live here in `cx/references/`. Skill-specific extracts live in `cx/skills/<skill>/references/`.
