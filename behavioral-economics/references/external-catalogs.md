# External Catalogs — Decision Lab & BeSci

**Online reference catalogs** that extend our book digests and templates with comprehensive, regularly-updated bias and tactic libraries. Cite these when a skill needs broader coverage than what's in Kahneman, Thaler & Sunstein, Ariely, or Wendel.

## The Decision Lab — Biases Index

**URL:** https://thedecisionlab.com/biases

The Decision Lab is a Canadian applied-research firm; their biases index is the most-used online catalog of cognitive biases. Each bias entry typically includes:

- Definition
- Why it happens (cognitive mechanism)
- Why it matters (consequences)
- How to avoid / leverage it
- Example
- How it was discovered (with academic citations)
- Related concepts

**Coverage** — ~100+ biases, including everything in our book digests plus extensions like:

- Bandwagon effect
- Bystander effect
- Curse of knowledge
- Decoy effect (and asymmetric dominance)
- Dunning-Kruger effect
- False consensus effect
- Fundamental attribution error
- Gambler's fallacy
- IKEA effect
- Just-world hypothesis
- Mere exposure effect
- Negativity bias
- Pareto principle (heuristic application)
- Pessimism bias
- Pygmalion effect
- Reactance
- Self-serving bias
- Survivorship bias
- Zeigarnik effect
- ...and dozens more not covered in any single book

**When to cite it:**

- A skill needs to reference a bias that isn't in the four book digests
- The user wants a comprehensive scan of "what biases could be in play here"
- Building training material or shared vocabulary with a team

**How to cite:**

> The Decision Lab. "Bandwagon Effect." Accessed May 2026. https://thedecisionlab.com/biases/bandwagon-effect

## The Decision Lab — Reference Guide

**URL:** https://thedecisionlab.com/reference-guide

A broader reference covering biases, heuristics, theories, key figures, and policy applications. Includes:

- Theories (Prospect Theory, Dual-Process, Hyperbolic Discounting, etc.)
- Heuristics (Anchoring, Availability, Representativeness, Affect, etc.)
- Key figures (Kahneman, Tversky, Thaler, Sunstein, Ariely, Wendel, Fogg, etc.)
- Policy applications (Nudge units, behavioral insights teams)
- Methodologies (RCTs, A/B testing, behavioral economics in research design)

**When to cite it:** when a skill needs to point at a *theory* or *figure* rather than a single bias.

## BeSci — Tactics Catalog

**URL:** https://besci.org/c/tactics

BeSci (Behavioral Science) is a community-built catalog focused on **applied tactics** — the design moves that operationalize behavioral insights. Where The Decision Lab catalogues biases (problems), BeSci catalogues tactics (solutions). Each tactic entry typically includes:

- Definition
- Mechanism (which bias or heuristic it leverages)
- Examples in the wild
- When to use vs. when not to use
- Evidence base (research links)

**Coverage** — many of the tactics in our `template-intervention-design.md` come from this catalog, plus extensions like:

- Active choice / required choice
- Anchoring
- Attribute framing
- Behavioral checklists
- Choice architecture
- Commitment devices
- Default options
- Friction (productive friction)
- Gamification
- Goal setting
- Implementation intentions
- Incentivization
- Loss aversion (as design tactic)
- Mental contrasting
- Personalization
- Pre-commitment
- Priming
- Reminders
- Salience
- Simplification
- Social norms (descriptive and injunctive)
- Stimulus control
- Streaks
- ...and more

**When to cite it:**

- Designing an intervention and you need broader options than the eight Slalom tactics in `template-intervention-design.md`
- Cross-referencing a tactic to its evidence base
- Comparing tactics across domains (health, finance, sustainability, education, civic engagement)

**How to cite:**

> BeSci. "Implementation Intentions." Accessed May 2026. https://besci.org/c/tactics/implementation-intentions

## Pitfalls

- **Citing the catalog in place of the underlying research.** The catalogs are *index* — they point at the academic source. When precision matters, follow the citation to the original paper.
- **Treating the catalog as exhaustive.** New biases and tactics are named all the time; the catalogs add but rarely retire entries. Some "biases" you'll find are weak findings or not well-replicated.
- **Drift toward bias-naming as analysis.** Naming a bias is a hypothesis, not a diagnosis. Validate that the bias is actually in play before designing around it. (See pitfalls in `template-nudges-biases.md`.)
