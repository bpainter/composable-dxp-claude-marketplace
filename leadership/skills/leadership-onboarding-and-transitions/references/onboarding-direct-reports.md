# Onboarding direct reports

The flip side of the first-90-days framework: when you're the manager of someone who's transitioning *to* you. The structure helps prevent the common pattern where a hire that looked great in the loop "wasn't a good fit" by month 4.

## The premise

Onboarding is a designed process, not "they'll figure it out." A senior hire's first 90 days has a specific architecture: front-loaded learning, then small ownership, then meaningful work. The manager's job is to design the architecture, set the cadence, and watch for early-warning signals.

Most onboarding failures are management failures. The hire didn't get clear expectations, didn't get a buddy, didn't get the cadence they needed, didn't get told when something was off. They left or underperformed and the team blamed the hire.

## Pre-day-1 (week before start)

What the manager does *before* the new hire shows up:

1. **Write the 30/60/90 plan.** Tied to the Performance Profile from the hiring loop. See `T_30_60_90.md` in `70_Templates/`.
2. **Block the first-week calendar:**
   - Day 1: 60 min with you (welcome, plan, working agreements)
   - Days 1–5: 15-min daily check-ins
   - Week 1: introductions to 4–6 key people (peers, cross-functional partners, key clients)
   - Week 1: their first owned deliverable specified
3. **Assign an onboarding buddy.** A peer (not their manager) who answers the "stupid" questions and surfaces early-warning signals.
4. **Send the new hire:** the Performance Profile, the 30/60/90 draft, key reading, the first-week schedule, who they'll be meeting and why.
5. **Confirm logistics:** laptop, accounts, access, badge, any onboarding cohort scheduling, expense card, travel approval.
6. **Tell the team.** Email or staff meeting announcement, with the new hire's name, role, what they'll be doing, and when they start.

## Day 1

90 minutes with you, focused on:
- Welcome (genuinely; this matters more than people admit)
- Walk through the 30/60/90 plan (it's a draft — they can push back, you'll iterate)
- Working agreements: how you communicate, how often you'll meet, how you handle disagreement, how you give feedback
- The first owned deliverable for the week
- Logistics: where to ask what kind of question (channels, docs, people)
- Their questions

End-of-day quick check-in (5 min): how was day 1? Anything blocked?

## Week 1

Daily 15-min check-ins:
- What did you work on today?
- What are you learning?
- What's blocked?
- Who do you need to meet that you haven't?

Plus the scheduled introductions and the first owned deliverable.

By end of week 1, the new hire should:
- Know who their key partners are
- Have their environment set up
- Have started on a small but real piece of work
- Feel oriented enough to know what to do Monday of week 2

## Weeks 2–4

30-min check-ins, 3x/week, tapering. Topics shift:
- Real work progressing
- Relationships forming
- Patterns the new hire is noticing about how the team works
- Anything they're confused about

By end of week 4, the new hire should:
- Have shipped or contributed materially to one piece of work
- Have a working relationship with their key partners
- Have started to form their own read of the team and the role
- Feel like they're "in" the team, not "joining"

**Week 4 explicit check-in (the manager's audit):**
- Are they on the 30/60/90 plan?
- Are peers reporting confidence in their work?
- Is the relationship with the team forming?
- Are there early-warning signals (see below)?

If concerns: discuss directly with the new hire and adjust the plan.

## Weeks 5–8

Weekly 1:1, MTOM format (see `manager-tools-one-on-one.md` in `leadership-meetings-and-cadences`). Real work owned. New hire is starting to be the person they were hired to be.

By end of week 8:
- They own meaningful work tied to a Performance Profile bullet
- They contribute in staff meetings and broader forums
- They're known by the team and adjacent stakeholders
- They've gotten and given some feedback

**Week 8 audit (more substantive than week 4):**
- Are they delivering against the 30/60/90?
- What's their read of the role at this point?
- Where are they struggling?
- What feedback have they received from peers (you should be soliciting this)?

This is the right point for any course-correction conversation. If something's not working, name it now — week 8 is recoverable; week 16 is much harder.

## Weeks 9–12

Weekly 1:1s. The 30/60/90 review at week 12 is the moment of truth.

**Week 12 explicit review:**
- Walk the 30/60/90 plan together. What's been delivered? What's slipped?
- What did the new hire get right that we should keep doing?
- What did we get wrong that we'd change?
- What does the next 90 days look like? (Often: phasing into normal performance management cadence.)
- Are they on track to deliver against the Performance Profile? Honest read.

This is also the moment to formally close the onboarding period. The 1:1 cadence transitions to the standard manager rhythm. The buddy relationship can taper (or continue informally).

## The 30/60/90 plan — what's in it

See `T_30_60_90.md` in `70_Templates/` for the canonical template. The structure:

**First 30 days (learning + relationships + low-risk wins):**
- Specific things they'll know (people, systems, current state of work)
- Specific people they'll have met (with named outcomes — not "meet 10 people" but "have working understanding of [team A]'s work")
- One small owned deliverable, low-risk, good for early credibility

**First 60 days (small ownership):**
- They own a defined piece of work, contributing to active delivery
- They have a public read of one substantive area
- They've given and received feedback in 1:1s

**First 90 days (meaningful ownership):**
- They own work directly tied to one or more Performance Profile bullets
- They've made one visible contribution the team noticed
- They've identified what *they* think the next 90 days should look like

Keep the plan to 1–2 pages. Specific. Outcome-based. Co-owned with the new hire (they shape it; you don't impose it).

## Early-warning signals

In the first 90 days, watch for:

- **Quiet beyond week 4.** Could be still ramping; could be struggling. Check in.
- **First owned deliverable slips, twice.** Beyond ordinary slippage. Could be capacity, could be skill gap, could be misunderstanding the priority.
- **Peers don't know what they do.** A direct quote from a 1:1 with a peer: "I'm not sure what [new hire] has been working on" — that's a signal.
- **They stop asking questions.** Often a worse sign than asking too many. May mean they've decided you can't help.
- **They're not on calendar with anyone outside immediate team by week 6.** Networking is stalling.
- **Negative read from buddy.** The buddy has a different relationship; sometimes they catch what you can't.

These don't always mean a bad hire — sometimes a slower ramp, a personality difference, a team-fit issue you can address. But they always mean a conversation. Don't wait.

## Course-correction or exit

If at week 8 you have a real concern:
1. **Direct conversation with the new hire.** Cite specifics. Don't soften so much they don't hear it.
2. **Reset the 30/60/90 if needed.** Adjust expectations to what's realistic.
3. **Give it 4 more weeks** (through week 12).
4. **Decide at week 12.** If the trajectory is up, continue. If not, exit.

The early decision is harder but cheaper. A bad hire who lingers 6+ months damages the team and the person. A clean exit at month 3 is recoverable for everyone.

## Onboarding cohort

If you're hiring 3+ people in a quarter, do an onboarding cohort:
- Weekly cohort session (30–45 min) for the first 6–8 weeks
- Shared content (firm overview, practice overview, key clients, key processes)
- Cohort buddies in addition to peer buddies
- Reduces individual manager load and creates peer support among new hires

## Common manager failures

| Failure | Fix |
|---|---|
| "They'll figure it out" | Write the 30/60/90 before they start |
| No buddy | Assign one, brief them, check in on the buddy relationship at week 4 |
| 1:1 cadence is the same as for senior tenured reports | Front-load: daily then 3x weekly then weekly |
| No introductions to key partners | Build the intro list pre-day-1; schedule the meetings yourself |
| Vague expectations | Performance Profile + 30/60/90 + week-by-week deliverables |
| Course-correction conversation deferred | Have it at week 8; don't wait |
| 30/60/90 plan written and never revisited | Review at week 4, week 8, week 12 explicitly |
