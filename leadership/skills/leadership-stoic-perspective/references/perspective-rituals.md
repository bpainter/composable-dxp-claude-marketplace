# Perspective rituals — the calendar architecture

Stoic perspective work only persists as designed habit. Without ritual structure, it's intention without practice. With it, the techniques compound over years.

This reference is the calendar architecture for the perspective practice — what to do daily, weekly, quarterly, annually, and how to start without overcommitting.

## The principle: small + consistent + structured

The leaders who actually maintain a perspective practice over years do small amounts daily, with explicit structure, in calendar-blocked slots. The leaders who try to "set aside an hour for reflection when I have time" do it once and never again.

5 minutes a day, every day, beats 60 minutes once a quarter.

## Daily — the morning ritual (5–10 min)

**Time:** First 5–10 min after waking, before the inbox. Coffee or tea acceptable; phone screen no.

**Format:**

1. **Negative visualization (60 sec).** "If [the thing I most rely on — role, health, family member] were gone today, what would I miss most? What would I have left undone?"
2. **Today's anxieties — dichotomy check (60 sec).** What am I most anxious about today? Of that, what's mine? What isn't? My internal goal for the controllable part is X.
3. **Today's intention (60 sec).** What's the one thing I want to bring to today? Patience with [person]? Presence in the [meeting]? Clarity on the [decision]?
4. **Optional: a stoic line of reading (3 min).** Marcus Aurelius's *Meditations*, Epictetus's *Enchiridion*, Seneca's letters, or a modern adaptation. Read one passage; don't try to read more.

The whole thing takes 5–10 min. Block it in calendar like a meeting.

## Daily — the end-of-day reflection (5–10 min)

**Time:** End of work day, before transitioning to evening / family.

**Format:**

1. **What was today like?** (60 sec to summarize.)
2. **Where did I react vs. respond?** Where did I get pulled out of equanimity? What triggered it?
3. **What did I waste energy on that wasn't mine to control?** Sort honestly.
4. **What's the one thing I'd do differently if I had it again?**
5. **What am I grateful for from today?** Specific. One thing. Often a small interaction or a moment of clarity.

Either written (a single page in a notebook or doc) or just thought through. Written is more lasting — see `T_Daily_Note.md` in `70_Templates/`.

## Weekly — Friday review (30 min)

**Time:** End of work week. Often pairs with the team's Friday celebrate ritual but is *separate* personal time.

**Format:**

1. **What pattern do I see this week in my reactions?** (Same person triggered me 3x; same kind of decision made me anxious every time; same source of energy waste.)
2. **What did I focus well on that was controllable?** Where did I bring my best work?
3. **What's drifting in the practice?** (Cadence, perspective, personal disciplines.)
4. **What's the next-week intention?** One specific perspective focus for the upcoming week.

This is also a good slot for the Big Rocks / Q2 review (see `q2-prioritization-big-rocks.md` in `leadership-goals-and-okrs`).

## Monthly — first-Friday read (60 min)

**Time:** Once a month. Slightly longer slot than the weekly.

**Format:**

1. **The arc of the month — am I drifting from intention?** Compare what you've done to the values / direction you wanted to be operating from.
2. **Identity-vs-role check.** Did the role consume too much identity this month? Did I take time outside the role?
3. **Voluntary discomfort — did I have any?** If no, schedule one.
4. **The advice-and-counsel network — am I reaching out enough?** Schedule a coffee or call with someone in your network.
5. **One Stoic technique to focus on next month.** (Negative visualization, dichotomy, voluntary discomfort, last-time meditation, view from above.)

## Quarterly — identity-vs-role retro (half-day)

**Time:** Once a quarter. Half-day, off-grid if possible.

**Format:**

1. **Walk the quarter.** What happened, what did I build, what did I let drift?
2. **The identity question.** Am I serving the role or being run by it? Specific evidence either way.
3. **The hedgehog check.** Am I focused on what matters most, or scattering across plausible-but-not-priority work?
4. **The relationships check.** The people who matter to me — am I investing enough? Or has the role crowded them out?
5. **The personal disciplines check.** Sleep, exercise, recovery, vacation taken. Honest scoring 1–5 each.
6. **The brutal facts.** What's the data I've been avoiding about my own work, my own health, my own role?
7. **Next quarter's perspective focus.** One specific practice to deepen.

This pairs naturally with quarterly OKR planning. The identity work is upstream of the work decisions.

## Annual — the life-arc reflection (full day)

**Time:** Once a year. Often around year-end or birthday. Full day, ideally outside normal environment (a long walk, a cabin, a park).

**Format:**

1. **The year that was.** What happened? What changed? What did you build, lose, learn?
2. **The 5-year arc.** Where were you 5 years ago? Where will you be 5 years from now? Are you on the trajectory you want?
3. **The 25-year arc.** Imagine yourself looking back from 25 years from now. What would you most want to be true about how you spent the next year?
4. **The negative visualization at scale.** If you knew this was your last year of full health, what would you do differently?
5. **The relationships review.** Who are the people who matter most? How are those relationships? What's needed?
6. **The values check.** What did you say you cared about a year ago? Did your behavior match? What needs to change?
7. **The next year's intention.** Not goals (those are OKRs). Intentions — how you want to be. What you want the year to be about.

Some leaders write this up; some just walk and think. Either works. The discipline is the time and the seriousness.

## Starting from zero

If you're not currently doing any of this, don't try to start all of it at once. The pattern that works:

**Week 1–2:** Just the morning ritual (5 min). Nothing else.
**Week 3–4:** Add the end-of-day reflection.
**Month 2:** Add the weekly Friday review.
**Month 3:** Add the monthly first-Friday read.
**Month 6:** First quarterly retro.
**Year 1:** First annual reflection.

If you try to install all six rhythms in week 1, you'll fail at all of them and reinforce the belief that "this stuff doesn't work for me."

## Defending the slots

Like all Q2 work, the perspective rituals will be displaced by Q1 + Q3 if you don't defend them. Specific tactics:

- **Calendar block as "unavailable."** Not "tentative."
- **Same time every day** for the daily rituals (forms the habit).
- **Don't email-check until the morning ritual is done.** The inbox is a perspective-killer.
- **Phone in another room** during the morning ritual.
- **The weekly Friday review is non-negotiable** even when the week is bad.
- **Quarterly retro** scheduled at the start of each quarter for the entire year (so it's blocked before the calendar fills).
- **Annual reflection** scheduled 12 months out (vacation-equivalent priority).

## When you fall off

You will. Everyone does.

The discipline is not perfect maintenance. The discipline is *resuming without self-criticism* when you fall off. A week missed isn't a failure; it's a week missed. Resume Monday morning.

The trap: missing 3 days, feeling shame, deciding "I'll start fresh next week," missing 3 weeks, deciding "I'll start fresh in the new year," missing the practice for 6 months.

Just resume. Tomorrow morning. 5 min.

## For neurodivergent leaders specifically

Per the `leadership-executive-coach` skill, neurodivergent (ADHD / autistic) leaders often struggle most with the daily-habit aspect. The structure is more important, not less. Specific aids:

- Calendar blocks with notifications
- Visual cues (notebook on the desk, app open)
- Accountability partner (someone you check in with weekly on the practice)
- Permission to vary the format if you do the practice
- Coupling with an existing habit (morning coffee → ritual; end-of-workday → ritual)

The neurodivergent brain often needs the structure to be more explicit and more redundant than the neurotypical brain. Build in the supports.
