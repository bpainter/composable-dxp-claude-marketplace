# Debrief facilitation script

For the hiring manager. 45-minute slot, well-run, produces a real decision instead of a vote.

## Pre-debrief (30 minutes before)

- Confirm every interviewer has filed their scorecard
- Read all scorecards yourself
- Note where leans diverge (which bullets, which interviewers)
- Identify the 1–2 bullets that need the most discussion
- Decide your own lean based on evidence so far (commit to it before the debrief; you'll update if new evidence emerges)

## The 45 minutes

### 0:00–0:05 — Round of leans, no rationale

> "Quick round — what's your lean? Hire, no-hire, or unsure. Just the lean — we'll get into rationale next."

Go around. Capture leans publicly (whiteboard, doc, whatever).

### 0:05–0:10 — Frame the discussion

> "Here's what I'm seeing in the room: [N hires], [N no-hires], [N unsures]. The bullets where we have the most divergence are [bullet A] and [bullet B]. I want to walk the Performance Profile bullet by bullet. For each bullet, the owner cites evidence first. Others add. Then we'll close out the bullet and move on. We'll come back to the disagreements at the end."

### 0:10–0:30 — Bullet-by-bullet walk

For each bullet (3–6 bullets, roughly 3–5 minutes each):

> "Bullet [N]: [read it aloud]. [Owner], what did you hear?"

Owner cites evidence. Specific. Quote-level.

> "Anyone else hear something on this bullet?"

Open the floor briefly. Cap to 1–2 additional contributions per bullet.

> "Where are we landing on bullet [N]?"

Confirm the bullet's evidence as a group. Move on.

### 0:30–0:38 — Probe the divergence

For bullets where leans diverged most:

> "[Strongest no-hire on this bullet], walk me through what you heard or didn't hear that's making you lean no."
> "[Strongest hire on the same bullet], does that change anything for you, or do you have evidence the other way?"

Force evidence on both sides. The hiring manager probes. Sometimes the no-hire is right and the loop missed something. Sometimes the no-hire is gut and falls apart under evidence.

### 0:38–0:42 — Identify gaps

> "What bullets did we underprobe? What evidence do we wish we had?"

Write down anything material. Decide whether to do a follow-up.

### 0:42–0:45 — Decision

The hiring manager makes the call:

> "Here's what I'm hearing: [summary]. My call is [hire / hire pending follow-up on X / no-hire]. Here's why: [evidence-grounded reasoning]."

If hire: move to offer. If pending: schedule the follow-up and the decision date. If no-hire: thank the loop, commit to closing the loop with the candidate within 48 hours.

## Common debrief moments and how to handle them

### "I just had a bad feeling."

> "Help me ground that. What specifically did you hear or see that's driving the feeling? If we can't ground it in a Performance Profile bullet, I want to set it aside."

Not because gut is wrong — sometimes gut catches what evidence missed. But you have to translate it into a probeable concern, otherwise it's just bias.

### "Cultural fit."

> "Help me unpack that. Do you mean [bullet 5 — collaboration]? Or do you mean something else?"

Force the abstraction down to a specific Profile concern. If it doesn't reduce to one, it's not actionable.

### "They reminded me of [previous bad hire]."

> "I hear that. Set that person aside for a minute — what specifically did this candidate say or not say that gives you concern?"

Pattern-matching to past hires is a bias trap. Force the discussion onto this candidate's actual evidence.

### Unanimous "hire" in 5 minutes.

> "Before we close — what's our biggest concern? If we hired them and they failed in 6 months, what would the failure mode be?"

Pre-mortem the unanimous hire. If no one can articulate any concern, you might have groupthink.

### Strong split (2 strong yes, 2 strong no).

> "Let's hear the strongest no first. What evidence?"
> "Now the strongest yes. What evidence?"

Don't take the average. The split usually means one side has evidence the other side doesn't. Find which.

### Hiring manager already decided before the debrief.

This happens. If you're going to do it, be honest:

> "I came in leaning hire. I want to hear the loop's evidence to test that lean — particularly any concerns I should weight harder."

This invites real dissent. The alternative (pretending to be open while signaling the verdict) trains the loop to rubber-stamp.

## Post-debrief

Within 24 hours:
- Write the decision and rationale into the candidate file (use `T_Interview_Debrief.md`)
- Communicate with the candidate (offer or close)
- Thank the loop (especially when they pushed back on you)
- Note any rubric or process issues for the next calibration session

## When you should override the loop

The hiring manager has the call. There are situations where the loop says "no" and you say "yes" (or vice versa). Use this sparingly. When you do:

- Be explicit: "I hear the loop's concerns. I'm overriding because [specific reasoning]."
- Document it. If the hire works out, it's evidence the override was warranted. If not, it's a learning case.
- Ask yourself why you brought the loop in the first place. If you override regularly, you're using interviewers as theater, and they'll stop investing.
