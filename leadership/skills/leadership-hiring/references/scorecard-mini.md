# Scorecard mini — in-conversation interview scorecard

When the agent needs to render a scorecard for an interviewer to use *right now*, use this. For the canonical, Bermon-facing template that becomes a real note in the vault, use `T_Interview_Scorecard.md` in `70_Templates/`.

## The mini scorecard

```
Candidate: [name]
Role: [role]
Interviewer: [name]
Date: [date]
Stage: [recruiter screen / hiring manager / peer / panel / close]
Bullets I owned: [list from Performance Profile]

---

Bullet [N] — [text of bullet]
Most-significant-accomplishment evidence:
- [specific quote, fact, or example]
- [specific quote, fact, or example]
Problem-solving / visualization evidence:
- [their approach to the real problem posed]
- [what they asked, what they assumed, what they proposed]
Strength on this bullet: [strong yes / lean yes / unsure / lean no / strong no]
Confidence: [1–5]

[Repeat for each bullet owned]

---

Overall lean: [hire / no-hire / unsure]
Overall confidence: [1–5]

The single biggest reason to hire:
[1–2 sentences]

The single biggest concern:
[1–2 sentences]

The one thing I want another interviewer to probe:
[open question]

Compensation / motivation signals heard (if any):
[notes]
```

## Filling it in well

- **Cite the candidate's words, not your impression.** "She said: 'I owned the migration end-to-end and ran the runbook reviews.'" not "Seemed like she had ownership."
- **Note what was *not* said.** "Asked twice about role scope; never asked about team norms." Absence is data.
- **Distinguish observation from interpretation.** "Took 8 seconds to answer the trade-off question" (observation) is different from "didn't seem confident" (interpretation).
- **Be specific on the open question.** "Wasn't able to dig into bullet 4 — recommend the Director slot probe their cross-team work" is actionable.

## Calibration

If your "strong" answers are the same as your peers' "weak" answers, the team isn't calibrated. Pull a calibration session — see `scaling-team-hiring.md`.
