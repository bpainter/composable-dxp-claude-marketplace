Use the `leadership-onboarding-and-transitions` skill to build a 90-day transition plan.

## When the user invokes this

Ask one question if not provided: **"Whose transition — yours, or a direct report's?"**

## Phase routing

### Your own first 90 days in a new role

1. Run **STARS diagnosis** for each major area of accountability:
   - **S**tart-up (build new) / **T**urnaround (save failing) / **A**ccelerated growth (scale winning) / **R**ealignment (fix drift) / **S**ustaining success (don't break)
   - Most situations are mixes — diagnose per area, not as one verdict
2. Plan the **listening tour** — who you 1:1 with in first 30 days, who skip-levels, who cross-functional, who external
3. Schedule **the five conversations with your boss:**
   - Situation diagnosis (week 2–4)
   - Expectations (week 3–6)
   - Style (week 4–8)
   - Resources (week 6–10)
   - Personal development (month 3–4)
4. Pick **early wins** (1–2 quick + 1 foundation), tested with the win matrix:
   - Visible + strategic = candidate
   - Visible + cosmetic = avoid (vanity wins burn credibility)
   - Strategic + invisible = stealth play; defer or pair with visible
5. Map the **advice and counsel network** — technical / cultural / political advisors; identify gaps
6. Build the **personal disciplines plan** — sleep, exercise, recovery, daily/weekly/quarterly rituals
7. Save as a 30/60/90 plan from `70_Templates/T_30_60_90.md` in `10_Practice/{My_Transition}/`

### Onboarding a direct report

1. Pull the role's Performance Profile from `10_Practice/People/Hiring/{Role}/_Brief.md`
2. Draft the **30/60/90 plan** tied to the Performance Profile bullets:
   - First 30 days: learning, relationships, low-risk wins
   - First 60 days: small ownership of real work
   - First 90 days: meaningful work tied to a Performance Profile bullet
3. Plan the **first-week schedule:** day-1 90-min session, daily 15-min check-ins, key intros (4–6 named people)
4. Assign an **onboarding buddy** (peer, not manager) with brief
5. Set the **manager 1:1 cadence:** daily week 1 → 3x/week weeks 2–4 → weekly weeks 5+
6. Schedule the **explicit reviews** at week 4 (early signals), week 8 (substantive), week 12 (formal review + transition to standard rhythm)
7. Save in `10_Practice/People/Reports/{Name}/_30_60_90.md`

## Don't

- Skip the STARS diagnosis on your own transition — most failures are misdiagnosed situations
- Wait until day 1 to build the new hire's plan — pre-day-1 prep is the highest-leverage work
- Promise more than you can deliver in the first 30 — under-promise; deliver the small wins on time
- Front-load too much listening for direct reports' onboarding — they need real ownership starting week 5–8
