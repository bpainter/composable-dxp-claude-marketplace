# Risk Language Alternatives for Proposals

## The Core Principle
Replace **absolute claims** and **open-ended commitments** with **defensible, measurable language** that sets clear expectations and bounds risk.

## High-Risk Language & Defensible Alternatives

### Performance Guarantees (VERY RISKY)

| Risky Language | Why It's Risky | Defensible Alternative |
|---|---|---|
| "We guarantee success" | Undefined success; unlimited liability | "We commit to delivering defined deliverables per the agreed timeline and scope" |
| "We will ensure [outcome]" | Implies unconditional delivery; unpredictable variables | "We will implement [specific approach] and support efforts to achieve [outcome], subject to assumptions outlined in Section X" |
| "We promise to..." | Creates expectation of absolute delivery | "We commit to; contingent on client cooperation and dependencies outlined herein" |
| "This will result in [X% improvement]" | Outcome depends on client execution, market factors | "Based on industry benchmarks, organizations implementing this approach typically see [X% range]; your results will depend on [specific factors]" |

### Scope & Deliverables (COMMONLY RISKY)

| Risky Language | Why It's Risky | Defensible Alternative |
|---|---|---|
| "Comprehensive solution" | Implies everything is included | "Solution covers the scope described in Section 2, limited to [specific items]" |
| "End-to-end implementation" | Undefined boundaries | "Implementation spans [specific phases: discovery, design, build, test, launch], as detailed in Section 3" |
| "As needed" | Open-ended scope creep | "Up to [specific hours/quantity], as outlined in deliverables" |
| "Full platform integration" | Assumes no limits on integrations | "Integration with [specific platform names/versions], limited to [X integrations]" |
| "Design iterations" | No revision limit | "Up to three (3) consolidated revision cycles per template, based on documented client feedback" |

### Support & Maintenance (EXTREMELY RISKY)

| Risky Language | Why It's Risky | Defensible Alternative |
|---|---|---|
| "Ongoing support" | Undefined duration, hours, scope | "Post-launch support for [X months], limited to [X hours/month], covering [specific categories]" |
| "24/7 availability" | Unsustainable commitment | "Support available [define hours: 8am-6pm, Mon-Fri], with emergency escalation process for critical issues" |
| "Unlimited fixes" | All issues are "bugs" under this language | "Remedy of defects discovered during [warranty period], defined as failures to meet documented acceptance criteria" |
| "Always available" | Implies immediate response | "Response within [X business hours] for [severity level]; 48 hours for non-critical issues" |

### Assumptions & Conditions (OFTEN OVERLOOKED)

| Risky Language | Why It's Risky | Defensible Alternative |
|---|---|---|
| "Assuming client cooperation" | Vague; hard to enforce | "Client will provide [specific deliverables: credentials, content, approvals] within [X business days] of request" |
| "Subject to external factors" | Too vague; creates disputes | "Timelines assume [specific factors remain stable]: no major scope changes, client provides timely feedback within defined windows, no regulatory changes" |
| "All necessary resources provided" | Undefined "necessary" | "Client will provide [specific resources]: access credentials within 3 business days, content in [format], [X] hours/week stakeholder availability" |
| "As soon as possible" | No accountability | "Within [X business days of client approval/feedback submission]" |

## Quantification Examples

### Deliverable Scope

**Instead of:** "Migrate website content"

**Use:** "Migrate up to 125 existing web pages from [current CMS] to [target CMS], in [target language(s)], excluding: blog posts, archived content (pre-2020), gated downloadables, third-party embedded content, and custom microsites. Redirects for migrated pages included; 404 cleanup is out of scope."

---

**Instead of:** "Design 10 templates"

**Use:** "Design 10 [specific type] templates in Figma, delivering: responsive layouts (desktop, tablet, mobile), design system integration, accessibility review against WCAG 2.1 AA, up to three (3) rounds of consolidated revisions. Updates post-launch are out of scope."

---

**Instead of:** "Conduct training"

**Use:** "Deliver four (4) instructor-led training sessions, each 2 hours, covering [topic areas], for up to 15 participants per session. Sessions recorded and made available; optional extended office hours available at additional cost."

### Timeline & Responsiveness

**Instead of:** "Provide feedback promptly"

**Use:** "Client will provide consolidated feedback and approvals within five (5) business days of submission. Timelines will extend proportionally if feedback is delayed beyond this window."

---

**Instead of:** "Quick turnaround"

**Use:** "Delivery within ten (10) business days of [milestone], subject to client provision of required inputs and approvals on schedule."

---

**Instead of:** "As needed support"

**Use:** "Hypercare support for 30 days post-launch, including [up to] 40 hours of support time, covering issue triage, workarounds, and escalation to engineering. Additional support available on a time-and-materials basis at [rate]."

### Quality & Acceptance

**Instead of:** "High-quality deliverables"

**Use:** "Deliverables will meet documented acceptance criteria, including: [specific functional requirements], adherence to [standards], documented in [format], and reviewed against [specific checklist]."

---

**Instead of:** "Tested thoroughly"

**Use:** "All deliverables will undergo [specific testing approach: unit testing, integration testing, user acceptance testing], documented in test reports. Known limitations are outlined in Section [X]."

---

**Instead of:** "Production-ready"

**Use:** "Deliverables will be ready for deployment to [specific environment], subject to [specific assumptions]: no data migration required, [third-party systems] already integrated, compliance review completed separately."

## Assumptions Framework

Every proposal should include an explicit "Assumptions" section that spells out:

1. **Client Responsibilities:**
   - Availability of stakeholders ([X hours/week])
   - Decision-making timeline ([X days for approvals])
   - Provision of assets (content, credentials, existing documentation)
   - Third-party coordination (if required)

2. **External Constraints:**
   - Regulatory environment remains stable
   - No major scope changes during engagement
   - Specified third-party systems remain available and compatible
   - Client's systems/infrastructure meet documented requirements

3. **Resource & Timeline:**
   - Team availability (no unexpected resource constraints)
   - No conflicting priorities on project resources
   - Specified timeline assumes no major unforeseen delays
   - Key team members available for duration of engagement

4. **Scope Boundaries:**
   - Work is limited to activities and deliverables described herein
   - [Specific categories] are explicitly out of scope
   - Any work outside defined scope requires a change order
   - Success metrics are defined in Section [X]

## Language Tone Guidelines

- **Be direct and professional**, not marketing-focused
- **Use simple sentence structure**, especially for complex concepts
- **Quantify whenever possible** (hours, days, deliverables, revisions)
- **Avoid absolute claims** ("always," "never," "guarantee," "ensure")
- **Use "will" for our commitments; "will support efforts to" for outcomes subject to client execution**
- **Define terms explicitly** (what is "success"? what is "testing"? what is "support"?)
- **Spell out assumptions** rather than hiding them in narrative
