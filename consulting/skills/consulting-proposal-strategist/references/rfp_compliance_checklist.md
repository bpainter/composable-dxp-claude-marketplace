# RFP Compliance & Strategy Checklist

## Pre-Drafting Analysis

### Understanding the RFP
- [ ] Identify the buyer's primary business challenge (not just stated problem—read between the lines)
- [ ] Extract all mandatory requirements (non-responsive = automatic disqualification)
- [ ] Identify all scored evaluation criteria and their relative weightings
- [ ] Calculate total available scoring points; prioritize where points are weighted highest
- [ ] Flag ambiguous, contradictory, or unrealistic RFP language
- [ ] Identify hidden scope (e.g., implied integrations, assumed regulatory work, unspoken timeline compression)
- [ ] Assess timeline realism; flag risks for delivery if timeline seems compressed
- [ ] List all evaluation committee roles (technical, financial, executive); consider how each will score

### Compliance Risk Assessment
- [ ] Does our firm have demonstrated past performance in this category?
- [ ] Are there capability gaps or risks in proposed delivery?
- [ ] Are there timeline risks (e.g., 3-month delivery, 6-month realistic need)?
- [ ] Are there pricing risks (fixed fee too tight; T&M scope too open)?
- [ ] Does our team have named individuals who can be committed for the full engagement?
- [ ] Are there subcontractor dependencies? If so, can we secure agreements before proposal submission?

### Strategic Positioning
- [ ] What are 3-5 win themes that align to buyer priorities AND our strengths?
- [ ] What are our genuine differentiators in this deal? (not generic marketing claims)
- [ ] What does past performance look like? (specific references, outcomes, team overlap)
- [ ] What concerns might evaluators have about our firm? (Address proactively, don't ignore)
- [ ] Who are the likely competitors? What's our positioning advantage?

## Proposal Structure & Drafting

### Compliance Mapping
- [ ] Build a matrix: RFP section → Our response location → How we address each requirement
- [ ] Ensure every mandatory requirement has an explicit response (don't assume "close enough")
- [ ] Use RFP language and section titles in proposal headers (makes scoring easier)
- [ ] Call out how we address each evaluation criterion within relevant sections
- [ ] Quantify compliance: "We will deliver X, subject to Y assumptions, on timeline Z"

### Executive Summary
- [ ] Demonstrate clear understanding of the buyer's business challenge
- [ ] State our approach concisely (one paragraph, not a full narrative)
- [ ] Lead with client outcomes, not our credentials
- [ ] Highlight 2-3 differentiators aligned to evaluation criteria
- [ ] Avoid marketing language; be direct and professional
- [ ] Do NOT oversell or make absolute promises

### Technical Approach
- [ ] Step-by-step methodology (make it scannable with headers and bullets)
- [ ] Define all deliverables explicitly (with quantities where relevant)
- [ ] List milestones and timeline (mapped to deliverable completion)
- [ ] Spell out assumptions and client responsibilities
- [ ] Define success metrics or acceptance criteria
- [ ] Avoid jargon; write for non-technical evaluators

### Team & Experience
- [ ] Name all key roles with specific responsibilities
- [ ] Highlight relevant past performance (be specific: client type, scope size, outcomes)
- [ ] Show team continuity (avoid high turnover on key roles)
- [ ] Match team credentials to RFP requirements explicitly
- [ ] Provide references with clear contact information

### Risk & Assumptions
- [ ] List all assumptions explicitly (don't hide them)
- [ ] Define client dependencies and required actions
- [ ] Spell out what's NOT included (prevents post-award disputes)
- [ ] Explain timeline risks; propose mitigation if timeline is aggressive
- [ ] Define success criteria or acceptance processes

## Language & Risk Control

### Eliminate Risky Language
Replace this → With this
- "Guarantee" → "Will use commercially reasonable efforts"
- "Ensure" → "Support efforts to; subject to assumptions"
- "Comprehensive" → "Limited to scope described in Section X"
- "Promise" → "Commit to; contingent on..."
- "End-to-end" → Explicitly define what's included and excluded
- "As needed" → "Up to [quantity] as outlined"
- "Ongoing" → "For [defined period], limited to [hours/scope]"

### Quantification Examples
- Instead of: "Migrate website content"
  Use: "Migrate up to 125 existing web pages from [source platform] to [target platform], excluding blogs, archives, gated assets"

- Instead of: "Design iterations"
  Use: "Up to three (3) consolidated design revision cycles per template, based on documented client feedback"

- Instead of: "Provide timely approvals"
  Use: "Client will provide consolidated written approval or feedback within five (5) business days of submission"

## Pre-Submission Review

### Content Quality
- [ ] Does every section tell a coherent story aligned to buyer priorities?
- [ ] Is language clear and professional throughout?
- [ ] Are there jargon or acronyms undefined for non-technical readers?
- [ ] Do all claims (past performance, differentiators) feel defensible?
- [ ] Are we making promises we can actually keep?

### Compliance Check
- [ ] Every RFP requirement has an explicit response
- [ ] Mandatory requirements are clearly satisfied (no creative interpretations)
- [ ] Evaluation criteria are visibly addressed in relevant sections
- [ ] Formatting and page limits match RFP instructions exactly
- [ ] Required documents (bios, references, org charts) are included
- [ ] Submission method and deadline are correct

### Consistency Review
- [ ] Executive summary aligns with technical approach
- [ ] Team bios match roles and responsibilities described
- [ ] Timeline and milestones are consistent across all sections
- [ ] Pricing aligns with scope and timeline (no obvious gaps)
- [ ] Past performance examples support claimed differentiators
- [ ] No contradictions between sections

### Risk & Language Review
- [ ] All risky language has been replaced with defensible alternatives
- [ ] Assumptions are explicit; no hidden scope
- [ ] Quantified scope (numbers, not vague language)
- [ ] Timeline is realistic given scope and resource constraints
- [ ] Pricing is defensible relative to scope and effort
- [ ] No overpromises or absolute guarantees

## Post-Win Integration

### Kickoff Alignment
- [ ] Share proposal with delivery team; align on commitments
- [ ] Identify any gaps between proposed and achievable approach
- [ ] Define scope clarification process if issues emerge
- [ ] Establish change order process if scope expands
- [ ] Confirm timeline and resource availability
- [ ] Address any concerns before formal engagement starts
