# Pipeline and Pursuit Framework

**Reference guide for sales-bd-strategist: disciplined pipeline management, lead qualification, pursuit strategy, and forecasting accuracy.**

---

## 1. Pipeline Stage Definitions & Progression Criteria

**Clear stage definitions prevent fuzzy forecasts and unqualified deals flowing to close.**

| Stage | Definition | Progression Criteria | Red Flags |
|-------|-----------|-------------------|-----------|
| **Prospect** | No active dialog; early awareness | Initial conversation scheduled or completed | No contact for 60+ days |
| **Engaged** | Initial conversation; interest confirmed | Problem/pain point discussed; Slalom value proposition acknowledged | Still "exploring" after 30 days |
| **Qualification** | Problem confirmed; budget + timeline indicated | BANT/MEDDIC framework applied; decision-maker engaged; next steps defined | Budget "TBD"; timeline >18 months out |
| **Pursuit** | Competitive bid phase; proposal submitted | Win theme developed; competition identified; active pursuit plan | No competitive intelligence |
| **Negotiation** | Commercial/legal terms in discussion | Contract sent to legal; timeline to signature <60 days | Talking months out |
| **Closed Won** | Signed agreement; engagement kicks off | All parties signed; SOW + PO in hand; project charter defined | Unsigned contracts; delayed kickoff |

**Progression gates:**
- Prospect → Engaged: Stakeholder meeting scheduled + attendance confirmed
- Engaged → Qualification: BANT questions answered (all 4 domains); decision-maker on a call
- Qualification → Pursuit: Win theme + competitive analysis documented; proposal submitted or scheduled
- Pursuit → Negotiation: Shortlisted (no more than 2 competitors); legal draft exchanged
- Negotiation → Closed Won: All signatures collected; PO number and funding confirmed
- Do NOT move to Closed until all documentation complete and funding transferred

## 2. Lead Qualification Deep-Dive

### BANT Framework (Quick Filter)

**Budget:**
- Is funding allocated (not "maybe next year")?
- How much? Is it realistic for the scope?
- Red flag: "We need to see your proposal first before we request budget." → Disqualify.
- Red flag: Budget is in procurement approval cycle with no sponsor champion → Slow-moving.

**Authority:**
- Who is the decision-maker? Not the person you're talking to, necessarily.
- Can they approve contracts? Or do they need buy-in from Finance, CEO, Board?
- Red flag: "I'll need to check with [other stakeholder]" x3 = unclear authority → Slow down; map stakeholders.

**Need:**
- Specific problem or initiative driving this? (Not a "nice-to-have")
- Have they quantified the impact? (Costs, lost revenue, inefficiency?)
- Red flag: "We're just exploring options." → Low urgency; long timeline.

**Timeline:**
- When do they want to start the engagement?
- Is there a forcing function (fiscal year end, board mandate, contract renewal)?
- Red flag: Timeline >18 months out → Too speculative; re-engage in 12 months.

**BANT decision tree:**
- All 4 clear and positive → Qualified; move to Pursuit
- 1 missing → Qualification stage; gather the gap
- 2+ missing → Disqualify or engage at much lower priority (Prospect tier)

### MEDDIC Framework (Deep Qualification for $1M+ Pursuits)

Use when opportunity is high-value, complex, or competitive.

**Metric (Success Measurement):**
- How will they measure success? Specific, quantified KPI?
- Example: "Reduce time-to-market by 30%" or "Consolidate platforms to 3 from 12"
- If they can't articulate this → They haven't socialized it internally; low commitment
- Use metric in proposals to tie recommendations to their outcome

**Economic Buyer (Budget Authority):**
- Not the sponsor; the person who controls the money
- Often: CFO, VP Finance, or department head with P&L authority
- Must engage economic buyer directly; sponsor alone is insufficient
- Red flag: "The sponsor will convince Finance for us" → You're doing their job; not qualified

**Decision Criteria (Evaluation Framework):**
- What's their evaluation framework? (Price, capability, vendor stability, references?)
- Are criteria weighted? (Do they care more about speed or cost?)
- Red flag: Criteria undefined → They haven't aligned internally; proposal will be judged inconsistently

**Decision Process (Steps & Timeline):**
- Timeline from now to signed contract? (Realistic; not aspirational)
- How many approvals? (Committee? Board? Vendor management review?)
- What's the next milestone? (RFP release? Proposal due? Presentation?)
- Red flag: "We'll tell you when we're ready" → No discipline; likely to slip

**Identify Pain (Business Impact):**
- What's the quantified impact of the problem? (Cost, inefficiency, risk, lost revenue?)
- Can they articulate why it matters now vs. later?
- Red flag: Problem is vague or downplayed → Not urgent enough to fund

**Champion (Internal Advocate):**
- Ideally someone with budget authority OR strong political influence
- Champions help navigate politics, introduce to economic buyer, advocate for you internally
- Must be someone who benefits from the solution AND has credibility
- Red flag: No champion, or champion is junior → Slower, more difficult pursuit

**MEDDIC decision:**
- 5-6 clear → Strong opportunity; confidence high; move forward
- 3-4 clear → Moderate; gaps need closure before heavy investment
- <3 clear → Weak; deprioritize or disqualify

### SPICED Framework (Modern Alternative)

Use if MEDDIC feels formulaic; more narrative and outcome-focused.

| Element | Definition | Key Questions |
|---------|-----------|---|
| **Situation** | Their business context & current state | What's their industry? Size? Current tech/process? |
| **Problem** | Specific pain point we can address | What's broken? What's frustrating them? |
| **Implication** | Why does this problem matter? | What's the business impact? Cost of doing nothing? |
| **Consequence** | Downstream effects; urgency creation | What happens if they don't solve it? Board risk? Competitive threat? |
| **Economic Impact** | Quantified value; ROI | How much do they lose monthly? What's the budget to fix it? |
| **Decision** | How will they choose a vendor? | Timeline? Approval process? Evaluation criteria? Champion? |

**SPICED vs. MEDDIC**: SPICED is more conversational; MEDDIC is more structured. Use SPICED if you're discovering; use MEDDIC if you're validating a complex opportunity.

## 3. Disqualification Criteria

**Be ruthless. Walking away saves time and maintains pipeline health.**

| Trigger | Why Disqualify | Action |
|---------|---|---|
| Budget not allocated | You can't compete against their other priorities | Disqualify; re-engage in 12 months if conditions change |
| No decision-maker | You're building on sand | Ask for introduction to economic buyer; if no, disqualify |
| Timeline >18 months | Too speculative; priority will shift | Disqualify; calendar reminder for 12 months out |
| "We need free consulting first" | They're not serious or not qualified | Disqualify; they're kicking tires |
| Problem outside Slalom capability | Mismatch; will result in lost deal or scope creep | Disqualify; refer to partner if possible |
| Margin economics don't work | Effort:value ratio too high | Disqualify; partner on it instead |
| Hostile competitor (e.g., locked in with Big Four) | Switching costs too high | Disqualify; track for recompete |
| No internal champion at all | Who advocates for you? No one. | Deprioritize to Prospect tier; wait for champion to emerge |
| Talking to you only to negotiate with current vendor | Leverage play, not real pursuit | Disqualify; they're using you as leverage |
| Org is changing (CTO leaving, restructuring) | Volatility; decisions will be delayed | Deprioritize; wait for dust to settle |

**Disqualification language:** "Based on [X], this isn't the right fit for us or Slalom right now. Let's stay in touch; if [condition] changes, we'd love to revisit."

## 4. Account Planning & Territory Mapping

### Account Segmentation

**Strategic Accounts:**
- Revenue: $2M+
- Multi-practice potential (Data + Strategy + Build)
- C-suite relationships
- 3+ year engagement horizon
- Assigned dedicated account leader + exec sponsor

**Growth Accounts:**
- Revenue: $500K–$2M
- Single practice or light expansion potential
- Director-level relationships
- 2-year horizon
- Shared account leader

**Transactional:**
- Revenue: <$500K
- Single project; limited upsell
- Maintain; don't overinvest
- Account manager tier

### Account Planning Template

**Account Name:**
- Current revenue (Slalom), growth rate
- Industry/vertical, customer size
- Slalom practice presence: [Practice] $[Revenue], [Practice] $[Revenue]
- Strategic fit: why does Slalom care about this account?

**Organizational Structure:**
- Key stakeholder map: Title, responsibility, Slalom relationship (yes/no), Power-Interest rating
- Example: VP Technology (High Power, High Interest) → Manage closely
- Identify sponsor (champion), economic buyer, user community

**Strategic Initiatives (Next 18 Months):**
- Business drivers: market/competitive pressure, board mandate, internal optimization
- Technology priorities: cloud migration, data modernization, digital transformation, etc.
- Where does Slalom fit? (Which initiative aligns with our services?)

**Current Competitive Landscape:**
- Who else plays here? (AWS, Google Cloud, Deloitte, Accenture, etc.)
- Where are we strong vs. weak?
- Proof points: relevant case studies, references, previous wins in this vertical

**Win Themes (Account-Specific, Not Generic):**
- Theme 1: [Outcome] enabled by [Approach], proven by [Case study/Reference]
- Theme 2: [Outcome] enabled by [Approach], proven by [Case study/Reference]
- Themes should be 1-2 sentences, compelling, tied to their strategic initiative

**Expansion Roadmap (12-Month):**
- Q1: [Opportunity/Initiative]; value: $[X]; champion: [Name]
- Q2: [Opportunity/Initiative]; value: $[X]; champion: [Name]
- Q3: [Opportunity/Initiative]; value: $[X]; champion: [Name]
- Q4: [Opportunity/Initiative]; value: $[X]; champion: [Name]

**Relationship Plan:**
- Quarterly business reviews: schedule + attendees
- Executive touchpoints: MD or Partner visits, frequency
- Value-add: insights shared (not pitches), articles sent, introductions made

## 5. Pursuit Strategy & Win Themes

### Competitive Analysis

**For each named competitor, document:**

1. **Their Positioning:** What do they claim to do well? (Speed, expertise, price, breadth, partnership)
2. **Our Positioning:** Where do we win? (Composable, execution-focused, local presence, no lock-in)
3. **Proof Points:** Case studies, references, previous wins vs. this competitor
4. **Likely Weaknesses:** Where might they struggle in this deal? (Price, execution risk, vendor lock-in)
5. **Our Counter-Messaging:** How do we position our strength vs. their weakness?

**Example:**
- Competitor: Deloitte
- Their positioning: Global reach, Big Four prestige, full-stack services
- Our positioning: Local-first, execution-focused, no bureaucracy, best-of-breed (not proprietary)
- Proof points: Case study of legacy SFRA → headless migration faster than Deloitte's COTS approach
- Their weakness: Slow decision-making, high overhead, vendor lock-in to proprietary tools
- Our counter-message: "We deliver faster and leaner with no vendor lock-in"

### Win Theme Development

**3-Step Process:**

1. **Research the Account**
   - What's their strategic initiative? (From account plan)
   - What's the business outcome they care about? (Speed, cost, quality, risk reduction)
   - How does our approach deliver that outcome better than competitors?

2. **Craft the Theme (1-2 sentences, outcome-focused, not feature-focused)**
   - Weak: "We do composable architecture well."
   - Strong: "Composable architecture reduces your time-to-market by 40% while maintaining flexibility to evolve."
   - Weak: "We have best-of-breed partners."
   - Strong: "Best-of-breed integration with no vendor lock-in means you own your roadmap, not your vendor."

3. **Validate with a Case Study or Reference**
   - Can you point to a similar client where this theme played out?
   - Can you offer a reference call from a similar company?
   - "We did this for [Company] in [Vertical]; happy to connect you with [Contact]."

**Number of Win Themes:** 2-3 per pursuit. More is noise; fewer leaves money on table.

### Proposal Strategy

**Structure (Tailored to Their Situation, Not Templated):**

1. **Problem Restatement** (Shows you listened)
   - "You told us X challenge. We understand why this matters: Y impact. Here's what we'd do: Z approach."

2. **Proposed Approach** (Tailored to Their Needs, Not Generic)
   - Phased timeline (not one big bang)
   - Resource model (dedicated team? Embedded? Hybrid?)
   - Success metrics tied to their KPIs (not generic delivery metrics)

3. **Team & Credentials** (Positioned to Their Specific Needs)
   - Don't list everyone; highlight who leads, their relevant experience
   - References: Ideally from their vertical or similar challenge
   - Avoid: Long bios of everyone on the team

4. **Success Metrics** (Quantified, Aligned to Their Outcome)
   - Not: "Deliver platform as specified"
   - Yes: "Reduce time-to-market for new commerce features by 6 weeks; maintain 99.9% uptime"

5. **Commercial Terms** (Pricing, Timeline, Deliverables)
   - Be explicit: What's in/out? What's the timeline? What's the cost structure?
   - Value-based pricing (when appropriate): "If you achieve 40% faster launch, let's share upside"

6. **Competitive Positioning** (Without Slamming Competitors)**
   - "Unlike [Competitor] who typically lock you into their proprietary platform, we use best-of-breed and give you portability."
   - Avoid negativity; focus on what makes us different.

## 6. Pipeline Forecasting & Analytics

### Metrics to Track

| Metric | Formula | Target | What It Means |
|--------|---------|--------|---|
| **Coverage Ratio** | Pipeline / Quota | 3.0x+ | Enough pipeline to hit quota 3x over |
| **Qualification Rate** | Pursuit stage / All opportunities | 60%+ | How much pipeline is actually qualified |
| **Win Rate** | Closed Won / Closed Won + Lost | 40%+ | Conversion efficiency |
| **Average Deal Size** | Total revenue / # of deals | Trend upward | Bigger deals (usually easier to close) |
| **Sales Cycle Length** | Close date - First contact | Shorter is better | How long deals take (track by vertical/practice) |
| **Forecast Accuracy** | Pipeline forecast vs. actual closed | 90%+ | How good are your predictions? |
| **Churn Rate** | Lost deals / opportunities | Lower is better | How many deals are you losing? |

### Pipeline Report Template (Weekly or Bi-Weekly)

**Executive Summary:**
- This week's closed deals (revenue, count, champion)
- Pipeline total: $[X]M
- Forecast this quarter: $[Y]M
- Coverage ratio: [X]x
- Red flags: [List any disqualifications, stalled deals, emerging risks]

**By Stage:**

| Prospect | — | $— | — | — | — |
| Engaged | — | $— | — | — | — |
| Qualification | — | $— | — | — | — |

| Negotiation | — | $— | 85% | $— | [Red flags] |
| **Total** | — | **$—** | — | **$—** | — |

**Trending:**
- Pipeline change week-over-week (up/down)
- New opportunities added
- Disqualifications
- Stage progression

**Risk & Mitigation:**
- Top 3 at-risk deals (why? what's the mitigation?)
- Upcoming closures (on track? risks?)
- Staffing/resource constraints that might impact pursuit quality

### Forecast Discipline

**Probability Assignment (Not Gut, Data-Driven):**
- Prospect/Engaged: 10%
- Qualification: 30%
- Pursuit: 60%
- Negotiation: 85%
- Closed Won: 100%

Adjust within stage based on:
- Qualification strength (MEDDIC score)
- Competition intensity
- Stakeholder alignment
- Timeline realism

**Sandbagging Prevention:**
- No "upside" pipeline; if it's not real, don't include it
- Deals that slip from one quarter to next are reported honestly
- Forecast accuracy is more valuable than inflated numbers
