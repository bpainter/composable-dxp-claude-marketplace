# Statement of Work (SOW) & Engagement Design Template

## 1. Executive Summary
2-3 paragraph overview of the engagement, client problem, and value proposition.

**Example:**
"[Client] is undertaking a digital transformation to modernize their order-to-cash process. Current manual workflows are creating fulfillment delays and customer dissatisfaction. This engagement will diagnose root causes of fulfillment bottlenecks and recommend an integrated technology and process roadmap to improve speed and accuracy. Expected outcome: 30% reduction in fulfillment cycle time and clearer technology investment roadmap."

## 2. Business Context & Problem Statement

### Client Situation
Brief description of client business, market position, and current challenges.

**Template:**
- Industry and business model
- Current business metrics (revenue, growth, customer base)
- Primary pain point or strategic challenge
- Why this matters now (competitive pressure, customer feedback, operational crisis?)

### Problem Definition
Clear, specific description of what we're solving for (not vague).

**Avoid:** "Improve digital transformation"
**Better:** "Reduce order fulfillment cycle time from 7 days to 3 days, and improve fulfillment accuracy from 92% to 97%"

**Define Success Metrics Upfront:**
- KPI 1: Fulfillment cycle time (current: 7 days → target: 3 days)
- KPI 2: Fulfillment accuracy (current: 92% → target: 97%)
- KPI 3: Customer satisfaction with delivery (current: NPS 35 → target: NPS 55)

## 3. Engagement Objectives & Scope

### Primary Objective
The singular, most important outcome of this engagement.

**Example:**
"Diagnose root causes of fulfillment delays and develop a detailed roadmap for process and technology improvements that will reduce fulfillment cycle time by 30% and improve accuracy by 5%."

### Scope of Work
What's included in this engagement:

**In Scope:**
- Document current order-to-cash process (interviews + observation of 3 warehouses)
- Benchmark fulfillment performance vs. industry best practice
- Identify root causes of delays and errors (system limitations, process gaps, skill gaps)
- Evaluate technology solutions (3-5 vendor assessments)
- Develop 2-3 process/technology roadmap options
- Recommend optimal roadmap with phased implementation plan
- Develop implementation playbook and change readiness assessment

**Out of Scope:**
- Detailed implementation and project management
- Vendor contract negotiation
- Staff reductions or organizational restructuring planning
- Technical architecture and build planning

### Timeline & Phases
Clear phases with start/end dates and deliverables:

**Phase 1: Discovery & Assessment (Weeks 1-3)**
- Deliverables: Current process map, interview notes, fulfillment benchmark analysis
- Timeline: Jan 15 - Feb 5
- Effort: 200 hours consulting time
- Client involvement: 10 interviews with operations, warehouse, IT teams

**Phase 2: Root Cause Analysis & Options Development (Weeks 4-6)**
- Deliverables: Root cause analysis, 3-4 process/technology options with trade-off analysis
- Timeline: Feb 8 - Feb 26
- Effort: 150 hours consulting time
- Client involvement: 2 workshop sessions (option validation, vendor demo observations)

**Phase 3: Recommendation & Implementation Planning (Weeks 7-8)**
- Deliverables: Strategic recommendation, detailed implementation roadmap, change readiness assessment
- Timeline: Feb 29 - Mar 14
- Effort: 120 hours consulting time
- Client involvement: Executive readout, implementation planning workshop

**Phase 4: Executive Presentation & Knowledge Transfer (Weeks 8-9)**
- Deliverables: Executive presentation, implementation playbook, team coaching
- Timeline: Mar 17 - Mar 21
- Effort: 50 hours consulting time
- Client involvement: Executive steering session, internal team workshop

**Total Duration:** 9 weeks
**Total Consulting Effort:** 520 hours
**Estimated Cost:** $[X based on hourly rate or fixed fee]

## 4. Approach & Methodology

### Discovery & Assessment
Describe your diagnostic approach and how you'll gather evidence:

- **Process Documentation**: Interview workflow participants; map current state end-to-end
- **Root Cause Analysis**: Use "5 Whys" and force-field analysis to identify systemic issues
- **Data Analysis**: Quantify fulfillment cycle time distribution, error rates by type, by warehouse
- **Benchmarking**: Compare metrics against industry best practices and peer organizations
- **Technology Assessment**: Evaluate leading solutions (3-5 vendors) against requirements
- **Stakeholder Perspective**: Understand how different teams (ops, IT, finance, customer service) see the problem

### Analysis & Recommendation Development
- Synthesize findings into root cause themes (process, technology, skills, governance)
- Develop 2-3 strategic options (each with pros, cons, and implementation requirements)
- Model financial impact of each option (cost, timeline, expected ROI)
- Facilitate workshops to validate findings and get stakeholder input on options
- Develop recommendation with clear rationale and risk mitigation

### Implementation Planning
- Create phased roadmap (quick wins in Phase 1, medium-term in Phase 2, etc.)
- Define roles and accountability for implementation
- Assess organizational change readiness (culture, skills, sponsorship)
- Develop change management and adoption plan
- Create implementation playbook and key decision points

## 6. Governance & Decision-Making

### Engagement Steering Committee
Primary decision-making body for engagement:

**Members:** VP Operations (sponsor), CIO, Chief Financial Officer, Consulting Engagement Lead
**Cadence:** Weekly 30-minute check-ins + 2-hour reviews at end of each phase
**Responsibilities:**
- Approve scope and approach
- Provide timely client input (interviews, data access, feedback)
- Review interim deliverables and provide direction
- Approve final recommendation
- Commit resources for implementation planning

### Escalation Path
How we handle blockers:

- **Operational Issues** (data access, interview scheduling): Escalate to VP Operations
- **Strategic Direction**: Escalate to Engagement Sponsor + CFO
- **Scope Creep**: Flag in weekly update; formal scope change process

### Decision Gates

**Gate 1: End of Phase 1 (Discovery)**
- Have we understood the problem correctly?
- Are we on track with our hypotheses?
- Should we adjust approach for Phase 2?
- **Decision:** Proceed to Phase 2 or reprioritize discovery

**Gate 2: End of Phase 2 (Options)**
- Are the options clear and viable?
- Do they represent distinct strategic directions?
- Ready to develop detailed recommendation?
- **Decision:** Proceed to Phase 3 or further refine options

**Gate 3: End of Phase 3 (Recommendation)**
- Is the recommendation supported by evidence?
- Can the client execute it with available resources?
- Do stakeholders align with direction?
- **Decision:** Approve recommendation; commit to implementation planning

## 7. Team & Resources

### Consulting Team
- **Engagement Lead**: Partner level; overall strategy and executive interface
- **Senior Consultant**: Day-to-day discovery and analysis
- **Analyst**: Process documentation, data analysis, benchmarking
- **Project Coordinator**: Scheduling, logistics, document management

### Client Resources Required
- **VP Operations** (Sponsor): 4 hours/week (steering, decisions, escalations)
- **Operations Manager** (Process Expert): 8 hours/week (interviews, data, validation)
- **IT Director**: 4 hours/week (technology assessment, vendor coordination)
- **Finance Analyst**: 4 hours/week (cost/benefit analysis support)
- **Warehouse Manager** (Observations): 8 hours/week (process documentation, pilot site)

## 8. Assumptions & Dependencies

### Key Assumptions
- Client will provide timely access to data and key personnel
- Current fulfillment process documentation is available (or can be quickly recreated)
- Key stakeholders (Operations, IT, Finance) will participate in interviews and workshops
- Client has 2-3 warehouse locations that represent operational patterns
- Decision-making authority is clear and available within the steering committee

### Dependencies
- Availability of historical fulfillment data (cycle time, error rates by type, by location)
- Cooperation from warehouse staff for process observation
- IT system documentation and vendor sales team availability for assessment
- Executive sponsor available for weekly steering and phase gate decisions

### Risks & Mitigation

| Risk | Impact | Probability | Mitigation |
|------|--------|-------------|-----------|
| Stakeholder resistance to process changes | Delays in discovery and buy-in | Medium | Early stakeholder engagement; acknowledge concerns; show peer examples |
| IT systems documentation unavailable | Limits technology assessment | Low | Consult with vendors; reverse-engineer from user experience if needed |
| Executive sponsor becomes unavailable | Decisions delayed; momentum lost | Low | Identify backup sponsor upfront; establish clear escalation path |
| Scope creep (requests to fix unrelated issues) | Budget overrun, schedule slip | Medium | Formal scope change process; document what's in/out; regular steering updates |

## 10. Success Criteria

**Engagement Success** (did we do good consulting?):
- ✓ Root causes clearly identified and validated with stakeholders
- ✓ Strategic options are distinct and represent meaningful trade-offs
- ✓ Recommendation is data-driven and implementable
- ✓ Stakeholders align with recommendation
- ✓ Implementation roadmap is detailed and accountable

**Business Success** (did the recommendation create value?):
- ✓ Fulfillment cycle time reduced to 3 days (target) within 12 months
- ✓ Fulfillment accuracy improved to 97% (target) within 6 months
- ✓ Customer satisfaction (NPS) improves to 55+ within 12 months
- ✓ Technology roadmap is clear and funded
- ✓ Team is trained and operating with new processes
