# Jobs-to-be-Done (JTBD) Discovery Framework

## Core Concept

A "job" is the functional, emotional, or social task a customer is trying to accomplish in a given circumstance. Not the product they buy, but the progress they seek.

**Key Insight**: Customers don't want products; they want the progress those products enable.

## Job Discovery Process

### Phase 1: Circumstance Mapping (2-3 weeks)

Understand **when**, **where**, **why**, and **how** the customer experiences the job.

**Interview Guide Questions:**
- What triggered you to address this need recently?
- Walk me through the last time you did [job]. What were you trying to accomplish?
- What made this the right time to address this problem?
- What was the situation at home/work/life that prompted this?
- What would have happened if you hadn't done anything?

**Capture (for each scenario):**
- Context and trigger event
- Customer's desired outcome
- Current solution or workaround
- Frustrations with current approach
- Competing solutions (product, partial solution, doing nothing)

### Phase 2: Job Stories (Structured Format)

Template: **"When [circumstance], I want [action/motivation], so that [outcome/aspiration]."**

Examples:
- "When I'm planning a family vacation and feeling overwhelmed by options, I want a tool that curates recommendations based on our budget and interests, so that I can make decisions quickly without research fatigue."
- "When I'm managing multiple vendor relationships and losing track of contracts, I want a centralized system that alerts me before renewal dates, so that I can negotiate better terms and avoid overpaying."

**Job Story Components:**
1. **Circumstance**: Context triggering the need
2. **Motivation**: What the customer wants to do or achieve
3. **Expected Outcome**: The progress or aspiration

### Phase 3: Competing Solutions Mapping

What does the customer do **today** to address this job?

- **Products**: Existing competitor products, adjacent category products
- **Workarounds**: Manual processes, spreadsheets, hiring consultants
- **Status quo**: Tolerating inefficiency, doing nothing
- **Hiring decisions**: When/why they choose each solution based on circumstance

Example Matrix:
| Circumstance | Current Solution | Strengths | Weaknesses | Unmet Needs |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Planning with limited budget | Spreadsheet + blog research | Free, flexible | Time-consuming, outdated info | Curated recommendations + price tracking |
| Evaluating complex options | Call a friend/mentor | Personalized advice | Bias, limited expertise | Unbiased comparison, quantified tradeoffs |
| Managing ongoing needs | Spreadsheet alerts | All-in-one | Manual updates, easy to miss | Automated alerts + renewal reminders |

### Phase 4: Unmet Needs Articulation

Prioritize by customer value and uniqueness:

**High-Value Unmet Needs:**
- Functional: What capabilities are missing?
- Emotional: What anxiety or frustration persists?
- Social: What status or identity concerns exist?

Example ranking:
1. **Reduce decision anxiety** (emotional) - Critical for choice-heavy scenarios
2. **Save time on research** (functional) - Functional efficiency
3. **Avoid overpaying** (financial) - Cost optimization
4. **Feel confident in decision** (emotional) - Decision satisfaction

## Innovation Opportunity Mapping

### Opportunity Canvas

For each unmet need, assess innovation potential:

| Unmet Need | Customer Segment | Market Size | Competitive Gap | Implementation Complexity | Opportunity Score |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Reduce decision anxiety | Families planning vacations | $10B+ leisure market | No AI-powered recommendation | Medium | HIGH |
| Avoid overpaying | Mid-market procurement | $500B+ supplier mgt market | Fragmented solutions | High | MEDIUM |
| Personalized budgeting | Freelancers + gig workers | $100B+ self-employed market | Generic accounting tools | Low | HIGH |

## Innovation Opportunity Examples

### Case 1: B2B Contract Management
**Job**: "When I'm managing vendor contracts across departments and worried about missed renewals, I want automated tracking and alerts, so that I can negotiate better terms and avoid surprises."

**Unmet Needs**:
- Emotional: Confidence that nothing is slipping through cracks
- Functional: Single source of truth across departments
- Social: Visibility to stakeholders without spreadsheet hell

**Solution Opportunity**: Centralized contract intelligence platform with auto-renewal alerts, negotiation playbooks, and cross-functional dashboards

### Case 2: Consumer Financial Wellness
**Job**: "When I'm living paycheck-to-paycheck and worried about unexpected expenses, I want a way to safely save without strict budgets, so that I can build confidence and financial stability."

**Unmet Needs**:
- Emotional: Control and agency (vs. judgment)
- Functional: Flexible, guilt-free savings mechanism
- Social: Privacy and dignity in financial management

**Solution Opportunity**: App that gamifies micro-savings and emergency fund building without shame or complexity

## Discovery Facilitation Tips

**Do:**
- Listen for circumstance and trigger events (not general complaints)
- Dig into "why" - go 3-4 layers deep
- Ask about recent actual experiences, not hypotheticals
- Observe how customer describe frustrations (language reveals emotional jobs)
- Map multiple scenarios - job varies by circumstance

**Don't:**
- Ask about product preferences ("Would you pay for this feature?")
- Suggest solutions during discovery phase
- Aggregate jobs too early - keep scenarios distinct
- Assume job is the same for all customer segments
- Confuse customer frustrations with true jobs

## Organizing Discovery Insights

**Output Structure**:
1. **Job Stories** (5-8 unique, circumstance-based stories)
2. **Competing Solutions Map** (what they do today)
3. **Unmet Needs List** (ranked by value + rarity)
4. **Innovation Opportunity Scorecard** (which needs are biggest opportunities?)
5. **Recommended Research Directions** (where to focus next)

**Tools**:
- Affinity mapping (group job stories by theme)
- Journey maps (visualize customer steps and emotional moments)
- Opportunity scorecard (prioritize which needs to address)
- Prototyping prompts (how might we address unmet need X?)
