# Innovation Charter — Template

The 8-section foundational artifact every real innovation lab, center, venture studio, or innovation function should have. A charter without all 8 sections becomes a permanent department; that's how labs become operating units that resist disruption rather than enable it.

## When to use

- Standing up a new innovation lab / center / studio
- Re-chartering a failing or drifting lab
- Designing the operating model for an internal innovation function
- Sponsor / executive sign-off on what innovation is *for*

## The charter

```
═════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
  INNOVATION CHARTER — [Lab / Center / Function name]
  Effective: [date]   Sponsor: [name, title]
═════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

1. MANDATE (one sentence)
─────────────────────────
"[Lab name] exists to [protect / enable / build] [what], for [whom],
because [why now]."

Mandate type: ☐ Defend the core    ☐ Find next-S-curve
              ☐ Build new business  ☐ Out-compete a faster rival
              ☐ Reposition the brand


2. SCOPE (what's in / what's out — esp. what's out)
───────────────────────────────────────────────────
IN scope:
  • [explicit categories of work]
  • [...]

OUT of scope (the discipline test):
  • [what we will NOT do, even when asked]
  • [...]

Engagement boundaries: [how outside teams request lab work]


3. ARCHETYPE
────────────
☐ Discovery Lab (research / JTBD / validation; doesn't ship products)
☐ Venture Studio (builds new businesses; separate P&L when needed)
☐ Center of Excellence (owns a capability; serves the org)
☐ Customer Experience Center (physical/virtual; demos and co-creation)
☐ Corporate Accelerator (cohort programs with external startups)
☐ Hybrid: [name two — no more]


4. FUNDING MODEL
────────────────
Annual budget: $[X]M
Funding source: [corporate / BU chargeback / outcome-share / hybrid]
Multi-year commitment: [years] (≥3 years required)
Independent budget line: ☐ Yes (not subject to BU cost cuts)
Performance-based advancement: [describe staged-funding pattern]


5. DECISION RIGHTS
──────────────────
Lab can decide alone:
  • [bet selection within H[X] zone, up to $X funding tier]
  • [methodology choices — discovery, validation, pretotyping]
  • [talent selection within rotational seats]

Requires escalation to sponsor:
  • [bets above $X funding tier]
  • [external partnerships above $X]
  • [strategic pivots]

Requires steering committee:
  • [annual budget changes]
  • [new lab archetype]
  • [scope changes]


6. TALENT MODEL
───────────────
Permanent vs. rotational mix: [60/40 typical]
Rotation duration: [12–24 months typical]
Entry process: [how people enter the lab]
Exit / re-absorption: [where rotators return — critical to design]
Compensation philosophy: [aligned to corporate / market / hybrid]

Ten Faces coverage (Kelley) — required roles:
  ☐ Anthropologist   ☐ Experimenter      ☐ Cross-Pollinator
  ☐ Hurdler          ☐ Collaborator      ☐ Director
  ☐ Experience Arch. ☐ Set Designer      ☐ Caregiver
  ☐ Storyteller


7. KPIs (by year)
─────────────────
Year 1 — capability and learning:
  • Capability built: [talent, IP, partnerships, customer relationships]
  • Experiments per quarter: [N]
  • Insights produced: [N]
  • Org reach: [# BUs engaged]
  • Customer experiences delivered: [N]

Year 2 — bets advancing:
  • Bets advanced through gates: [N]
  • Pilot launches: [N]
  • Validated learning: [hypotheses tested / invalidated]
  • Ecosystem partnerships: [N]

Year 3+ — outcomes:
  • Option value secured: $[X]M
  • Revenue from H2/H3 bets: $[X]M (if applicable)
  • Capability sustained
  • Talent flow: [N rotators in, N out, N to operating BUs]

Note: revenue KPIs in year 1 = lab is being killed before it starts.
Negotiate this at charter signing.


8. SPONSORSHIP & SUNSET
───────────────────────
Executive sponsor: [name, title]
Reporting line: [where the lab sits — CEO / COO / CSO / CIO]
Steering committee: [composition]
Sponsor commitment: [meetings/quarter, escalation availability,
                    political cover]
Successor sponsor: [name — name them in advance, before they're needed]

Sunset criteria (when to retire / restructure):
  • 18+ months with no advanced bets (Discovery lab)
  • 30+ months with no validated business (Venture studio)
  • Capability not adopted by 50%+ of org (Center of Excellence)
  • Customer engagement metric below baseline (Experience center)
  • Sponsor changed twice without clear succession

Re-charter cadence: [annual / biennial review]
═════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

SIGNATURES:
  Sponsor: ___________________  Date: __________
  Lab Lead: __________________  Date: __________
  CFO / Controller: __________  Date: __________  (acknowledges
                                                   funding model,
                                                   not metrics)
═════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
```

## Diagnostic prompts during chartering

1. **What protects what?** If the charter doesn't answer this, the lab will get absorbed back into the operating BU within 24 months.
2. **Is the multi-year funding committed in writing?** Verbal commitment dies with the next budget cycle.
3. **Are KPIs horizon-appropriate?** Revenue in year 1 for an H3 lab = pre-set failure.
4. **Is the successor sponsor named?** Most lab failures correlate with sponsor exit; don't wait until it happens.
5. **Are sunset criteria explicit?** A lab that should die but doesn't poisons the next attempt.
6. **Is talent rotation designed?** Pure-permanent labs become priesthoods; pure-rotational labs lose institutional memory.
7. **Are Hurdlers and Storytellers staffed?** Most labs over-index on Anthropologists and Experimenters.

## Hand-off

Skill owners:

- `innovation-lab-architect` — primary owner; runs the chartering
- `innovation-strategist` — sets the mandate that the charter codifies
- `innovation-portfolio-architect` — designs the gate/governance the lab operates within
- `innovation-value-engineer` — sets the KPI math (capability vs. revenue)
- `innovation-leadership-coach` — coaches the sponsor relationship and succession
