# Slalom Innovation Context

How innovation work fits inside Slalom's operating model and what's distinctive about doing it here. Use this to ground client-facing recommendations and internal practice decisions.

## Slalom in one paragraph

Privately held, partner-owned consulting firm founded in 2001 in Seattle. ~12,000 employees across 49 offices. Positioned between the Big Four (Deloitte, Accenture) and pure strategy firms (McKinsey, BCG). Core differentiator: **strategy to execution** — Slalom advises *and* builds. Most innovation engagements come in two flavors: (a) helping clients design innovation capability they don't have, and (b) running discovery/validation as the front-end of a build engagement.

## Where innovation fits inside Slalom

- **Capability home for innovation strategy** — primarily **Strategy** capability, with cross-pollination to **Transformation**, **CX**, **Data & AI**, and **Enterprise**.
- **Bermon's home** — **Enterprise** capability, **Composable DXP** practice. Innovation work shows up as digital experience platform reimagining, headless commerce strategy, and content operating model design.
- **Slalom Build** — In-house product engineering arm. The execution layer when an innovation bet needs to ship software. 7 Build centers. Same time-zone delivery is a competitive edge for fast learning cycles.
- **Slalom Studios** — Where applicable, the design/research/strategy front-end that often runs JTBD discovery and validation work.

## Frameworks already in play (the firm-wide stack)

- **Pursuit Excellence (PE)** — sales-stage discipline. Stages 1–6 in Salesforce. Innovation thinking shows up most in Stages 2 (Engage) and 3 (Develop) — reframing the client's problem, surfacing latent JTBDs, designing differentiated discovery proposals.
- **Slalom Summit** — delivery methodology. 6 phases (Mobilize → Discover → Deliver → Transition → E&O → Close) with 5 Risk Gates. Innovation work primarily lives in **Discover** (RG2), with operating-model design landing in early **Deliver**.
- **Delivery Excellence (DE)** — quality framework. Innovation engagements still need DE rigor — the experimentation discipline doesn't replace governance, it complements it.
- **Customer Love** — Slalom's NPS / satisfaction program. Lab and validation work should produce Customer Love evidence, not just feature deliveries.

## What to lean into (Slalom-distinctive moves)

- **Strategy + Build coupling.** When an idea is validated, Slalom can ship it. Most consulting firms hand off a roadmap. Frame innovation engagements as discovery sprint → validation → MVP build, not as standalone strategy.
- **Local-first, same time zone.** Slalom delivery is in client time zones. Innovation cycles can be tight (weekly experiments) without coordination tax. Use this in scoping.
- **Composable thinking.** Slalom's modern commerce/Enterprise practices think in MACH/composable terms. Apply this to innovation operating models too — modular labs, replaceable services, explicit interfaces between strategy/research/build.
- **Behavioral economics depth.** Bermon's BE background and Slalom's CX practice mean client innovation work can ground in BE rigor (loss aversion in adoption design, anchoring in price studies, choice architecture in onboarding).

## What to avoid (Slalom-specific failure modes)

- **Innovation theater dressed as engagement.** A "innovation workshop" with no validation plan or kill criteria isn't innovation work — it's facilitation. Always anchor to a portfolio decision.
- **Treating discovery as Phase 0 only.** Slalom Summit's Discover phase is *one* discovery cycle; innovation portfolios need *recurring* discovery. Build the recurring cadence into the operating model.
- **Confusing CX research with JTBD.** CX often lives in current-state journeys; JTBD lives in pre-product circumstances. Both have a place; don't substitute one for the other.
- **Big-bang transformation programs.** *Why Digital Transformations Fail* is in this plugin's references for a reason. 80% of transformations fail, and the failure pattern is consistent: too big, too long, not portfolio-shaped, no learning loops.
