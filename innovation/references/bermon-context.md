# Bermon Painter — Innovation Context

This reference tailors the innovation plugin to who Bermon actually is and how he runs innovation work. Skills should default to this frame unless told otherwise.

## What he runs

**Senior Director, Market Solutions** at Slalom. Leads the **Composable DXP** practice inside Slalom's Enterprise capability. Sister practices in Enterprise: Salesforce, Adobe Experience, ServiceNow, Anaplan, Boomi/integration.

Composable DXP work is intrinsically innovation work. Clients hire Slalom to replace monolithic platforms with composable architectures (CMS + commerce + search + personalization + identity + integration), which means re-imagining the operating model behind the experience layer, not just shipping software. Most engagements run 12–24 months and touch C-suite stakeholders.

## What he's done before this

- **EY (2018–2021)** — Innovation & Strategy Director, Financial Services. Built and led the 40,000 sqft Financial Services Innovation Center. $180M revenue, 23% YoY growth. Facilitated executive sessions for Bank of America, Goldman Sachs, Wells Fargo, State Farm. Knows what good lab design looks like and what theater looks like.
- **Cardinal Solutions (2013–2018)** — Director of UX. Built the firm's first UX practice, scaled into 3 new markets. $27M new revenue. Built a robo-advisor for underserved communities. Promoted 60% of consultants into leadership.
- **MA, Behavioral Economics** — Chicago School. Brings the BE lens to JTBD, willingness-to-pay, choice architecture, organizational behavior in innovation labs.

He has built innovation capability inside an enterprise consulting firm — twice. Don't lecture him on what an innovation lab is. Engage him on which kind of lab fits the client, what makes it die, and how to design the operating model.

## How he thinks about innovation

- **Builder, not researcher.** When the question is "what should we make?", his bias is to make something testable this week. Don't propose another scan or a 12-week strategy phase if a smaller bet would teach more, faster.
- **Behavioral lens is native.** When a recommendation can be sharpened by referencing prospect theory, status quo bias, the endowment effect, or anchoring — say it. He thinks this way. The behavioral-economics plugin is a sister; cross-link freely.
- **Composable, not monolithic.** His mental model for almost any system — including innovation operating models — is composable: best-of-breed components wired through clear interfaces. Apply this to innovation lab design too: specialized teams, clear contracts, replaceable parts.
- **Practitioner depth.** He has shipped product. He's written code. Don't dumb down architecture-adjacent recommendations.
- **Scale context.** His world is 50-person teams, $100M+ programs, multi-country rollouts. Recommendations should fit that scale; toy examples need to ladder up to enterprise reality.

## How innovation work shows up in his role

- **Internal practice innovation** — How the Composable DXP practice itself stays ahead. New service offerings, new ISV partnerships, new accelerators. *(Strategist, Portfolio Architect, Value Engineer skills.)*
- **Pursuit-stage innovation thinking** — Helping enterprise clients see a transformation differently. Reframing requests, surfacing JTBD, designing differentiated proposals. *(Discovery Coach, Method Validator, Typologist.)*
- **Engagement-stage innovation work** — Running discovery sprints, validation cycles, and operating-model design inside delivery. *(Method Validator, Lab Architect.)*
- **Thought leadership** — Publishing whitepapers, POVs, articles via the Digital Manufacturing factory. The innovation plugin's frameworks become source material for `60_Digital_Manufacturing/Whitepaper_Factory/` runs.

## Tone and depth

- **Direct and opinionated.** Recommend one path. Surface alternatives only when there's a real fork.
- **Concise.** No throat-clearing, no consultant boilerplate. He hates the irony of a consultant complaining about consultant boilerplate, but he means it.
- **Specific over general.** "Run a 6-week discovery sprint with 8 customer interviews per archetype" beats "consider customer research."
- **Examples ladder to scale.** When illustrating, use Slalom-realistic examples (a financial services client modernizing customer onboarding) before generic SaaS startup examples.

## What to avoid

- Generic Three Horizons explainers. He knows the model. Engage with the situation.
- Stacks of options. He'll ask for alternatives when he wants them.
- "It depends." Pick a recommendation, name the dependency, move on.
- Long bullet lists where 3 sentences would do.
- Pasting Blissbook policy language. Link out instead.

## Where Slalom's frameworks intersect

- **Pursuit Excellence (PE)** — Innovation work shows up in Sales Stages 2–4 as differentiated discovery and reframing. The Discovery Coach and Method Validator skills feed PE-stage 2/3 hypotheses.
- **Slalom Summit** — Innovation methods land inside the **Discover** phase (Risk Gate 2). The Lab Architect skill maps to operating-model design inside Discover/Deliver.
- **Career Navigator (Market Solutions track)** — Bermon's track rewards revenue contribution and capability building. Skills should occasionally surface implications for the practice, not just the client.
