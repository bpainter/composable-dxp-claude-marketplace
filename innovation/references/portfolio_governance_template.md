# Innovation Portfolio Governance & Operating Model

## Portfolio Allocation Framework

### Three Horizons Model

| Horizon | Time Frame | Investment % | Focus | Examples | Success Metric |
|---------|-----------|---|---|---|---|
| **Core (H1)** | 0-12 months | 70% | Existing customer value, efficiency, margin improvement | Product updates, feature releases, cost reduction | Margin uplift, NRR, customer retention |
| **Adjacent (H2)** | 1-3 years | 20% | New markets, customer segments, business model | New service lines, geographic expansion, adjacent product | New revenue stream launch, market share gain |
| **Disruptive (H3)** | 3+ years | 10% | Transformational bets, new markets, industry redefinition | New business model, new customer job, market creation | Market validation, proof of concept |

### Innovation Ambition Matrix

Map initiatives across:
- **X-axis**: Core offering ← → New offering
- **Y-axis**: Existing customers ← → New customers

**Quadrants:**
1. **Core Excellence**: Incremental improvements for existing customers
2. **Adjacent Growth**: New offerings for existing customers OR same offerings for new customers
3. **Disruptive**: New offerings for new customer segments (highest risk, highest potential)

## Stage-Gate Governance Model

### Decision Gates (Standard for regulated/enterprise environments)

**Gate 0: Idea Screening (Intake)**
- Criteria: Strategic fit, customer problem clarity, rough feasibility
- Duration: 1-2 weeks
- Decision: Fund discovery or reject idea
- Advance Criteria: Validated problem statement, identified target customer, preliminary market size

**Gate 1: Discovery → Alpha** (Business Case)
- Criteria: Problem validated with customers, solution concept tested, business case drafted
- Duration: 4-8 weeks
- Decision: Fund MVP development or pivot idea
- Advance Criteria: 10+ customer interviews confirming need, MVC tested with target customer, revenue model outlined, resource requirements clear

**Gate 2: Alpha → Beta** (Go/No-Go)
- Criteria: MVP complete, learning metrics positive, go-to-market plan drafted
- Duration: 4-12 weeks
- Decision: Fund pilot/limited launch or kill initiative
- Advance Criteria: MVP adoption/engagement >30%, churn <10%, market size estimate updated, pricing model tested

**Gate 3: Beta → Launch**
- Criteria: Pilot results validate business model, full go-to-market plan ready
- Duration: 4-8 weeks
- Decision: Full-scale launch or harvest/retire initiative
- Advance Criteria: Pilot NPS >50 or equivalent, unit economics viable, sales/ops infrastructure ready

### Portfolio Steering Review Cadence

**Quarterly Business Review (QBR) - 2-3 hours**
- Present all active H1, H2, H3 initiatives
- Update on progress against learning metrics
- Discuss blockers and needed resource shifts
- Rebalance portfolio if market conditions change
- Approve funding for new phases or ideas

**Monthly Status Check - 45 minutes**
- High-level status on active initiatives
- Escalate blockers that need executive intervention
- Confirm resource availability and dependencies

## Learning Metrics vs. Outcome Metrics

### Learning Metrics (Week 1 - Proof of Concept)
Track whether assumptions are confirmed or invalidated:
- **Customer Validation**: # of customer interviews, % confirming problem statement
- **Concept Testing**: # of MVCs tested, user engagement rate, feature preference clarity
- **Assumption Validation**: Which of 5-8 critical assumptions have we tested? # confirmed, # invalidated
- **Speed**: Days from hypothesis to test, # experiments per week, cycle time

### Pilot Metrics (Week 4-12 - Limited Launch)
Track whether the business model works at small scale:
- **Adoption & Engagement**: Users/customers acquired, activation rate, repeat usage
- **Unit Economics**: CAC (cost to acquire), LTV (lifetime value), LTV:CAC ratio, churn
- **NPS or Satisfaction**: Net Promoter Score, customer satisfaction, recommendation likelihood
- **Revenue/Impact**: Revenue generated, margin, payback period

### Launch Metrics (Month 3+ - Full Scale)
Track whether initiative contributes to portfolio goals:
- **Market Traction**: Market share, customer count, revenue, growth rate
- **Strategic Impact**: Competitive positioning, capability building, new market creation
- **Financial Performance**: Profitability, ROI, shareholder value contribution

## Funding Models

### Fixed Budget Model
- Allocate fixed budget pool to each horizon (70/20/10 split)
- Initiative sponsors propose projects within their horizon budget
- Enables predictability and prevents scope creep
- Works well for mature organizations with stable innovation capability

### Performance-Based Advancement
- Gate funding: Small investment to prove concept before major investment
- Example: $50K discovery budget → $500K alpha if learning targets met → $2M beta if metrics achieved
- Aligns spending with progress and risk
- Encourages rapid learning and resource reallocation
- Better for organizations new to systematic innovation

## Sponsorship & Political Capital

- **Align Sponsor Level to Initiative Ambition**: H1 projects → function sponsor; H2 → business unit lead; H3 → C-suite
- **Executive Visibility**: H3 projects need monthly C-level check-ins; H1 projects report quarterly
- **Failure Tolerance**: Establish explicit permission for H3 projects to fail; celebrate learning
- **Credit Attribution**: Share credit for successful innovations; don't punish reasonable failures

## Common Governance Pitfalls

| Pitfall | Symptom | Fix |
|---------|---------|-----|
| **Innovation Theater** | Many projects, little revenue impact | Tighten gate criteria; require validated learning before funding advancement |
| **Pilot Purgatory** | Successful pilots never launch | Build scale-up pathway; fund launch phase explicitly; assign product owner early |
| **Portfolio Creep** | Too many initiatives, diluted focus | Set initiative cap (e.g., max 15); kill underperforming ideas quarterly |
| **Misaligned Incentives** | Teams optimize for project completion, not learning | Measure innovation managers on learning velocity, not project status |
| **Insufficient Sponsorship** | H3 projects lack resources and political cover | Require C-suite sponsor for disruptive initiatives; monthly visibility |
