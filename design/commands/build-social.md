Build a LinkedIn or Instagram social asset with format-specific composition and brand consistency.

Steps:

1. **Confirm surface and aspect ratio** before any composition:
   - LinkedIn: 1200×1200 (B2B default), 1200×627 (link preview), 1080×1350 (vertical carousel — high reach).
   - Instagram: 1080×1080 (post), 1080×1350 (portrait), 1080×1920 (story / Reel cover).
   - For full inventory: [[surface-social-linkedin]], [[surface-social-instagram]].

2. **Calibrate via [[design-taste]]** — single post or series? Audience? Stylistic register? Default for Slalom B2B: premium-restrained or editorial-tech, NOT minimal-soft.

3. **For a series (≥3 posts), define the asset system via [[design-social-asset]].** Write `asset-system.md` covering: aspect ratio commit, type system (hero ≥ 80pt at 1080×1080), color system, layout grid, brand mark placement, series consistency rules.

4. **Compose** the asset(s) following the system:
   - **First fold (top 40%)** — the hook lives here.
   - **Second fold (next 40%)** — support, evidence, context.
   - **Third fold (bottom 20%)** — brand mark, page indicator, CTA.

5. **For carousels:** cover earns the swipe. Pages 2 to N-1 deliver one idea each. Last page commits with specific CTA. Page numbers (1/N) on every page.

6. **Supporting work:**
   - Hero imagery → [[design-imagery]] (default higgsfield: nano banana 2 for stills, seedance 2 for video).
   - Icons → [[design-iconography]].

7. **Run the four checks** ([[design-taste]]) plus the **0.4-second test**: can the first fold be understood in 0.4 seconds at thumbnail size?

8. **Run [[pre-delivery-checklist]]** plus social-specific gates:
   - Aspect ratio matches platform spec.
   - Hero readable at 280px (mobile thumbnail).
   - Brand mark on every carousel page.
   - Page numbers on every carousel page.
   - Alt text drafted.
   - Suggested post copy drafted.

9. **Hand off** the file(s) plus alt text plus suggested caption-text-for-post.

Anti-bland reminders (cite [[ai-tells-forbidden-patterns]] section "Surface-specific tells: social"):
- No teal-quote-card.
- No "10 things you didn't know" without surprising payoff.
- No "lessons learned" generic platitudes.
- No pointing-at-camera headshot.
- No motivational-quote graphic on branded background.
