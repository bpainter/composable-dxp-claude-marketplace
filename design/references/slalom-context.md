# Slalom Consulting Context

This reference provides organizational context for all consulting skills. Slalom is the firm these skills operate within — understanding its model, values, and approach shapes how recommendations are framed and deliverables are structured.

## Company Profile

Slalom is a privately held, partner-owned technology consulting firm founded in 2001 in Seattle. ~12,000 employees across 49 offices in 44+ markets worldwide. Upper mid-tier positioning: smaller than Big Four (Deloitte, Accenture) but deeper in execution than pure strategy firms (McKinsey, BCG).

**Core identity**: "Strategy to execution" — Slalom advises clients AND builds the solutions. This is the key differentiator. Most consulting firms hand off a deck; Slalom hands off working software.

## How Slalom Works

**Engagement model**: ~70-80% staff augmentation (consultants embedded in client teams), ~20-30% project-based (scoped engagements with defined deliverables). Typical project engagements run 6-18 months; enterprise relationships span years.

**Local-first delivery**: Teams work in client time zones (0-20% travel vs. Big Four's 50%+). This shapes how we scope, staff, and communicate — proximity and responsiveness are competitive advantages.

**Slalom Build**: In-house product engineering delivery through 7 Build centers (Seattle, Chicago, Boston, Houston, Denver, Toronto, Charlotte). Agile/lean methodology, MVP-driven, same-timezone. When a project needs custom software, Build is the execution arm.

**Team structure**: Associate Consultant → Consultant → Senior Consultant → Principal → Director → Senior Director → Managing Director. MDs are practice/office leaders equivalent to partners at other firms.

## Service Pillars

1. **Strategy** — Roadmaps, transformation planning, change management, workforce optimization
2. **Data & Analytics** — Data platforms (Snowflake, AWS), governance, BI, pipeline engineering
3. **AI/ML** — AI strategy, responsible AI, generative AI applications, process automation
4. **Cloud & Infrastructure** — Migration, modernization, cloud-native development, FinOps
5. **Digital Product Building** — Custom software via Slalom Build, product engineering
6. **Customer Experience** — Service design, CX transformation, interaction design
7. **Modern Commerce** — Salesforce Commerce Cloud, composable/headless, MACH architecture

## Enterprise Organization

Enterprise teams serve Global 1000 accounts with multi-year strategic relationships. Key verticals:

- **Financial Services** (largest): 75% of Fortune 500 financial services companies. Focus: AI-enabled efficiency, legacy modernization, regulatory compliance, open banking.
- **Healthcare**: 9/10 top health plans, 10/20 top hospitals. Focus: interoperability, AI clinical support, digital engagement, value-based care.
- **Retail & Commerce**: All top 5 Fortune 500 retailers. Focus: composable commerce, omnichannel, personalization, supply chain.
- **Technology, Telecom, Public Sector**: 500+ public sector organizations.

Enterprise engagements are typically longer (12-24+ months), cross-practice (Strategy + Build + Data + Experience), involve C-suite relationships, and require governance across multiple teams/locations.

## Composable DXP practice (Bermon's practice)

This is the user's practice — **Composable DXP**, inside Slalom's **Enterprise** capability. Sister practices in Enterprise cover Salesforce, Adobe Experience, ServiceNow, Anaplan/business planning, and Boomi/integration.

The practice focuses on:

- **Composable digital experience platforms** — modern, modular DXP architectures (CMS, commerce, search, personalization, identity, integration) wired together by APIs and event streams rather than monoliths.
- **MACH principles**: Microservices, API-first, Cloud-native, Headless.
- **Headless implementations**: Decoupling frontend presentation from backend commerce/content logic.
- **Key technologies**: React/Next.js frontends, Salesforce B2C Commerce (OCAPI, SCAPI), Amplience headless CMS, Contentful, integration patterns via Boomi.
- **Slalom Composable Accelerator**: Reference implementation for migrating from monolithic Salesforce SFRA/SiteGenesis to headless. Uses session bridging via Salesforce SLAS, React frontend, Amplience integration.
- **Client scenarios**: Legacy monolith modernization (gradual), hybrid implementations (phased headless), greenfield composable builds.

## Implications for Skill Behavior

When these skills generate deliverables, frame recommendations through Slalom's lens:

- **Scope quantification matters**: Slalom's SOW discipline means vague scope = margin erosion. Always quantify.
- **Composable/headless is a strategic priority**: When architecture comes up, lean toward composable, API-first, MACH-aligned approaches.
- **Execution is assumed**: Don't stop at strategy recommendations — include implementation considerations, Build center involvement, staffing models.
- **Partner ecosystem**: Recommend from Slalom's partner stack (AWS, Salesforce, Snowflake) rather than abstract "pick a cloud provider."
- **Client-centered language**: Slalom earns trust through clarity and delivery, not jargon or thought leadership posturing.
- **Enterprise scale**: Assume multi-team governance, steering committees, cross-practice collaboration for large engagements.
