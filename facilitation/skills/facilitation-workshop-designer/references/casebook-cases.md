# Collaboration Code — Casebook (real session designs)

Per-case digests for the 19 sessions in Rob Evans' *Collaboration Code: 2019 Casebook* (EY wavespace edition, 2021). Each entry: sponsor, outcomes sought, participants, agenda spine (Scan/Focus/Act with named modules), outcome, practitioner's note.

For the high-level book digest, see [`book-collaboration-code-casebook.md`](../../../references/book-collaboration-code-casebook.md).

## How to read a case

Each case is a real engagement contributed by a working practitioner. Read for:

- **Sponsor and stakes** — what kind of pressure was on the room
- **Participant mix** — who needed to be present and why
- **Agenda spine** — which Scan/Focus/Act modules were chosen, in what order, with what timing
- **What worked / what didn't** — the practitioner's reflection is the apprenticeship layer

The most relevant cases for Slalom Composable DXP work are noted in the [book digest](../../../references/book-collaboration-code-casebook.md).

## Cases by length

- **Three-Day Sessions** (15 cases): 19.01 through 19.15
- **Two-Day Sessions** (5 cases): 19.16 through 19.20

(The 2019 Casebook does not include a separate One-Day section; one-day designs live in the Tools volume.)

### 19.01 — Globalizing a Promising Manufacturing Technology

**Sponsor.** A global consumer goods company's Beauty/Cleansing business; senior leaders across Supply Chain, R&D, Commercial, and IT commissioned a 4-day DesignShop from Imaginal Labs (Rob Evans facilitating) to validate scaling "Technology X" — continuous mixing, concentrates/pre-mixes, and modular formula design pioneered at a plant near Rome.

**Outcomes sought.** Establish the commercial business case for Technology X; identify end-to-end synergies and barriers; decide which opportunities to pursue with execution teams and timing; generate a concrete plan by region, need state (body, face, hair), and plant with reliable estimates of GP impact from both COGS and top-line opportunities.

**Participants.** 43 participants plus plant reps and 9 external Chat Room hosts on Day One; supported by 9 KnowledgeWorkers. Theme: "Speed to Beauty Through Technology X."

**Agenda spine.** Scan: Plant tour and Learning Lab Booths (3 stations on Batch vs. Continuous Mixing, Tech X Demo, Equipment); Mezzogiornocitta Q&A Panel; Chat Rooms in two sessions of 4 rooms each (Step Change, Consumer R&D, Tax Strategies, "What Good Looks Like" external/internal supplier examples); Affinity-style Big Ideas reports; Personal Vision/Take-a-Panel/Share-a-Panel; "Back From the Future" synthesis; The Future Comes Alive skits. Focus: Future State Design Challenges (Rapid Globalization, Radical Leverage of Suppliers, Size of the Prize, Radically Rapid Plants, Radically Agile Innovation, Compete Differently); First Draft of the Future Round One/Two with Scouting Round and Why It Won't Work Post-It feedback. Act: Synthesis Conversation, Buckets of Work (Formulation, Global Expansion, Network Redesign, Impact on GP, Integration), Vote With Your Feet, two work rounds, Final Reports, Commitment Statement.

**Outcome.** Deep alignment around concrete implementation plans for Technology X across Beauty Cleansing — formulation approach, GP-impact modeling, global expansion plan with investment/timing, supply-chain network redesign, and competing-differently work products, delivered as a magazine in client hands by week's end.

**Practitioner's note.** Rob notes the live video call with US senior leaders right after the plant tour was a deliberate move to demonstrate top-down commitment. He calls it "an extraordinarily successful session that produced deep alignment around the implementation plans the group developed."

### 19.02 — Company Travel and Expense

**Sponsor.** A large company's Travel and Finance teams, who needed to revolutionize a travel ecosystem that had no single source of truth, inadequate reporting, and little fraud protection. Ken Martin of EY wavespace led the session with a KreW of 6.

**Outcomes sought.** Break down silos and form one end-to-end T&E team; align on the "wheel" of leading T&E events; build a first-draft Capability Model (Policy, Process & Controls, Performance Measures, Organization, Data, People, Technology); produce a preliminary Service Delivery Model and a sprint action plan that participants would advocate for.

**Participants.** 80 participants spanning Finance, HR, Business Operations, IT, and growth-market regional voices.

**Agenda spine.** Scan: Mentimeter warm-up word cloud, Marshmallow Challenge, "Wheel of the World" PROGRAM NAME overview as game-show, five Leading Practice Trade Shows (Travel Analytics, Lessons Learned Expense, Fraud, Lessons Learned Travel, T&E Demo), "A Walk in the Persona's Shoes" with five personas (Self-serve, Parts Unknown, VIP/EA, Frequent, Sales Traveler), and Event Map Validation where teams "lit up" T&E capabilities for assigned personas. Focus: Day 2 was a 6-hour Design Principles working session — teams took slices of the wheel with Capability Briefings and SMR-facilitated dialogue, capping with Personal Vision of Success (Take-A-Panel) and Sharing Personal Visions. Act: Day 3 Synthesis Conversation in concentric circles, Buckets of Work (Technology and Data, Domain Compliance, MVP, Capability Requirements, Change Management, Integration), self-selected Work Round One and Two, Sponsor Closing with Commitment Statement.

**Outcome.** Aligned capability model, persona-driven event maps, prioritized opportunities, and a service-delivery sprint plan ready to drive PROGRAM NAME's release.

**Practitioner's note.** Ken built a detailed PowerPoint strawdog he walked the SDT through in advance — that artifact is available on request as an exemplar.

### 19.03 — Finding a Cure for Brain Cancer

**Sponsor.** Catharine Stace, CEO of the Australian Cure Brain Cancer Foundation, who contacted Gail Taylor (Tomorrow Makers) to accelerate the search for a cure. Designed and facilitated by Gail with Matt Taylor.

**Outcomes sought.** Find a cure for brain cancer within 10 years (with a 5-year stretch goal); broaden participation beyond researchers and surgeons to include survivors, family members, technicians, and entrepreneurs; launch the Global Brain Exchange (GBX).

**Participants.** 45 participants — researchers from US, UK, and Australia, plus survivors and non-experts — supported by 9 KnowledgeWorkers (some apprentices from The Difference, two with relatives lost to brain cancer). Held in The Difference environment in Sydney over 2.5 days.

**Agenda spine.** Scan: "Here We Go!" — Charlie Teo cutting a ribbon to 2024 and inviting all through a future-portal; Remembering (teams remember success — taking out friction, paradigm changes, role of disruptive technology); Our Journey shares; 150-year Scenario Timeline (1870s through 2050); Connect a GBX small-group lunches; Formal Opening introducing SCAN/FOCUS/ACT; Hunting and Gathering across 200+ articles/artifacts; the Glass Bead Game (Hesse-style tile play). Focus: Design of a Design A, B & C with strategy/admin method/performance specs models built physically; Performance Specs sleep-on-it; The GBX Plan So Far presentation; Models conversation; Individual Reading on networks/meshworks/IP/open source. Act: Synthesis Conversation, Buckets of Work (initially 11, distilled to 6: Communication/Vision/Mission, Prior Experience, Identify what we want of GBX, Engagement strategy & funding, Rollout strategy, Collar team), networking iterations, final report-outs.

**Outcome.** Within the first 5 years the team attracted a $100M grant. Discovered the funding "Valley of Death" mid-research as a system-level problem to redesign. Many participants remain fundraisers, board members, and advocates today.

**Practitioner's note.** Gail: "One of the ahas of the participants was how much they could contribute greatly without being experts in brain science. And the researchers in brain science saw the benefit of working outside their field." All major players acknowledge they never would have gotten so far without the DesignShop.

### 19.04 — Building Innovation Into Social Impact Work (PatchWorks Event)

**Sponsor.** The Rockefeller Foundation, 2016 in Rome. Designed and led by Dan Newman of the Matter Group.

**Outcomes sought.** Share innovation approaches across grantees; evangelize tools for social-impact innovation; map use cases to existing/required tools; identify gaps and unmet needs; document new applications; sketch a high-level business model aligning tool users and developers. Theme: "Homo Faber."

**Participants.** 60 participants — 50% grantees, 50% from Asia/Pacific/Africa/LatAm, 30% working with end users.

**Agenda spine.** Scan (Day 1): "Terms of Art" panels for arrival writing; Introduction Circles by theme/geography/birth-month; patches formed around three Terms of Art with flocking rules (max 4 tool developers, max 2 funders, ≥4 countries); set up nine tool families (social innovation labs, design thinking/rapid prototyping, fellowships, horizon scanning, awards, accelerators, scenarios, innovation mindset, systems mapping); "Explain it to a Child" — actual 14-year-olds critique team posters; Homo Faber lecture (David Bodanis on Cavendish Lab); Tools for Innovation Ecosystems discussion circles; Inventions collaborative game. Focus (Day 2): Case Studies Chat Rooms in 3 rounds of 25 min; Patches De-brief; Fail Fair with cause/consequence Post-Its and affinity diagram; Improving Tools; Tools for Innovation Ecosystem (B) First Draft. Act (Day 3): Patches "What Next?" with walkabout; 2-hour Synthesis Conversation; Buckets (Criteria for Goodness, Map Value Chain/Biz Model, Unmet Needs, New Apps for Existing Tools); 3.75-hour team work with check-ins and Final Reports.

**Outcome.** Documented unmet needs, refined nine tool families, business-model sketches for the social-innovation tools ecosystem, and a "what's next" plan for participants and the broader community.

**Practitioner's note.** Dan: "The design is potentially interesting both because I think it's a pretty good design and because the intent of each module is laid out in black and white." Special design feature: "explain it to a kid" with real 14-year-olds critiquing the work.

### 19.05 — Improving the Launch of New Drugs

**Sponsor.** A senior executive at "GloPhaCo" (global pharma) who'd seen Imaginal Labs work in a previous role. Bain analysis showed half of launches missing peak sales, half of those by 50%+. Carolyn Buck Luce and Rob Evans of Imaginal Labs. Sold via a full-day Discovery Day with 10 prospective participants using a Take-A-Flipchart "look back from October 2016" exercise.

**Outcomes sought.** Redesign launch processes; develop measurable definition of Launch Excellence; identify gaps and high-leverage opportunities; create a roadmap of short-term (90-day) and long-term (6–18 month) solutions; align resourcing, governance, incentives, metrics, and country/region coordination.

**Participants.** 31 senior leaders from across the organization, supported by an Imaginal Labs KreW of 6.

**Agenda spine.** Scan: Sponsor Opening; Panel Discussion (IMS Consulting, Dupont, North Highland); 6 Chat Rooms (Integrated Market Access, Integrated Process, Commercial Capabilities, Pharma Launch Cases, Unpacking the Commercial Map, Launch Measures and Metrics) with Home Room teams; History of the Future (25 years back/10 years forward); Personal Vision of Launch Excellence (Take-A-Panel/Share-A-Panel/Synthesis); Directed Readings (Quick Win, Your Medical Mind, Evolving Power of the Payer, Good Business Decisions, Challenger Launch); Make It Come Alive skits. Focus: Design Challenge Stretch Scenarios (Driving Alignment with Incentives, Defining Launch Excellence, Creating a Single Source of Truth, Product Archetypes, Integrated Decisions); First Draft of the Solution then Shift and Share, Second Draft (Governance, Integrated Process, Implementing Product Archetypes, Differentiating Value Props, The Real Story); Why It Won't Work; Authors. Act: Synthesis Conversation, Buckets of Work / Vote With Your Feet (Governance with definitions/measures/RACI/portfolio prioritization, Front-End Integrated Process, Back-End Integrated Process, Single Source of Truth, Integration Team), two work rounds, Final Reports, Commitment.

**Outcome.** The sponsor calculated multiple billions in savings and increased profitability over five years of implementation. Her commitment to the room: "My commitment to you is a plan for leadership and a plan for all of us to act as that virus positively affecting the organization."

**Practitioner's note.** The Discovery Day Take-A-Panel ("Today is October 28, 2016. Reflect back on the December 2014 DesignShop…") doubled as a sales tool and pre-design exercise — a successful wavespace business-development pattern.

### 19.06 — Developing Engineering Simplicity for AeroCo

**Sponsor.** AeroCo, a large European aircraft manufacturer needing significant cultural change in Engineering. Dan Newman of the Matter Group designed and led with Viornery.

**Outcomes sought.** Build awareness of the need for change and the burning platform; build an Engineering transformation roadmap on AeroCo Operating System (AOS) principles and Top 5 transverse topics (NRC, RC, LTR, Sourcing, MODs Simplification); clarify leadership behaviors enabling agile, collaborative ways of working; delimit an "Experimentation Zone."

**Participants.** 80 participants, mostly engineers.

**Agenda spine.** Scan: Patch Creation around a Hypertile-written act of professional judgment (flocking rules: 6–8/patch, no more than 2 from any CoC, ≥2 nationalities); Win As Much As You Can prisoner's dilemma; Patch Take-A-Panel future scenario (quicker, cheaper, simpler); Fishbowl with BSDs; 2-hour Reading exercise (Kahneman, Temporary Autonomous Zones, Kill the Company, Zappos/Valve, Cathedrals/Michelangelo, Social Insects, Brain science, Zen, Cybernetics, Stafford Beer); Three Cats Model presentation/demo; Three Cats Design teams; Decision Processes Map and "Magic Rock" exercise (eliminating time, friction, cost); Patch design of friction-less AeroCo with patch-visit walkabout. Focus: Cluster Decisions on hexagons; Decisions & Vantage Points (re-design at higher Vantage Points); Design Challenges (No staff over 40, rapid re-work, AeroCo China launch in 12 months, passenger-to-cargo conversion) and Alternatives with new team members; Patches design and assess multiple decision models; Plenary Discussion on change levers; First Draft (Skills Management, Design Experimentation Zone, Transformation Map, Alternative Decision Models, Patches & Nodes Engineering Social Network); Why It Won't Work. Act: 2.5-hour Synthesis Conversation; Buckets of Work; 5-hour Work in Teams with ambassador "negotiation" with Sponsor Team; Patch Commitments per individual.

**Outcome.** Transformation roadmap by Centre of Competence with milestones and owners, alternative decision models for engineering judgment, rules of engagement between Engineering and AOS, and a defined Experimentation Zone.

**Practitioner's note.** Dan brought academic content into the room (Kahneman, Stafford Beer, Bodanis) as deliberate provocation — engineers got a 2-hour reading block with assigned provocative questions to lubricate post-fishbowl discussion.

### 19.07 — Building Tech Company Growth Platforms

**Sponsor.** New CEO of "CommTech," a tech infrastructure company taken private after losing a lucrative mobile telephony contract. Rob Evans of Imaginal Labs led with three other facilitators (one supplied by the consulting partner) and a 12-person KreW.

**Outcomes sought.** Identify 1–2 Growth Platforms per business unit that roll up to 5–7% growth for 2020 and embed them in business plans GMs believed in; identify inorganic growth/acquisition targets; reinforce CommTech values (Accountability, Collaboration, Transparency, Resilience, Respect); shift a "learned helplessness" culture.

**Participants.** ~120 leaders from the Extended Leadership Team plus 5 consulting partners across 6 BUs. Logistics were tight — 75% of normal space — driving a parallel-BU Focus/Act design.

**Agenda spine.** Scan: Sponsor Opening (CEO + Chief Strategy Officer); CommTech 2019 Jeopardy game from the pre-read Fact Book (covering Tech Platform, Customer Needs, Workforce of the Future, Trends, Team capabilities, Tech resources); 13 Chat Rooms (BU strategies x 6, Tech Platform, Analyst views x 4, First Party Data Strategy, Data Privacy) in 3 rounds of 30 min; Domino-style insights reports; Marshmallow Challenge with TED talk debrief; History of the Future (20 back/10 forward); Take-A-Panel pairs/Share-A-Panel/Synthesis on Force Field Diagram by BU; A Book in an Hour homework with author roleplay (Play to Win, etc.). Focus: Overview of Growth Platforms with Apple/AWS exemplars and criteria/filters; Ideate Platforms by BU (Rob:48, Jonah:25, Heather:15, Clair:5, Josh:7+12, Integration:4); Build Out Platforms with templates; Stress Tests (potentially fatal); Reports with green/yellow/red Post-It feedback; What Needs To Be True; BU Leaders Present with finger-poll confidence vote; Authors module. Act: Synthesis Conversation, BUs convene with own Integration Teams plus Uber Integration (CEO/CSO/CFO/CPO/external lead); two Working Rounds with Shark-Tank–style CFO grilling for numbers; BU final reports; company-wide communications drafted by HR.

**Outcome.** A concrete set of Growth Platforms per BU validated against criteria; an Uber Integration Team plan; a new Vision Statement that earned a standing ovation when revealed (replacing one developed at $millions by an external branding firm). Two months later the CSO reported: "Thanks to the DesignShop, our culture change is progressing even faster than our results."

**Practitioner's note.** The CEO had his assistant collect phones into charging-station boxes at name-badge pickup — devices "parked" for the duration. Rob calls it "one of the most successful DesignShops I have supported." A KreW-produced video became an unexpected gift; the CEO's reaction: "I think we should show this as many times as we can."

### 19.08 — UNICEF Programme Division Planning Retreat

**Sponsor.** UNICEF Programme Division (PD) leadership; Dan Newman of the Matter Group designed and delivered. PatchWorks format renamed "Base Camps" because non-Americans don't recognize patchwork quilts.

**Outcomes sought.** Convert sterile form-filling into robust dialogue on PD's priorities, impact, and ways of working; build a shared understanding of PD's contribution to the 2018–21 Strategic Plan; identify 2–3 Big Ticket Items; build/validate a results framework mapped to BUs; identify needed enabler changes; provide context for BUs to draft their OMP contributions; enhance solidarity and equity in the division.

**Participants.** ~60 people including 2–3 from each BU plus SD, EMOPS, PRD, OoL.

**Agenda spine.** Scan (Sun-Mon): Sun: History of the Future timeline to 2040 + Sponsor Welcome. Mon: Terms of Art with Post-Its (Accountability, Responsibility, Community Engagement, Last Mile, Rights-based, Adolescent Health, Mainstreaming); BU Patch — Context & Challenges; BU Patch Jigsaw building Theory of Change Causal Chains from 25 pre-printed result-area Post-Its; Mixed Teams Delivery Vision ("Leave No Child Behind"); Neural Networks talk by Bodanis; Multi-BU Jigsaw round 2; Theory of Change introduction; Three Cats training and teams (Real PD/Conceptual PD/Mechanical PD); Mixed teams Draft OMP with explain-it-walkabout. Focus (Tue): Mixed Teams Global & Country Uptake Strategies (1/region); Divisional Big Ticket Items harvest and clustering; parallel teams on Knowledge Management, Resource Mobilization, Quality of Life, Ways of Working; Multi-BU Cross-Division Intersections Marketplace in 3 rounds; BU Patch reprioritise with OMP draft. Act (Wed): 2-hour Synthesis Conversation; Buckets of Work (Rules of the Game cross-BU, PD role/results statement, PD Engagement Plan, OMP/IRRF timeline, PD-wide Big Tickets, Knowledge Management, Resource Mobilization, Internal management/staff QoL/gender); team work then BU work to document specific contributions, results with indicators, function mix, enablers.

**Outcome.** Each BU left with the building blocks to draft OMP contributions; a small set of Big Ticket Items with accountabilities; documented "rules of the game" for intra-divisional collaboration; engagement plan and timeline.

**Practitioner's note.** Dan: "The Base Camps had their own narratives and then developed their strategies together with the others in mixed teams. We did a follow-up with the same people two months ago [in 2019] and you could really see how well the earlier event worked." (Note: Pages 86–96 of the casebook also describe a 1.5+1.5-day HealthCo Executive Alignment session by Tom Kehner using theDifference a2c's Three Conversations — Context, Possibilities, Action — with modules including Strategic Priority Huddle Groups, Cornerstone Chat Rooms, Anchors to Rockets, Bus to Utopia, Pivot Conversation, Design Sprints, Goal Rave, and a Casey-Kasem-style Top 65 Goals wall after homework synthesis.)

### 19.10 — The Multi-Faith Movement for Social Justice

**Sponsor.** Rev. Dr. Katharine Henderson, president of Auburn Seminary in NYC; Carolyn Buck Luce (Auburn board) proposed convening recipients of Auburn programs. Tested via a one-day Discovery Day with 20 participants. Carolyn Buck Luce and Rob Evans of Imaginal Labs facilitated. Branded "MountainTop."

**Outcomes sought.** Enhance awareness/alignment on the social-justice landscape; explore differing theories of change; discover ways to scale collective impact; create an actionable common vision with measurable progress; equip leaders with shared tools, methods, and relationships; for Auburn specifically, develop strategies to support the movement over 3 years.

**Participants.** 80+ multi-faith leaders across religious traditions, plus Auburn staff working separately on Day 3 afternoon. Held at the Taylor-designed Vanderbilt Center for Better Health in Nashville. KreW of 9.

**Agenda spine.** Scan: Convener's Opening with reading/prayer; Starting Panel on religiously-based transformation (Henderson, Harkins, Ray, Twist); 11 Chat Rooms in 3 rounds of 30 min (SONG bridge-building, Marriage Equality lessons, Activating Millennials, Evangelical Immigration consensus, Connecting Faith Traditions, PICO scaling, Changing Relationship to Money, Tech for Movement Building, Collective Impact, Healthy Ecosystem characteristics, Becoming a Digital Village Elder); Domino-style Big Ideas reports; 3-words exercise on the current state of the movement; History of the Future (30 back/10 forward); Personal Vision; Sharing Personal Visions; Similarities and Differences with gallery; Theory of Change; Haiku Assignment. Focus: Haiku Reports; Create the Problem (16 teams paired Current/Future on 8 topics: Seat at the Table, Spirituality + Organizing, Coordination, Funding, Millennials, Measurement, Tools for Leaders, Building Power); Identify Opportunities pairing teams; A Walk in the Park silent reflection; Synthesis Conversation; Vote With Your Feet. Act: Working Teams (Inclusive/Diverse Movement, Learning from Opponents, Shared Vision, Shared Database, Mentorship, Spirituality in the Movement, Moving Process Forward, Political Power); Scouting Reports in 3 rounds; final reports; Auburn-only afternoon Synthesis and working teams.

**Outcome.** Auburn completed every aspect of its strategic plan ahead of schedule; spawned a series of MountainTop convenings continuing to this day; Auburn committed a large portion of its new offices to a Design Space using Taylor System/Collaboration Code methods to host sessions and educate faith leaders.

**Practitioner's note.** Music (Fred Johnson) and morning prayer/reading from rotating faith traditions were structural elements throughout — opening, transitions, and closes were musically and spiritually framed, modeling the multi-faith collaboration the room was working toward.

### 19.11 — Big Pharma Platform Strategy

**Sponsor.** A top-three pharmaceutical company's Parenterals (injectables) platform leader, building a 7-year integrated platform strategy that shifts from site/function focus to integrated platform. Rob Evans and Carolyn Buck Luce of Imaginal Labs facilitated with a KreW of 7.

**Outcomes sought.** Shared understanding of unmet patient/doctor/stakeholder needs; new model for the Make organization (Plan→Source→Make→Deliver) with engagement plan; a scalable strategy that could extend across the rest of the company's Supply Chain; organizational alignment and readiness to implement.

**Agenda spine.** Scan: Sponsor Opening; Strategy Framework framing entrepreneurs drawing on company resources; Jeopardy! **Practitioner's note.** Heavy use of TED Talks as a Scan provocation (12 talks across 12 small teams) — designed to transfer leadership/creativity/collaboration concepts laterally into the strategy work, not as background reading.

### 19.12 — Design Sprint for Peace in Venezuela

**Sponsor.** Dr. William Ury (Program on Negotiation, Harvard Law School; co-author of Getting to Yes) responding to a Venezuelan businessman ("Sr. Z") asking for help. Imaginal Labs (Rob Evans, Tom Kehner) and Jac Howard supported. Branded "VNI — the Venezuela Negotiation Initiative." Followed earlier Korean Negotiation Initiative format ("SWAT Team for Peace").

**Outcomes sought.** Map the Venezuelan situation; simulate a "think tank" to design a possible Government of National Unity (GNU); plan tactical next steps for a peaceful transition; end with a committed team to work on the issue full-time on the ground.

**Participants.** ~12 — peacemakers, designers, negotiation experts, retired military, two senior British former officials (remote), Venezuelan businessmen Sr. B and Sr. C, Spanish/English translators throughout. Held in a Boulder AirBnB outfitted with Tom Kehner's Cindy Walls.

**Agenda spine.** Scan/Balcony (Day 1): Welcome with Ury's SWAT Process (Balcony, Bridge/Simulation, 3rd Side Actions); Introductions Exercise (how known in world / known to people who love you / what brings you here / what to get from this); Presentation on SWAT Process; Overview of 5 Scenarios; Drill into GNU/Negotiated Transition; Force-Field Analysis; Understanding the Key Players (KNI experience, war decision table, Victory Speeches, "Who can do what tomorrow?"); Mapping Key Players with 3 Post-Its each in affinity diagram; Uncommon Knowledge ("what don't they know that gives you cause for hope?"); Time Travel 5 years into the bright future, bring back an artifact. Focus/Bridge (Day 2): Overnight Wisdom dream; Expert Zoom on Leadership; Victory Speeches Round 1 (Trump, Maduro) then Round 2 (Guaido, Padrino); Menu of Trust Building Measures; Building Models of the Future (GNU model + Guarantees to protagonists) with Why It Won't Work Post-Its; iterate. Act/Third Side (Day 3): Overnight Wisdom affinity diagram with vote-as-heat-map; Expert Call with Gary Slutkin (Cure Violence Global) on interrupting violence as epidemic; ACT — Access/Credibility/Trust mapping; Vote With Your Feet into 2 buckets with strict rules ("don't put anyone's name on an action without talking to them"); Action Plans; Honorable Closure; Gratitude & Celebrations.

**Outcome.** A Think Tank with productive relationships on both sides of the conflict (still distrustful of each other), regularly sharing progress with the Boulder core team for ongoing coaching.

**Practitioner's note.** Rob describes the AirBnB as a defining choice — homelike atmosphere (living room as Radiant Room, breakouts in den/study/kitchen/patio) eased participants into "this unusual approach to peacemaking." Post-lunch walks were structural. In the parallel Israel-Palestine sprint, participants from different sides cooked an Israeli breakfast together for the team.

### 19.13 — University of New South Wales Innovation Center

**Sponsor.** Brad Furber, newly appointed leader of UNSW's Michael Crouch Innovation Center (MCIC). Gail and Matt Taylor as 2015 Artists in Residence. 2.5-day session, no restrictions on who could attend.

Focus ("I Found"): How I See It / How Do We See It (each participant tells story of how MCIC succeeded by 2025); Walk About; Making the Impossible Possible (4 teams on future challenges, 3 teams on how nature builds complexity); Creating David ("creativity is the elimination of options"); What Is Success (name what to accomplish before noon tomorrow). Act ("A Work Of"): Embedding Our DNA self-organized teams (Culture, Virtual Networks, Networking & Value Webs, Communications Outward, Operations, Business Model, BHAGs); Curation Team weaves together the Differences That Make a Difference (Bateson).

**Outcome.** A well-documented business plan for the Innovation Center that was iterated through follow-up meetings with a core 25, then marketed inside and outside the university.

**Practitioner's note.** The author of the original UNSW 2025 strategy approached Gail at the end and thanked her — "You all have done a lot with the university's vision that you started with. In doing the work with MCIC, you have helped the University improve their 2025 plans." Gail: "Yes, we thought the original was kind of boring." Department heads found the broad open process "kind of scary."

### 19.14 — Global Access to Medicines

**Sponsor.** "BigPharma" (one of the world's largest pharma companies) commissioned EY's Carolyn Buck Luce to deliver a 3-day DesignShop on socially responsible commercial strategies for the "neglected four billion." Rob Evans facilitated.

**Outcomes sought.** Define what global access means for BigPharma as a commercial enterprise; identify best-in-class partner alignments; develop vision and building blocks for Global Access strategy; identify near-term initiatives and a strategic framework for "Commercial trials"; propose org/investment requirements that reduce internal barriers.

**Participants.** 56 BigPharma leaders from headquarters and developing markets, plus 20 external guests on Days 1 and 2 — academics (Erasmus, Harvard Kennedy, Michigan), Gates Foundation, Axios International, Grameen America, ICCR, PharmAccess, Scojo, UNICEF, World Bank, WHO, World Heart Federation, and others.
