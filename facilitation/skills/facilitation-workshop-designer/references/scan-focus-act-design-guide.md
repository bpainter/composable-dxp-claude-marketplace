# Scan / Focus / Act design guide

How to sequence modules within each phase. Pulls from the Collaboration Code Tools catalog ([`book-collaboration-code-tools.md`](../../../references/book-collaboration-code-tools.md)).

## What each phase produces

- **Scan** — divergence and discovery. The room ends with a wide field of possibilities, perspectives surfaced, and an agreed problem statement (or set of statements).
- **Focus** — narrowing and design. The room ends with 2–4 candidate solutions evaluated against criteria, with trade-offs named and convergence underway.
- **Act** — commitment and mobilization. The room ends with decisions made, owners named, communications drafted, and next checkpoints set.

## The narrative inside each phase

Each phase has its own arc — a mini Scan/Focus/Act inside the macro phase (Pattern 222 *Recursion*).

### Inside Scan
1. **Open the field** — invitations for what's true, what's hard, what's possible
2. **Gather perspectives** — from the room and from outside (research, customer voice, expert input)
3. **Surface tension** — what's the elephant; what's the assumption nobody's testing
4. **Theme** — find patterns in what's been gathered
5. **Frame the problem(s)** — Pattern: Creating the Problem; this is Scan's payoff

### Inside Focus
1. **Establish criteria** — what does a good solution look like? (Design Criteria canvas)
2. **Generate solutions** — sketches, prototypes, proposals (silent generation, then share)
3. **Evaluate** — against criteria, trade-offs surfaced
4. **Iterate** — second draft, refined; reduce the candidates
5. **Converge** — land the candidate that survives scrutiny

### Inside Act
1. **Commit** — the decision is made and named
2. **Plan** — buckets of work, owners, timing (Action Plan, Buckets of Work, RACI)
3. **Communicate** — who needs to know what (Communication Plan)
4. **Mobilize** — first steps in the next 7 days; symbolic action
5. **Close** — ritual of closure

## Common Scan modules (sequencing examples)

The Collaboration Code Tools catalog includes ~50 Scan modules. The combinations that work well:

### "Surface current state and pain" (1.5–2 hours)
- **Hassle Map** (45 min) — current-state pain
- **Empathic Interview** debrief (30 min) — voice of customer
- **Affinity Diagram** (30 min) — theme the patterns
- **Plus/Delta** (10 min) — quick energy check

### "Surface possibilities and futures" (1.5–2 hours)
- **History of the Future** (Timeline) (30 min) — backcast from 5 years out
- **Best Case / Worst Case Planning** (45 min) — bounding scenarios
- **Spoze** (30 min) — what-if generation
- **Theme together** (15 min)

### "Surface stakeholder perspectives" (1.5 hours)
- **In the Shoes of...** / Stakeholder Scenarios (30 min) — perspective-taking
- **Empathy Map** (30 min) per stakeholder — externalize each view
- **Take-A-Panel** / Personal Vision (30 min) — synthesis

### "Build the room's common language" (45 min)
- **Terms of Art** (20 min) — surface the language already in use
- **Story Circles** (20 min) — narrative inclusion
- **One Word** (5 min) — quick energy check

## Common Focus modules

The Collaboration Code Tools catalog includes ~22 Focus modules. The combinations that work well:

### "Frame the problem" (1 hour)
- **Create the Problem** / Create the Gap (30 min) — gap definition
- **Problem Statement** (20 min) — formal articulation
- **Why It Won't Work** (10 min) — pre-mortem on the framing

### "Generate first-draft solutions" (1.5 hours)
- **Magic Buttons** (15 min) — what would unlock everything
- **First Draft of the Solution** (60 min) — small-group prototype
- **Gallery Walk** (15 min) — see all the drafts

### "Apply criteria and converge" (1.5 hours)
- **Design Criteria** canvas (30 min) — establish criteria explicitly
- **Prioritization Matrix** (30 min) — basic or head-to-head
- **Take-a-Stand Matrix** (15 min) — force a position
- **Discussion** (15 min) — surface dissent

### "Refine the second draft" (1.5 hours)
- **Second Draft of Our Solution** (60 min) — refined concept
- **Simulation** (20 min) — run the future to test it
- **Sleep on It** (10 min framing) — homework for Act Day

## Common Act modules

The Collaboration Code Tools catalog includes ~18 Act modules. The combinations that work well:

### "Form workstreams and assign owners" (1 hour)
- **Buckets of Work** / Vote with Your Feet (30 min) — workstream assignment by movement
- **RACI Chart** (20 min) — responsibility assignment
- **Integration Team** (10 min) — name the carry-forward team

### "Build the roadmap and timeline" (1 hour)
- **Transformation Roadmap** (30 min) — multi-horizon plan
- **Action Plan** (20 min) — first 30 days
- **What Has to Be True?** (10 min) — surface assumptions

### "Communicate and commit" (45 min)
- **Communication Plan – Immediate** (15 min) — who needs to know what this week
- **Commitment Statement** (20 min) — each owner states aloud
- **Symbolic Action** (10 min) — physical-act-as-commitment

### "Close" (30 min)
- **Final Report Out** (15 min) — gallery
- **Plus/Delta** or **One Word** (10 min) — energy check
- **Closing Ritual** (5 min)

## Sequencing rules of thumb

1. **Energy peaks midway through divergence.** Schedule the most intellectually demanding Scan modules in the first half of Day 1, not at the end.
2. **Focus drains energy faster than Scan.** Plan more breaks during Focus blocks.
3. **Act feels easy because the hard work is done.** It is not — most Act modules are short for a reason; they're integrative.
4. **End each day with reflection, not action.** Act Day reflection > start-of-day energy.
5. **Open every day with a shared frame.** "Yesterday we... today we..."
