# Canvas selection guide

Extended logic for which canvas to reach for. Use as the lookup when designing strategy / innovation sessions.

## The Prepare canvases (used early in any new team)

### Team Charter Canvas
**Question**: How will we work together?
**When**: Day 1 of any new team formation; quarterly reset for ongoing teams
**Time**: 60–90 min
**Blocks**: Purpose, Members, Deliverables, Values, Decision-making, Working agreements
**Common pitfall**: Treating it as a one-time exercise rather than a living document

### Screenplays
**Question**: How does each role contribute to each meeting?
**When**: When meeting roles are unclear or contested
**Time**: 30 min per recurring meeting
**Common pitfall**: Skipping it because it feels procedural; the cost is recurring meeting friction

## The Point of View canvases (vision and direction)

### 5 Bold Steps Vision®
**Question**: What's our vision, and what 5 bold moves get us there?
**When**: The team has a vague vision and needs to convert it to commitment; new leader mobilizing a team
**Time**: 90–120 min
**Blocks**: Vision, 5 Steps, Supports, Challenges
**Common pitfall**: Vision too abstract; Steps that aren't bold enough (this is the failure mode to fight)

See [`template-strategy-sketch.md`](../../../templates/template-strategy-sketch.md).

### Cover Story Vision®
**Question**: How will the future look from the outside, looking back?
**When**: The team can't even start; they need an imagination unlock
**Time**: 60 min
**Blocks**: Magazine cover, headline, pull-quotes, the story behind it
**Common pitfall**: Letting it stay metaphorical; you must convert to concrete strategy after

### Design Criteria Canvas
**Question**: What constraints and requirements must our design meet?
**When**: After Point of View, before Ideate, to keep ideation grounded
**Time**: 45 min
**Blocks**: Must, Should, Could (MoSCoW); the rubric the team will judge against
**Common pitfall**: Vague criteria that don't actually constrain ideation

### Storytelling Canvas
**Question**: How will we tell this story to outsiders?
**When**: Communicating a strategy externally; pursuit-stage proposals; vision communication
**Time**: 60 min
**Blocks**: Hero, audience, journey, ending
**Common pitfall**: Confusing storytelling with presentation; stories have arc, not bullets

## The Understand canvases (the world and the user)

### Context Canvas®
**Question**: What forces shape our environment?
**When**: Strategy work needs PESTEL-style scan; entering a new market
**Time**: 60–90 min
**Blocks**: Demographic trends, economy, regulation, technology, customer needs, uncertainties, rules and regulations, competitors
**Common pitfall**: Treating it as analysis rather than facilitation surface — the team must build it together

### Customer Personas
**Question**: Who specifically are we designing for?
**When**: Empathy work; design thinking discovery
**Time**: 30 min per persona (after research; not from imagination)
**Blocks**: Demographics, behaviors, needs, pains, gains, channels, quotes
**Common pitfall**: Composite personas built from stereotypes rather than evidence

### Customer Journey
**Question**: What does the user experience over time?
**When**: Understanding end-to-end experience; surfacing handoff failures
**Time**: 90 min for a focused journey; 1–2 days for a comprehensive one
**Blocks**: Stages, actions, touchpoints, thoughts, emotions, opportunities
**Common pitfall**: Internal-process journey instead of user-experience journey

### Empathy Map
**Question**: What does the user think, feel, see, hear, say, do?
**When**: After interviews; synthesizing user research
**Time**: 30–45 min per persona
**Blocks**: Says, Thinks, Does, Feels, Sees, Hears, Pains, Gains
**Common pitfall**: Filling it from stereotypes instead of interview evidence

### Stakeholder Map
**Question**: Who has interest, who has influence?
**When**: Complex stakeholder environments; change management
**Time**: 60 min
**Blocks**: 2x2 (interest x influence); name and label each stakeholder
**Common pitfall**: Treating it as static — stakeholder positions shift through engagement

## The Ideate canvases (generate)

The Ideate stage is less canvas-heavy — it relies on HMW prompts, sticky-note generation, and selection structures. Reach for:

- **Inspiration cards** — pre-built cards with categories, methods, or analogies to spark ideation
- **Idea Sketching templates** — structured one-page idea capture
- **Pitch It template** — 2-minute idea pitch structure (problem, solution, why us, ask)

## The Prototype canvases (the model)

### Business Model Canvas (Strategyzer)
**Question**: How does this business actually work?
**When**: Modeling any business — new venture, new product, transformation
**Time**: 90–120 min for a first pass; iterates over weeks
**Blocks**: 9 — Customer Segments, Value Propositions, Channels, Customer Relationships, Revenue Streams, Key Activities, Key Resources, Key Partnerships, Cost Structure
**Common pitfall**: Reaching for the BMC first — for an early-stage idea, the Lean Canvas is usually better
**Sequence**: Customer Segments → Value Propositions → right side (relationships, channels, revenue) → left side (resources, activities, partnerships) → cost structure last

See [`template-business-model-canvas.md`](../../../templates/template-business-model-canvas.md).

### Value Proposition Canvas (Strategyzer)
**Question**: Does our offer fit the customer's job?
**When**: Value proposition contested or thin; alongside the BMC
**Time**: 60–90 min
**Blocks**: Customer profile (Jobs, Pains, Gains) + Value Map (Products & Services, Pain Relievers, Gain Creators)
**Common pitfall**: Filling from inside-out (we have product X) rather than outside-in (customer has job Y)

### Business Model Mix
**Question**: How do multiple business models fit together?
**When**: Portfolio companies; products with multiple revenue streams; ecosystem plays
**Time**: 90 min
**Common pitfall**: Forcing complexity; sometimes the simple BMC is enough

## The Validate canvases (test)

### Riskiest Assumption Test
**Question**: What's the assumption that, if wrong, kills the idea?
**When**: Before any expensive prototype or build
**Time**: 30 min to identify; days/weeks to test
**Common pitfall**: Picking the easy assumption to test instead of the riskiest

### Validation Canvas
**Question**: What did we test, with whom, and what did we learn?
**When**: After running a test; before deciding next
**Time**: 30 min
**Blocks**: Hypothesis, test, success criteria, results, learning, next decision
**Common pitfall**: No falsifiable success criteria — "we'll see how it goes"

### Experiment Canvas
**Question**: How will we run this experiment?
**When**: Designing the test
**Time**: 30–60 min
**Blocks**: Hypothesis, test design, sample, duration, success metric, fail metric
**Common pitfall**: Ambiguous fail metric; without a fail threshold, the experiment can't conclude

## The Scale canvases (operationalize)

### Innovation Matrix
**Question**: Where does this innovation sit on impact vs. uncertainty?
**When**: Portfolio prioritization
**Time**: 60 min
**Blocks**: 2x2 (impact x uncertainty); Horizon 1 / 2 / 3 mapping
**Common pitfall**: Confusing uncertainty with risk

### Portfolio Map
**Question**: What's the balance of our innovation portfolio?
**When**: Quarterly portfolio reviews
**Time**: 90 min
**Blocks**: Each initiative plotted by stage, investment, expected return
**Common pitfall**: Overweighting Horizon 1 because it's certain

### Scaling Roadmap
**Question**: How do we go from validated concept to scaled offering?
**When**: After validation, before commercial launch
**Time**: 1–2 days
**Common pitfall**: Skipping the hand-off from innovation team to operations

## Sequencing canvases in a workshop

A common pursuit-stage discovery workshop:

1. **Team Charter** (60 min) — set working agreements
2. **Context Canvas** (60 min) — what's true about the world the client operates in
3. **Customer Journey** (90 min) — what's true about the customer's experience
4. **Empathy Map** for the most-affected persona (30 min)
5. **Value Proposition Canvas** (60 min) — what offer fits this job
6. **Business Model Canvas** (90 min) — model the business
7. **Riskiest Assumption** (30 min) — what kills this if we're wrong

Total: ~7 hours of canvas work, fits in a 1-day workshop with breaks and integration time.
