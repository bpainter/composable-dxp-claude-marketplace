# Virtual / hybrid meeting checklist

Pre-, during-, and post-session checks for remote and hybrid sessions. Honors Patterns 217 (*Virtual participation*), 235 (*Designing Virtual Sessions*), 312 (*Facilitating Online DesignSessions*).

## T-1 week

- [ ] Confirm tooling: video platform, shared canvas (Miro / FigJam / Mural), backchannel chat, recording (with consent)
- [ ] Pre-work brief sent (use [`template-pre-work-brief.md`](template-pre-work-brief.md))
- [ ] Calendar invites include: dial-in link, canvas link, agenda link, contact for tech issues
- [ ] Time zones explicit in every artifact; nobody guessing
- [ ] Bandwidth check for any participants in low-connectivity locations
- [ ] Hybrid: confirm room AV — multiple cameras, omnidirectional mic, second screen for the canvas

## T-1 day

- [ ] Co-facilitator coverage (one drives the canvas; one drives the conversation; one watches chat — at least two roles for groups >8)
- [ ] Tech rehearsal with all facilitators
- [ ] Canvas pre-built with templates / sticky-note grids ready
- [ ] Music playlist ready (Pattern 130) for openings / breaks / energy shifts
- [ ] Backup plan if primary tool fails (alternative video link; phone bridge; offline canvas)

## T-30 minutes

- [ ] All facilitators in the room/call early
- [ ] Audio check, video check, screen share check
- [ ] Canvas is open and visible; no surprises when participants arrive
- [ ] Greet early arrivers warmly; chat is open

## During the session

### Opening rituals
- [ ] Camera-on encouragement (with explicit grace for those who can't)
- [ ] Round-robin check-in even if briefly — ensures every voice has been heard once before any decision
- [ ] Norms named explicitly: how to ask questions (chat vs. raise hand), when cameras are expected, how breaks work

### Throughout
- [ ] Check chat every 5 minutes minimum (the silent voices live there)
- [ ] Use polls and dot-voting in the canvas to make participation visible
- [ ] Break every 50–60 minutes (vs. 75–90 for in-person — virtual fatigue is faster)
- [ ] Hybrid: every 10 minutes, deliberately turn the camera or attention to remote participants; do not let in-room dynamics swallow them
- [ ] Hybrid: every input from in-room must be surfaced for remote (typed, repeated, captured on canvas)

### Energy management
- [ ] Movement breaks (stand up, look out window, stretch) — explicit, not assumed
- [ ] Music between activities to mark transitions
- [ ] Watch for camera-off proliferation as a signal of energy loss
- [ ] If energy drops, change modality (canvas → discussion, discussion → silent writing, silent writing → polling)

### Decision moments
- [ ] Decisions captured live on the canvas, not in the facilitator's notes
- [ ] Read the decision back; ask for thumbs up / sideways / down before moving on
- [ ] Owner names go on the canvas next to the decision, not in chat

## Closing rituals

- [ ] Recap of decisions visible on the canvas
- [ ] Each owner says their commitment out loud (one sentence)
- [ ] Plus / Delta or one-word close so the room can register feedback before leaving
- [ ] Confirm where the artifacts will live and who has access

## T+24 hours

- [ ] Recap sent (use [`template-workshop-recap.md`](template-workshop-recap.md))
- [ ] Canvas snapshot exported and stored alongside the recap
- [ ] Recording (if taken) stored in agreed location with retention rule
- [ ] Action items entered into the team's actual task system (not left in chat)

## Hybrid-specific traps

The most common hybrid failures:

- **In-room side conversations that exclude remote** — name them and bring them back to the canvas
- **Camera framing that puts remote off to the side of in-room** — remote gets a dedicated screen at the same height as in-room participants
- **Voting/polling that uses raised hands in-room and chat for remote** — use the same digital tool for both
- **In-room people having coffee while remote watches** — break together or not at all
- **Sponsor in the room, team remote** — flip it: put the sponsor on the call with everyone

## When to refuse the hybrid format

Hybrid is harder than either pure-virtual or pure-in-person. If the workshop has high stakes (decision quality matters more than convenience), recommend either:

- **All in-person**: invest in travel for the small number of remotes
- **All virtual**: equalize all participants

Hybrid is appropriate when the alternative is no session at all, or when the in-room/remote split aligns to a natural sub-team boundary that you can lean into.
