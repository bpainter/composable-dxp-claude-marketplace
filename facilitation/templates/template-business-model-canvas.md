## Block-by-block facilitation prompts

### 1. Customer Segments
**Question**: For whom are we creating value? Who are our most important customers?
**Prompts**: Mass market, niche, segmented, diversified, multi-sided platform. Distinct segments require distinct value propositions.

### 2. Value Propositions
**Question**: What value do we deliver to the customer? Which customer problem are we solving? Which needs are we satisfying?
**Prompts**: Newness, performance, customization, getting the job done, design, brand/status, price, cost reduction, risk reduction, accessibility, convenience.

### 3. Channels
**Question**: Through which channels do our customer segments want to be reached?
**Prompts**: Five phases — awareness, evaluation, purchase, delivery, after-sales. Channel types: direct (own sales force, web), indirect (partner stores, wholesalers).

### 4. Customer Relationships
**Question**: What type of relationship does each customer segment expect us to establish and maintain?
**Prompts**: Personal assistance, dedicated personal assistance, self-service, automated services, communities, co-creation.

### 5. Revenue Streams
**Question**: For what value are our customers really willing to pay?
**Prompts**: Asset sale, usage fee, subscription fees, lending/renting/leasing, licensing, brokerage fees, advertising. Pricing mechanisms: fixed (list, volume-dependent) or dynamic (negotiation, yield management, real-time market).

### 6. Key Resources
**Question**: What key resources do our value propositions require?
**Prompts**: Physical, intellectual (brands, patents, copyrights, data), human, financial.

### 7. Key Activities
**Question**: What key activities do our value propositions require?
**Prompts**: Production, problem solving, platform/network.

### 8. Key Partnerships
**Question**: Who are our key partners and suppliers?
**Prompts**: Strategic alliances between non-competitors; coopetition (between competitors); joint ventures; buyer-supplier relationships.

### 9. Cost Structure
**Question**: What are the most important costs inherent in our business model?
**Prompts**: Cost-driven (lean, low price, automation) vs. value-driven (premium, customization). Characteristics: fixed costs, variable costs, economies of scale, economies of scope.

## Facilitation sequence

1. **Customer Segments first.** Without a defined segment, value propositions float.
2. **Value Propositions next.** What problem you solve for that segment.
3. **Right side (relationships, channels, revenue).** How customers interact and pay.
4. **Left side (resources, activities, partnerships).** What it takes to deliver.
5. **Cost Structure last.** Sums the left side and tests viability against revenue.

## Working agreements during the canvas session

- One sticky note per idea — keep it atomic
- Different colors for different customer segments
- Anything questioned moves to a parking lot block, not deleted
- Photo the canvas every 30 minutes for version history
- The canvas is alive — expect 5–10 iterations before it settles

## When this canvas is the wrong tool

Use the Lean Canvas instead if:
- Pre-revenue startup; problem-solution fit isn't established
- Single product / single segment; the BMC's separation creates more boxes than insight

Use the Value Proposition Canvas (Strategyzer) alongside the BMC when:
- The Value Propositions block is feeling thin or contested — the VPC zooms into the value proposition / customer fit specifically
