# Strategy sketch — 5 Bold Steps Vision

The 5 Bold Steps Vision® canvas, used to convert a vague vision into five concrete steps with named supports and challenges. From Patrick van der Pijl, Lokitz, and Solomon's *Design a Better Business* (Wiley, 2016). For background see [`book-design-a-better-business.md`](../references/book-design-a-better-business.md).

The 5 Bold Steps Vision® is a registered trademark of designabetterbusiness.com.

## When to use this canvas

- The team has talked about its vision for months and made no progress
- A new leader is mobilizing a team and needs to convert ambition to plan
- Strategy has settled into platitudes and needs to be made operational
- Pursuit-stage vision workshops with a client where the deliverable is "what does the future look like"

This is the canvas to reach for when the question is "we know we want to be different — but what would we actually do?"

## Block-by-block facilitation prompts

### Our Vision

A single sentence. Specific enough that it forces a choice. Measurable in some way (qualitative or quantitative).

**Bad**: "Be the leading customer-centric company."
**Good**: "By 2028, every healthcare system in our region uses our platform as the default front door for patient engagement."

### The 5 Bold Steps

Each step is a bold *move*, not a wish. Active verbs. Each one stands alone — could be the headline of an internal memo a year from now.

**Bad**: "Improve customer service."
**Good**: "Replace our 8 regional support teams with one 24-hour service operation by Q3 2027."

The five steps should be:
- **Bold** — would surprise someone not in the room
- **Distinct** — not five flavors of the same idea
- **Sequential or parallel** — clear about which steps depend on which
- **Owned** — each step has a sponsor before the canvas is "done"

### Supports

What you have, or can get, that makes these steps possible:
- People (named teams, named experts)
- Capabilities (technical, organizational, brand)
- Relationships (key partners, customer advocates, executive sponsorship)
- Assets (capital, IP, infrastructure)
- Cultural strengths

### Challenges

What stands in the way. Honest naming, not platitudes:
- Capability gaps (what we can't do today)
- Resistance (who will block this and why)
- Resource constraints (people, time, capital)
- Market or competitive pressures
- Cultural or political obstacles

The discipline: Challenges block Supports must outweigh. If the Challenges side is heavier than the Supports side, the vision is fantasy and the room needs to face that before leaving.

## Facilitation sequence

1. **Vision first** (15 min — silent generation, then group convergence on a single sentence). Do not move on until the room can speak the vision aloud and recognize it as the same sentence.
2. **5 Bold Steps in pairs** (25 min — pairs draft, then full group sees all candidates and votes to the final 5). Ideas in surplus, then convergence.
3. **Supports** (15 min — group brainstorm; one sticky per support).
4. **Challenges** (20 min — slow it down; this is the hardest block; the room will be tempted to soften).
5. **Reality test** (15 min — for each Step, do the Supports realistically outweigh the Challenges? If not, what would have to be true?).
6. **Owners** (10 min — one named owner per Step before the canvas is photographed).

Total: ~100 minutes. Don't try to do this in 60.

## What this canvas pairs with

- **Cover Story Vision**® — when the room can't even start; the magazine-cover prompt unlocks the conversation
- **Design Criteria** — the criteria the 5 Steps must meet; usually facilitated right after the vision settles
- **Business Model Canvas** — when one of the Steps is "build a new business model," the BMC is the next-level-down canvas
- **Transformation Roadmap** (from Collaboration Code Tools) — sequencing the Steps over multi-year horizons
