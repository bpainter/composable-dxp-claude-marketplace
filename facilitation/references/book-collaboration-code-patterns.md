# Book digest: *The Collaboration Code — Patterns*

**Author**: Rob Evans (with origins in MG Taylor System & Method developed by Matt and Gail Taylor, late 1980s)
**Edition referenced**: EY wavespace edition (January 2021), based on Second Edition.
**Why this book exists**: To unpack the "secret sauce" of high-performance collaborative work — the principles and patterns that make a wavespace event different from a normal meeting.

## What this book is

A pattern language for collaborative work. ~50 named patterns organized into nine sections (numbered 100–330). Each pattern is a practitioner-tested principle: the *why* behind a recurring move. Patterns are not exercises (those live in *Tools*) and not models (those live in *Models*). Patterns explain why you'd reach for those things in the first place.

## The nine pattern sections

| Section | Range | Theme |
|---|---|---|
| **100 The Physical Environment** | 101–110 | Room design, materials, what your space teaches participants before you say a word |
| **120 The Energetic Environment** | 121–133 | The intangible container — psychological safety, time, music, brave space |
| **200 Preparation** | 201–208 | What happens before the event starts — and why it starts long before participants arrive |
| **210 Participants** | 211–218 | Who's in the room — whole-system inclusion, breakouts, virtual participation, diversity |
| **220 Workshop Design** | 221–235 | The design moves — requisite variety, recursion, iteration, anticipatory design, virtual session design |
| **240 Process** | 241–253 | How the room moves — sapiential leadership, real-time re-design, allowing latency, honoring resistance |
| **260 Outcomes and Deliverables** | 261–264 | Making the solution last past the event — engaging deliverables, rituals of closure |
| **300 Learning Facilitation** | 301–312 | The facilitator's craft — moving toward fear, conscious co-facilitation, shifting group energy |
| **320 Mastering Facilitation** | 321–331 | The advanced practice — self-transformation, awareness, taking a stand |

## Pattern numbering convention

Three-digit codes. The hundreds digit is the section. The tens digit groups related patterns. The ones digit is the specific pattern. Cross-references in the book use these codes (e.g., "see Pattern 215 The right sizes for breakout groups"). The pattern library skill (`facilitation-collaboration-pattern-library`) preserves this convention.

## Selected patterns by relevance to Bermon's work

These are the patterns to reach for first when designing Slalom Composable DXP workshops:

- **104 Everything speaks** — every detail of the room communicates intent
- **122 Energetic container** — what holds the work safely
- **132 Brave space** — invitation to risk over comfort
- **201 It starts before it "starts"** — the pre-event work that decides outcome
- **203 Don't meet — MOBILIZE!** — frame the gathering as a mobilization, not an information exchange
- **205 Sponsor Design Team as microcosm** — the SDT is the room in miniature
- **211 Get the whole system in the room** — who must be present for the work to land
- **215 The right sizes for breakout groups** — small-group sizing for productive divergence
- **217 Virtual participation** — including remote participants in a physical room
- **221 Requisite variety of design** — the design must match the complexity it's trying to handle
- **222 Recursion** — using the same shape at every scale (10 minutes, 1 hour, 1 day)
- **225 Go slow to go fast** — early time invested compounds
- **228 Rhythms of the body and the day** — when to push, when to break, when to feed people
- **234 Graceful failure** — designing for the activity that doesn't land
- **235 Designing Virtual Sessions** — virtual is not a degraded version of in-person; it's a different design problem
- **243 Move the group** — physical movement as a process tool
- **244 Don't argue. Test!** — replace debate with prototype-and-decide
- **252 Human technology (humans first)** — tooling serves the room, not the other way around
- **253 Honor resistance** — resistance is information; do not bulldoze it
- **263 Rituals of closure** — how the event ends shapes what gets carried forward
- **264 Engaging deliverables** — the artifact must be usable, not archival
- **301 Preparation is the antidote to fear** — calm under uncertainty comes from rehearsal
- **303 It's not about YOU** — the facilitator's ego is the largest risk to outcomes
- **305 Trust in the process (and the KreW)** — when in doubt, do not abandon the design
- **309 Conscious co-facilitation** — design for partner work, including handoffs and tells
- **312 Facilitating Online DesignSessions** — online-specific facilitation moves

## How to reach for this in real work

When designing a Slalom session, walk the section headings (100 → 320) before walking the agenda. Catch what you're forgetting. Most workshop misses are a single pattern not honored — usually one of: pre-event mobilization (200s), whole-system inclusion (210s), recursion (220s), or rituals of closure (260s).
