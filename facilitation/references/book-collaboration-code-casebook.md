# Book digest: *The Collaboration Code — 2019 Casebook*

**Curator**: Rob Evans, with contributions from global wavespace practitioners
**What this book is**: Real-world session designs from working facilitators across the globe. Where *Patterns* explains why and *Tools* explains what, the Casebook shows what good actually looks like in the wild — the sponsor brief, the agenda, the design choices, the things that worked and didn't.

## Why this volume matters

A workshop is not a list of activities. It's an integrated artifact — sponsor frame, room, sequencing, energy management, breakout structure, recap — and the integration is hard to learn from principles alone. The Casebook is the apprenticeship volume. Reading three or four cases adjacent to whatever you're designing is worth more than re-reading any single principle.

Cases are sequenced by length:

- **Three-Day Sessions** — the most common wavespace format

## What every case includes

A case write-up follows a consistent shape — useful as a self-documenting structure for any session you run:

- **Background** — the sponsor's problem
- **Outcomes Sought** — what success looked like
- **Participants** — who was in the room and why
- **Sponsor Design Team** — the SDT that designed the session with the facilitator
- **Agenda** — Scan / Focus / Act flow with named modules and timing
- **Modules deployed** — pulls from the *Tools* volume, named explicitly
- **Patterns honored** — references back to *Patterns* (e.g., "204 Sponsor Design Team as microcosm")
- **Outcomes achieved** — what the room produced
- **Reflections** — what the facilitator learned

## How to use the Casebook

Three reads:

1. **Before designing a session of similar length and stakes** — pull two or three relevant cases. Note the agenda spine (which Scan/Focus/Act tools were chosen, in what order, with what timing).
2. **When stuck on a specific design problem** — search the topic index. Most workshop-design problems someone has already solved.
3. **For module sequencing** — see which Scan tools chain naturally into which Focus tools and which Act tools. The chains in real cases are more reliable than the catalog's neutral organization.

## How this book complements the others

- *Patterns* — the principles
- *Tools* — the catalog of moves
- *Models* — the diagnostic lenses

Read in pairs: a case alongside the *Tools* it uses; a case alongside the *Patterns* it honors.
