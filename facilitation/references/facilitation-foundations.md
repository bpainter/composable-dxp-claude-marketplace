# Facilitation foundations

Cross-cutting principles every skill in this plugin reaches for. The common ground.

## What facilitation is, and what it is not

**Facilitation is the discipline of designing and running structured group processes that produce decisions, alignment, or work product.** It is *not* chairing a meeting, presenting slides, taking notes, or being the smartest voice in the room. The facilitator owns the *process*; the participants own the *content*.

The five things that distinguish facilitation as a craft:

1. **Purpose-first design.** Sessions exist to produce something — a decision, an aligned plan, an artifact, a shared understanding. If you cannot name the something, do not run the session.
2. **Sequenced movement.** A session moves people through stages — typically divergence → insight → convergence → decision (Hoffman, Klein/Newman) or Scan → Focus → Act (MG Taylor / Evans). The order is intentional and irreversible without cost.
3. **Visible process.** Whiteboards, sticky notes, public lists, shared canvases — externalize thinking so the group can see, manipulate, and own it.
4. **Equitable participation.** The loudest person should not dominate; the quietest should not disappear. The facilitator manages this with structure (silent generation, round-robin, voting) rather than personality.
5. **Closure with commitment.** The session ends with a documented decision, named owners, and a checkpoint date. No "we'll figure it out later."

## The four canonical frames

This plugin synthesizes four frames that overlap heavily but use different vocabularies. They are interchangeable in practice. Pick the one your room understands.

### The 5 Ps (workshop-design lingua franca)

- **Purpose** — measurable outcome ("prioritize Q2 initiatives and align on resource trade-offs"), not vague topic ("discuss strategy")
- **People** — who's in the room, hierarchy, relationships, who's pre-aligned, who's friction
- **Preparation** — pre-work, pre-read, shared artifacts, room/tech setup, sponsor briefings
- **Process** — sequenced activities and their timing
- **Payoff** — decisions, owners, timeline, recap, follow-through

### Divergence → Insight → Convergence → Decision (DICD)

The verbal version. Generate possibilities, find the patterns, narrow to the few that survive scrutiny, commit to one.

### Scan → Focus → Act (MG Taylor / Evans)

The wavespace version. Scan the landscape (current state, possibilities, constraints), focus on the few solutions worth designing, act on commitments to mobilize the work. Spans hours, days, or multi-week engagements at the same shape.

### The Six Jobs (Klein & Newman)

The facilitator's own working frame: Scoping, Working with Sponsors, Preparation, Designing, Delivery, Value Capture (+ Satisfaction). What *you* do, sequenced from "we got the call" to "we keep the work alive."

## When to facilitate vs. when to do something else

Facilitation is not the right tool when:

- **The decision is already made.** You don't need a workshop; you need an announcement. Forcing a workshop here destroys trust.
- **There is no real choice.** If only one option meets constraints, name it and move.
- **One person holds the whole picture.** A 1:1 conversation is faster.
- **The conflict is interpersonal, not topical.** Mediation, not facilitation.
- **The right people aren't available.** Reschedule. A workshop without the people who can decide is theater.

Facilitation *is* the right tool when:

- The decision needs cross-functional buy-in to stick
- The right answer is not knowable until you put diverse perspectives in the room
- The group needs shared understanding before it can act
- A pattern has gotten stuck and needs to be broken with new structure
- The work product itself benefits from co-creation (a journey map, a service blueprint, a vision)

## Meeting vs. workshop

Meetings and workshops are different objects with different rules.

| | Meeting | Workshop |
|---|---|---|
| Duration | 15–90 min | 2 hours – multiple days |
| Output | Decisions, alignment, status | Designed artifact (plan, model, prototype) |
| Energy management | Light | Critical (hydration, food, breaks, sequencing) |
| Sponsor involvement | The chair *is* the sponsor | A separate Sponsor Design Team usually exists |
| Pre-work | Optional, light | Required, substantial |
| Documentation | Notes, action items | Recap, photos of artifacts, ongoing artifact in shared space |
| Failure mode | Bad chairing | Bad design |

The Meeting Design (Hoffman) skill owns meetings; the Workshop Designer skill owns workshops. They share principles but differ in scale.

## The facilitator's stance

Across every source in this plugin, four postures recur:

- **Openness** — you don't have the answer; the group does. Stay curious about what surfaces.
- **Neutrality** — your job is not to advance your view. If you have one, share it as data, not as the answer.
- **Confidence** — you know the structure even when the content is uncertain. Silence and tension are not failures; they are where good thinking happens.
- **Presence** — body language, eye contact, energy. People read your nervousness. Calm is contagious; so is panic.

## The Slalom context

Slalom Composable DXP and Enterprise capability work routinely calls for facilitation in three forms:

- **Pursuit and pre-sale workshops** — discovery sessions with prospects, vision workshops, executive co-creation, capability roadmaps. Pursuit Excellence stages 2–4. ([Pursuit Excellence framework](../../../50_Knowledge/Frameworks/Pursuit_Excellence/README.md))
- **Engagement kickoffs and discovery** — Mobilize and Discover phases of Slalom Summit; charter alignment, persona work, current-state mapping, journey mapping, requirements discovery. ([Summit framework](../../../50_Knowledge/Frameworks/Slalom_Summit/README.md))
- **Internal practice operations** — quarterly planning, retros at the practice level, OKR setting, hiring debriefs, calibration prep.

Bermon's career bar: Senior Director / Market Solutions track. Workshops at this level are expected to produce contracted outcomes, not just nice conversations. The facilitation in this plugin assumes that bar.
