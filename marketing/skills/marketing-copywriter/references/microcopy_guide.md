# Microcopy: The Art of Tiny, Mighty Copy

## What is Microcopy?

Microcopy is the small text in your interface that guides, informs, and reassures users:
- Button labels: "Save," "Submit," "Next"
- Form labels and instructions: "Email address," "Password must have 8+ characters"
- Helper text: "We'll never spam you"
- Tooltips: "Why we need this"
- Empty states: "You haven't added any items yet"
- Error messages: "That password is too short"
- Success messages: "Email sent!"
- Confirmation modals: "Are you sure you want to delete?"
- Placeholder text: "e.g., john@example.com"

Microcopy isn't decoration. It's functional, critical to usability, and can increase conversions by 10-20% when done well.

---

## The 3 I's of Microcopy

### Inform
Help users understand what's expected, why you're asking, and what happens next.

**Button label** (not just "Submit"):
- "Create account" (clearer than "Submit")
- "Start free trial" (benefit-focused)
- "Send invoice" (specific action)
- "Save changes" (confirm what's saved)

**Helper text**:
- "We'll use this to send you updates. You can unsubscribe anytime." (answer "why?")
- "Minimum 8 characters, with at least one number" (clear requirement)
- "This cannot be undone" (warning for destructive action)

**Placeholder text**:
- "e.g., john@example.com" (show expected format)
- "Search by name or email" (clarify what you can search)

### Influence
Subtly encourage the right action using psychology, reassurance, and social proof.

**Reassurance copy** (reduce friction):
- "30-day money-back guarantee. No questions." (remove risk)
- "Your card won't be charged until you confirm." (remove surprise)
- "This saves most teams 5+ hours per week." (social proof)

**Urgency without pressure**:
- "Only 2 spots left" (limited availability, not aggressive)
- "Trial ends in 5 days. Upgrade anytime." (heads up, not threat)

**Positive framing**:
- "Create account" (positive) vs. "Don't skip" (negative)
- "Save changes" (affirmative) vs. "Unsaved changes warning" (defensive)

### Interact
Prompt the next action or interaction in a friendly, clear way.

**CTAs**:
- "Save and continue" (what happens after click)
- "Send password reset email" (specific action)
- "Skip this step" (acknowledge user choice)

**Engagement prompts**:
- "Swipe to see more" (guide discovery)
- "Enter email to get started" (specific ask)
- "Choose a plan below" (navigation hint)

## Microcopy by Context

### Form Labels & Instructions

**Standard fields**:
- **Email address** (clear field name)
- Helper: "We use this to confirm your account and send updates"
- Placeholder: "e.g., you@example.com"

**Password field**:
- **Create a password** (actionable, not just "Password")
- Helper: "At least 8 characters, with at least one number or symbol"
- Strength indicator: "Weak/Strong" as they type

**Checkbox**:
- **I agree to the Terms** (full statement, not truncated)
- Helper below: "We respect your privacy. Read the full terms."

---

### Error Messages

**Bad error** (doesn't help):
"Error 422"
"Invalid input"
"Something went wrong"

**Good error** (explains + guides):
"That email is already registered. Did you mean to sign in instead?"
"Password too short. Try 8+ characters with at least one number."
"Couldn't save. Check your connection and try again."

**Pattern**:
- [ ] Name the problem
- [ ] Explain why it happened
- [ ] Show the solution

---

### Success Messages

**Bad success** (no context):
"Success"
"Complete"
"Done"

**Good success** (confirms + next step):
"Account created! Check your email for a verification link."
"Payment received. Your invoice has been sent to accounting@company.com."
"Form saved. You can continue editing anytime."

**Pattern**:
- [ ] Confirm what happened
- [ ] What happens next
- [ ] When it happens

---

### Empty States

**Bad empty state** (confuses):
Blank screen with no context

**Good empty state** (guides):
- **Headline**: "You haven't added any projects yet"
- **Explanation**: "Projects help you organize your work"
- **CTA**: "Create your first project"

---

### Confirmation Modals

**Destructive action**:
- **Headline**: "Delete this project?"
- **Body**: "This will permanently delete all tasks and files in this project. This cannot be undone."
- **Actions**: "Cancel" / "Yes, delete"

**Non-destructive confirmation**:
- **Body**: "Send email to john@example.com?"
- **Actions**: "Cancel" / "Send"

---

### Tooltips & Inline Help

**When to use**:
- Explaining why you're asking for a field
- Clarifying complex terms
- Showing keyboard shortcuts
- Providing guidance without interrupting

**Best practices**:
- Keep under 2 sentences
- Appear on hover (desktop) or tap (mobile)
- Don't repeat the label
- Use plain language

**Examples**:
- "Why do we need this?" → "This helps us send you a password reset if you ever forget it"
- "What's a phone number?" → "We use this to verify your account if needed"
- "SKU?" → "Product code (e.g., SHIRT-S-BLU for blue shirt, small)"

## Microcopy Writing Principles

### 1. Be Specific
**Vague**: "Enter your details"
**Specific**: "Enter your work email (you'll use this to sign in)"

### 2. Be Action-Oriented
**Passive**: "Password is required"
**Active**: "Create a password"

### 3. Be Concise
**Wordy**: "In order to ensure your account is secure, we require a password"
**Concise**: "Create a strong password"

### 4. Be Conversational
**Corporate**: "Submit form to complete transaction"
**Conversational**: "Finish checkout"

### 5. Be Empathetic (Especially for Errors)
**Cold**: "Invalid email format"
**Empathetic**: "That doesn't look like an email. Try something like: john@example.com"

### 6. Use Positive Framing
**Negative**: "Don't forget to verify your email"
**Positive**: "Verify your email to unlock features"

### 7. Guide, Don't Assume
**Assumes knowledge**: "Select your instance"
**Guides**: "Choose which workspace you want to sign into"

## Button Label Patterns

| Action | Button Label | NOT |
|--------|--------------|-----|
| Create | "Create [item]" | "Submit," "Add," "New" |
| Save | "Save changes" | "Submit," "OK" |
| Delete | "Delete [item]" | "Remove," "Do it" |
| Start | "Start free trial" | "Begin," "Go" |
| Sign up | "Create account" | "Register," "Sign up" |
| Send | "Send email" | "Submit," "Go" |
| Continue | "Continue to checkout" | "Next," "Proceed" |
| Confirm | "Yes, [action]" | "OK," "Confirm" |
| Cancel | "Cancel" or "Skip" | "No," "Abort" |
| Learn more | "Learn more" | "Read more," "Details" |

## Microcopy Tone by Context

### Professional SaaS
- Clear, confident, concise
- Example: "Billing address won't be public. Only for invoices."

### Friendly B2C
- Warm, conversational, relatable
- Example: "No spam. We promise. Unsubscribe anytime."

### Emergency/Error
- Empathetic, solution-focused, human
- Example: "Oops, we lost your progress. Here's the last saved version."

### Financial
- Trustworthy, specific, reassuring
- Example: "Your card won't be charged until you confirm."

---

## Microcopy Audit Checklist

For any interface, ask:
- [ ] Is every button label clear about what happens?
- [ ] Do form instructions explain why you're asking?
- [ ] Are error messages helpful or confusing?
- [ ] Is there placeholder text showing expected format?
- [ ] Are empty states guiding users to next action?
- [ ] Do success messages confirm + show next step?
- [ ] Is tone consistent across the product?
- [ ] Could any text be shorter while staying clear?
- [ ] Would a first-time user understand what to do?
- [ ] Are there any jargon terms that should be explained?

## Microcopy Examples: Before & After

### Form Field
**Before**: "First name" (assumption of understanding)

### Error Message
**Before**: "Invalid input" (confusing)

### Empty State

**After**: "No invoices yet. Ready to send one? Here's how."

### Button
**Before**: "Submit" (generic)

### Helper Text
**Before**: (none)
**After**: "We never share your email. You can unsubscribe anytime." (reassurance)

### Success Message
**Before**: "Complete" (vague)
**After**: "Invoice sent! You can see it in the Sent folder." (specific + location)
