# Copy Writing Frameworks and Formulas

## The AIDA Framework (Attention, Interest, Desire, Action)

Used for sales pages, email, and ads.

**Attention**: Hook that stops the scroll or compels them to read
**Interest**: Why this matters or what's at stake
**Desire**: How their life changes with the solution
**Action**: Clear, benefit-focused CTA

### Example (SaaS Landing Page)
**Attention**: "Spend hours fixing spreadsheet errors instead of strategy? We get it."
**Interest**: "Finance teams lose 30% of their time to manual data tasks."
**Desire**: "Automate your data pulls, freeing your team for analysis and forecasting."
**Action**: "Try free for 14 days. No credit card required."

---

## The PAS Framework (Problem, Agitate, Solve)

Emphasizes empathy and pain before relief.

**Problem**: Name the specific problem (make them nod)
**Agitate**: Show consequences if unsolved (emotional escalation)
**Solve**: Present your solution as relief

### Example (B2C Product)
**Problem**: "You hate morning emails. There are 47 by 9am."
**Agitate**: "Your inbox controls your day. You're always behind."
**Solve**: "SmartFilter learns your priorities. Only important emails land in Inbox. The rest? Organized by category. You'll actually get to what matters."

---

## The Before/After/Bridge Framework

Great for emotional resonance, especially lifestyle and B2C.

**Before**: Paint picture of current reality (struggle, frustration)
**After**: Paint picture of transformed reality (relief, confidence, success)
**Bridge**: How your solution creates the transformation

### Example (Fitness)
**Before**: "Monday morning. You hit snooze for the third time. You're tired, unmotivated, and haven't worked out in weeks."
**After**: "Monday morning. You wake up energized. Your workout is done by 6:30am. You feel strong, confident, and ready for your day."
**Bridge**: "Fitly's AI coach learns your schedule and energy patterns, then programs workouts you actually want to do. Consistency becomes automatic."

## The Feature-to-Benefit Translation

Never lead with features. Always translate to user benefit.

**Feature**: "Cloud-based platform with real-time collaboration"
**Benefit**: "Your team syncs instantly. No more waiting for the latest file version. No more accidental overwrites."

**Feature**: "Mobile app with offline mode"
**Benefit**: "Work anywhere. No WiFi? No problem. Sync when you're back online."

**Feature**: "256-bit AES encryption"
**Benefit**: "Your data is as secure as military-grade encryption. Sleep soundly knowing your information is protected."

---

## The Value Stack Framework

Perfect for pricing pages and feature lists.

Instead of: "Advanced reporting, custom dashboards, API access"
Frame as: "Make data-driven decisions with 50+ pre-built reports customized to your business. Build custom views that matter to you. Connect Slack, Zapier, and other tools without code."

Each feature ties to a benefit, not just listing capabilities.

---

## The Objection Handling Framework

Anticipate and address buyer concerns before they ask.

**Objection**: "This looks expensive"
**Answer**: "Most of our customers save $50K/year in labor time alone. They pay for themselves in 3 months."

**Objection**: "How long does implementation take?"
**Answer**: "Most companies go live in 2 weeks. Our onboarding team guides you step-by-step."

**Objection**: "What if we don't like it?"
**Answer**: "30-day money-back guarantee. No questions. We only succeed if you do."

## Email Copy Formulas

### Welcome Email
- **Subject line**: Curiosity or welcome (not spam)
- **Opening**: Acknowledge decision to sign up, set expectations
- **Body**: Lead with value (what they'll get from your emails, not what you sell)
- **CTA**: One clear action (explore product, watch tutorial, book call)

Example:
Subject: "Your first task: [benefit statement]"
Body: "Welcome! You signed up for a reason. Let's get you to [outcome] as fast as possible. First step: [action]. This usually takes 5 minutes and unlocks [benefit]."

### Nurture Email
- **Subject line**: Specific value or curiosity
- **Opening**: Reference their behavior or context
- **Body**: One idea or story, with practical takeaway
- **CTA**: Next step (read article, download resource, book call)

Example:
Subject: "The mistake we see most in [domain]"
Body: "We analyzed 500 companies like yours. Here's what separates the winners: [insight]. How to apply this: [framework]. Questions? Reply to this email or book time."

### Abandonment Email
- **Subject line**: Reason to come back (curiosity, discount, or FOMO)
- **Opening**: Acknowledge they didn't complete action, remove pressure
- **Body**: Address likely objection or friction point
- **CTA**: Easy re-engagement (try again, get help, get discount)

Example:
Subject: "One question before you go"
Body: "Noticed you started but didn't finish signup. No judgment—sometimes timing isn't right. Two quick questions: [option A] or [option B]? Or reply and tell us what would help."

## Microcopy Patterns

### Form Labels
**Bad**: "Contact info"
**Good**: "How should we reach you? (Email works best)"

### Helper Text
**Bad**: "Required field"
**Good**: "We'll use this to send you updates. You can unsubscribe anytime."

### Placeholder Text
**Bad**: (empty field)
**Good**: "e.g., john@example.com"

### Error Messages
**Bad**: "Error 422"
**Good**: "That password is too short. Try 8+ characters with a number."

### Success Message
**Bad**: "Complete"
**Good**: "Account created! Check your email for a welcome message. You can also get started here."

### Empty State
**Bad**: (blank page)
**Good**: "You haven't added any projects yet. Ready to create one? Start here."

## Tone Frameworks

### Professional (B2B, SaaS)
- **Tone**: Confident, informed, approachable
- **Vocabulary**: Clear, specific, evidence-based
- **Sentence structure**: Varied but readable; avoid run-ons
- **Example**: "Our analytics dashboard saves finance teams 10 hours per week on reporting. See how Acme cut close time by 30%."

### Friendly (B2C, Lifestyle, DTC)
- **Tone**: Conversational, relatable, human
- **Vocabulary**: Simple, accessible, personal
- **Sentence structure**: Short, varied, natural flow
- **Example**: "We get it—laundry sucks. Dirty clothes pile up. You're out of clean socks again. SockFresh solves this. Fresh socks delivered every month. One less thing to worry about."

### Authoritative (Finance, Legal, Enterprise)
- **Tone**: Trustworthy, credible, clear
- **Vocabulary**: Precise, no shortcuts, backed by evidence
- **Sentence structure**: Direct, structured, no ambiguity
- **Example**: "We manage $10B in assets for 500+ institutional clients. 99.99% uptime SLA. SOC 2 Type II certified. Independent audits available upon request."

### Empathetic (Support, Emergency, Apology)
- **Tone**: Understanding, solution-focused, human
- **Vocabulary**: Simple, compassionate, action-oriented
- **Sentence structure**: Short, warm, direct
- **Example**: "Your order didn't arrive. That's frustrating and we're sorry. Here's what we're doing: [action]. A $25 credit has been applied to your account. Questions? Reply here or call us."

## Headline Formulas

### Curiosity Headline
"The [adjective] [noun] that [benefit]"
Example: "The email template that increased signups by 40%"

### Problem/Promise Headline
"[Specific problem]. [Clear solution]."
Example: "Your team spends 3 hours in standup meetings. Try async standups instead."

### Question Headline
"What if [aspirational outcome]?"
Example: "What if you could ship features 10x faster?"

### Contrarian Headline
"[Conventional wisdom] is [why it's wrong]"
Example: "Long email subject lines convert better than short ones. Here's why."

### Number/Data Headline
"[Number] [surprising insight] about [topic]"
Example: "87% of teams still manage projects in spreadsheets. Here's how to escape."

### How/Why Headline
"How to [benefit] in [timeframe]"
Example: "How to reduce your customer churn by 20% in 90 days"

## Copy Testing Checklist

Before launching copy, ask:
- [ ] Is the headline compelling? Does it stop the reader?
- [ ] Is the problem clear? Do I nod at "yes, that's my problem"?
- [ ] Is the solution obvious? Do I understand what you offer?
- [ ] Is the benefit clear? Do I know how my life changes?
- [ ] Is it believable? Is there proof or evidence?
- [ ] Is the CTA clear? Do I know what to do next?
- [ ] Is it jargon-free? Would my non-technical friend understand it?
- [ ] Is it scannable? Can I skim and understand the key points?
- [ ] Is it empathetic? Does it show you understand me?
- [ ] Is it concise? Any words I can cut without losing meaning?

## Common Copy Mistakes

1. **Leading with features, not benefits**: "Auto-scaling infrastructure" vs. "Never worry about traffic spikes melting your server again."

2. **Using marketing speak**: "Best-in-class solutions," "cutting-edge," "world-class" (no evidence)

3. **Burying the headline**: Making users scroll to understand what you offer

4. **Too many CTAs**: Confusing readers about what to do next

5. **Ignoring objections**: Not addressing why someone might not buy

6. **Using jargon**: "Synergistic paradigm shift" vs. "Work better together"

7. **Not scannable**: Dense paragraphs; no breaks, bullets, or headers

8. **Mismatched tone**: Overly casual for finance, overly formal for lifestyle

9. **No social proof**: Stats, testimonials, logos—buyers want to know others trust you

10. **Weak CTA**: "Submit" instead of "Claim your free trial"
