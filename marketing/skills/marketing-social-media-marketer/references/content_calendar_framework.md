# Social Media Content Calendar Framework

## Content Pillar Structure

Define 3-5 content pillars that guide what you post, ensuring diversity and consistency without burnout.

### Example Pillars for B2B SaaS (LinkedIn)
1. **Thought Leadership**: Industry insights, trends, contrarian takes, founder voice (20%)
2. **Educational**: How-tos, tips, frameworks, case studies, data insights (30%)
3. **Company Culture**: Behind-the-scenes, team spotlights, company news (15%)
4. **Customer Stories**: Testimonials, case studies, success metrics, social proof (25%)
5. **Community Engagement**: Asks, polls, questions, response posts (10%)

### Example Pillars for B2C (Instagram)
1. **Product Showcase**: Product photography, features, styling (25%)
2. **Lifestyle / Aspiration**: How customers use product, dream moments (30%)
3. **Behind-the-Scenes**: Creation, team, day-in-the-life, raw moments (20%)
4. **Educational**: Tips, hacks, guides, "how to" content (15%)
5. **Entertainment**: Relatable reels, trending audio, personality (10%)

## Posting Cadence by Platform

### LinkedIn
- **Company page**: 2-3 posts per week (Tuesday-Thursday, 8am-10am your audience timezone)
- **Founder/executive**: 3-5 posts per week (mix of weekday and weekend)
- **Engagement window**: Peak comments in first 1 hour; engagement continues for 48 hours

### Instagram
- **Feed posts (carousels, static)**: 2-3 times per week
- **Reels**: 1-2 times per week
- **Stories**: 1-3 times per day (morning, afternoon, evening)
- **Best posting times**: 9-11am, 12-1pm, 5-7pm (audience dependent; test your analytics)

## Content Batching Strategy

Batching saves time and ensures consistency:

1. **Batch by pillar**: Spend 2 hours writing 4 thought leadership posts instead of writing 1 at a time
2. **Batch by format**: Create all video captions at once, all carousel hooks together
3. **Batch by timing**: Plan monthly content in a single session, weekly in smaller sprints
4. **Batch by creative**: Photo shoot = 20-30 images used across multiple posts (carousel, stories, reels)

**Sample batching schedule**:
- **Week 1**: Write all captions and hooks for the month
- **Week 2**: Design graphics and source media
- **Week 3**: Schedule content in platform/scheduling tool
- **Weeks 4+**: Monitor analytics and optimize next month's strategy

## Optimization Loop

### Weekly Review
- Check analytics: what got most engagement?
- Identify patterns: format, time, pillar, topic
- Note: What surprised you? What flopped?
- Adjustment: Next week, double down on winners

### Monthly Reporting
- Engagement rate trend
- Top 5 posts by engagement, reach, CTR
- Audience growth rate
- Conversion tracking (UTM clicks, DM volume, sales attributed)
- Recommendations for next month

## Caption Writing Formula (AIDA)

**Attention**: Hook that stops the scroll (first sentence)
**Interest**: Why this matters, data point, or story setup (sentence 2-3)
**Desire**: Emotional benefit or proof that this is valuable (sentence 4-5)
**Action**: Clear CTA (final sentence or emoji)

### Example (Instagram Carousel)
"Swipe through our process for [benefit]—it sounds simple, but it's changed everything for our customers.

[Story or context]

This is what we've learned after [time period]:
- [Key learning 1]
- [Key learning 2]
- [Key learning 3]

Save this, share with [audience], and let me know what resonates."

## Platform-Specific Best Practices

### LinkedIn
- **Comment first, then post**: Engage with 5-10 posts in your network before posting your own
- **Reply to every comment** in the first hour; this signals to the algorithm to show your post more
- **Use line breaks** for readability; long text blocks don't perform
- **Test posting times**: Experiment with 6am, 8am, 10am, 12pm, 5pm to find your audience's peak time
- **Tag people strategically**: 1-3 relevant tags drive visibility without feeling spammy
- **Ask questions**: Posts with questions get 5x more comments than statements
- **Avoid sharing external URLs** if possible; LinkedIn prioritizes native content

### Instagram
- **Use 20-30 hashtags** (not stuffed in caption; reply to your own post with hashtag comment)
- **Post Stories consistently**: New stories daily signal active account (pushes to top of follower list)
- **Engage with others' Stories**: Mention creators, repost UGC (gives you visibility)
- **Captions should be scannable**: Use emojis as visual separators, not as text replacement
- **Trending audio window**: Use trending sounds within 7 days of trend peak for algorithm boost
- **Turn off likes** if your goal isn't vanity metrics; instead, focus on comments and saves
- **DMs are gold**: Respond to all DMs within 24 hours; this drives engagement and conversion

## Measuring Success: Metrics That Matter

### Awareness Stage
- Reach (unique accounts who see content)
- Impressions (total times content is seen)
- Video views (first 3 seconds, completion rate)
- Follower growth rate

### Engagement Stage
- Engagement rate (comments + shares + saves) / impressions
- Comment count and quality
- Share rate (external shares)
- Save rate (indicates value)

### Conversion Stage
- CTR (click-through rate to link)
- DM conversion (profile visits → DMs)
- Website traffic from social (UTM tracking)
- Email sign-ups from social link
- Direct sales attributed to social

### Community Stage
- DM volume and response quality
- Repeat commenters (loyal community)
- UGC volume and reach
- Follower quality (not just quantity)

## Red Flags in Your Strategy

1. **Posting inconsistently**: Irregular posting kills reach. Consistency > perfection.
2. **Not responding to engagement**: Ignoring comments signals disinterest to the algorithm.
3. **Over-posting with no strategy**: 10 posts/day with no clear pillar dilutes your message.
4. **Measuring only vanity metrics**: Likes don't drive business outcomes.
5. **Copying competitors**: Your unique voice is your edge. Copy frameworks, not content.
6. **Ignoring analytics**: Posting blind means you're guessing, not learning.
7. **Buying followers or engagement**: Fake engagement kills credibility and algorithm performance.
8. **No clear CTA**: Engagement without conversion is wasted effort.
