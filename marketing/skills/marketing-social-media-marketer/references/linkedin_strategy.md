# LinkedIn Strategy: From Organic to Leads

## LinkedIn's Algorithm (2025)

LinkedIn's algorithm prioritizes:
1. **Engagement velocity**: Comments, shares, and reactions in the first hour (signals relevance)
2. **Connection with the author**: If you interact with this person regularly, their content ranks higher in your feed
3. **Legitimate engagement**: Comments with substance > likes > low-effort reactions
4. **Content type**: Text posts with hooks outperform reshares of external links
5. **Video uploads** (native): Native LinkedIn videos get 20x more views than YouTube links
6. **Relevance**: Are comments, shares, and saves coming from your target audience?

---

## Content Performance Baseline (2025)

**Typical LinkedIn post engagement rates by type**:
- Text post with strong hook: 3-7% engagement rate
- Carousel (PDF or multi-image): 5-12% engagement rate (highest ROI)
- Native video: 4-10% engagement rate
- Document (long-form): 2-5% engagement rate
- External link share: 0.5-2% engagement rate (lowest)

**Why carousels win**: They are consumed fully (swiping), encourage saves, and get algorithm boost for completion behavior.

## LinkedIn Content Strategy for Thought Leaders

### The Authority Arc
Build credibility through a narrative arc:

1. **Share perspective**: "Here's how I think about [topic]"
   - Your unique POV grounded in experience
   - Mix of industry insight and contrarian takes
   - Creates differentiation

2. **Build with vulnerability**: "Here's what I got wrong" or "Here's where I struggled"
   - Vulnerability increases relatability and engagement
   - Shows growth mindset
   - Makes you human, not just a guru

3. **Deliver proof**: "Here's what actually works" with data, case study, or framework
   - Backs up your claims with evidence
   - Practical and actionable
   - Demonstrates competence and results

4. **Make it useful**: "Here's how you can apply this"
   - Templates, frameworks, checklists
   - Reduce friction to adoption
   - People save and share useful content

## B2B Lead Generation on LinkedIn

### The LinkedIn Sales Funnel

| Stage | Tactic | Goal | Example |
|-------|--------|------|---------|
| **Awareness** | Organic content, thought leadership | Get on people's radar | 500+ monthly views |
| **Interest** | Case studies, how-tos, industry insights | Build credibility | 100+ comments, profile visits |
| **Consideration** | DM outreach with personalization | Start conversation | 10+ qualified DM conversations |
| **Decision** | Calendar link, proposal, trial access | Close | 3-5 new opportunities |

### Organic Lead Gen Strategy
1. **Share POV and insights** (2-3x/week): Establishes authority
2. **Case studies and proof** (1x/week): Shows you deliver results
3. **Engage your network's posts** (daily): Creates visibility, builds relationships
4. **Monitor DMs and comments** (daily): 80% of leads come from direct outreach, not passive clicks

### DM Outreach Framework

**Step 1: Profile Visit** (without immediate message)
- Visit their profile, read recent activity, understand context
- This increases chance they notice you

**Step 2: Engage Authentically** (before the ask)
- Comment on their recent posts with genuine, value-adding comment
- Builds recognition and social proof

**Step 3: Personalized DM** (5-7 days later)
- Reference specific thing you noticed (post, company update, mutual connection)
- Mention why you think there's mutual value
- Keep it short (2-3 sentences)
- Include a specific ask (call, demo, info)

**Bad DM**: "Hi John, I work at [company] selling [solution]. Let's connect. Link in profile."
**Good DM**: "John, saw your post about [topic]. We worked with 3 companies in your space and found [specific insight]. Would you be open to a brief call to discuss?"

## Employee Advocacy at Scale

**Why it works**: Content from employees gets 8x more engagement than company page posts.

### Setup
1. **Train employees**: Show them what to post (don't be spammy)
2. **Provide content**: Curate 2-3 posts per week they can share
3. **Make it easy**: Share direct links, not just guidelines
4. **Incentivize**: Recognize/celebrate top sharers, tie to company goals

### Content to Amplify via Employees
- Company news and announcements
- Customer wins and testimonials
- Thought leadership from leadership
- Team milestones and culture
- Industry insights

### Results
- 5-8x engagement multiplier
- Broader reach (employees' networks ≠ company page followers)
- Higher trust (message from person > message from brand)
- More qualified leads (reaching decision-makers in companies)

## LinkedIn Paid Strategy

### Campaign Types

**Sponsored Content** (best for awareness and engagement)
- Your organic content boosted with paid reach
- Target by: job title, industry, company, seniority
- Budget: $500-2000/month for testing

**Lead Gen Forms** (best for capture)
- Form auto-fills with LinkedIn profile data
- High conversion because friction is low
- Budget: $1000-5000/month

**Conversation Ads** (best for engagement)
- Automated DM message when users click
- Route to demo booking or asset
- Budget: $500-1500/month

**Retargeting** (best for nurture)
- Target people who visited your website
- Reinforce message, build top-of-mind
- Budget: $300-1000/month

### Paid Best Practices
- **Test audience segments**: Small budgets ($100-200) to find who responds
- **Test creative**: Multiple headlines and images
- **Test CTAs**: "Learn more," "Download," "Book a demo," "Get started" have different conversion rates
- **Track conversions**: Use UTM parameters, pixel tracking, or lead form attribution
- **Optimize for engaged audience**: Don't optimize for clicks; optimize for landing page conversions or DM engagement

## LinkedIn Video Strategy

### Why Video Works
- Native LinkedIn videos get 5-20x more views than external video links
- Video completion rate signals quality (algorithm boost)
- Video allows storytelling and personality (increases engagement)
- Short-form video (30-90 seconds) performs better than long-form

### Format Ideas
1. **Founder/leader talking to camera**: Industry takes, career advice, company updates
2. **Customer testimonial**: 30-60 second snippet of customer talking about results
3. **Behind-the-scenes**: Product development, team moments, day-in-the-life
4. **How-to / Tutorial**: 60-90 seconds of demonstrating a framework or tactic
5. **Data visualization**: Animated infographic explaining industry trend

### Upload Best Practices
- **Upload native, don't link**: Use LinkedIn's video upload, not YouTube
- **Add captions**: 85% of videos are watched without sound
- **Optimize thumbnail**: Upload custom thumbnail (compelling visual)
- **Hook in first 3 seconds**: Lead with benefit or curiosity
- **Keep it short**: 30-90 seconds optimal (longer needs strong hook)
- **Call-to-action**: Direct viewers to engage or visit link

## LinkedIn Company Page Optimization

### About Section
- **Headline**: Clear, benefit-driven description (not just company tagline)
- **Description**: 3-4 sentences on what you do, for whom, why it matters
- **Website link**: Direct to most important landing page (not homepage)
- **Specialties**: 5-10 keywords, searchable topics

### Featured Section
- **Featured content**: Curate your best 5-10 pieces (case studies, product pages, brand guidelines)
- **Employees**: Showcase team; increases trust
- **Life tab**: Showcase culture, team, behind-the-scenes

### Hashtag Strategy
- **Follow industry hashtags**: #SaaS, #HRTech, #DigitalTransformation (audience targets these)
- **Use branded hashtag**: #YourCompanyName (builds community)
- **Use post-specific hashtags**: 3-5 most relevant (algorithm relevance)
- **Avoid**: Stuffing hashtags in the middle of text; looks spammy

### Posting Cadence
- **Company page**: 2-3 posts per week (more is noise; less loses momentum)
- **Employee shares**: Encourage 5-10 employees to share weekly
- **Total reach**: 3x posts/week from company + employee amplification = significant monthly reach

## LinkedIn Lead Gen Checklist

- [ ] Your profile is 100% complete with professional photo, headline, about section
- [ ] You post original insights or curate industry content 3x/week
- [ ] You engage on others' posts daily (comments, not just reactions)
- [ ] Your DMs are open and you respond within 24 hours
- [ ] You have a 30-second pitch for what you do and who it's for
- [ ] You track profile views and DM conversation trends monthly
- [ ] You have a calendar link (Calendly, Notion) in profile or DM follow-up
- [ ] You're NOT being spammy: mass DMs, generic outreach, or self-promotion only
- [ ] You understand your audience's pain points and speak to them
- [ ] You measure success by DM conversations → calls booked, not just likes
