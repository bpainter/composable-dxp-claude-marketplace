# Community Building & Growth Strategy

## Community Strategy Framework

### Three Engagement Phases

**Pre-Event** (4+ weeks before)
- Build awareness and excitement
- Secure attendees, speakers, sponsors
- Create content and messaging
- Establish partnerships

**During Event** (event day)
- Facilitate connections and conversations
- Create memorable moments
- Capture engagement and content
- Build relationships

**Post-Event** (ongoing)
- Sustain momentum and relationships
- Gather feedback and iterate
- Plan next gathering
- Nurture community members

## Audience Personas & Journey

### Example: Tech Professional Community

**Persona: Sarah (Early-Stage Founder)**
- Motivation: Network with other founders, learn best practices
- Pain point: Hard to find relevant events in her niche
- Engagement: Attends 2-3 events/year, active on social
- Retention: Keeps attending if makes meaningful connections

**Persona: Mike (Senior Developer)**
- Motivation: Stay current on tech trends, mentor others
- Pain point: Events are often too beginner-focused or shallow
- Engagement: Prefers deep technical content and peer discussions
- Retention: Keeps attending if learns something new

**Persona: Jane (Community Organizer)**
- Motivation: Build platform, create impact for her community
- Pain point: Feels invisible/unrecognized for her work
- Engagement: Wants to volunteer or speak at events
- Retention: Keeps engaged if gets recognition and growth opportunity

### Attendee Journey Map

```
AWARENESS
- Friend recommendation
- Social media post
- Email from community group
- PR coverage

CONSIDERATION
- Read event details
- Check speaker bios
- Review past attendee testimonials
- Verify event relevance

DECISION
- Register for event
- Pay for ticket (if applicable)
- Add to calendar
- Share with network

ATTENDANCE
- Check-in and get badge
- Network in hallway
- Attend sessions
- Participate in activities

POST-EVENT
- Share photos/thoughts on social
- Complete feedback survey
- Follow up on new connections
- Decide if attending next event
```

## Community Growth Model

### Three-Month Growth Cycle

**Month 1: Awareness**
- Target: Reach 1,000 people with event awareness
- Tactics: Email to existing community, social media, partnerships, PR
- Metrics: Impressions, email open rate, website traffic
- Messaging: "What to expect" and "Why this event"

**Month 2: Consideration**
- Target: Convert 20-30% of aware audience to registrants
- Tactics: Speaker announcements, testimonials, FOMO messaging, early bird pricing
- Metrics: Registration rate, email CTR, social engagement
- Messaging: "Here's why you should come" and "Don't miss out"

**Month 3: Engagement**
- Target: 80%+ of registered attendees actually show up
- Tactics: Reminder emails, content prep, networking setup, day-of activation
- Metrics: Attendance rate, engagement metrics, social sharing
- Messaging: "Get ready" and "Here's what to expect"

## Retention Strategies

### Immediate Post-Event (Days 1-3)
- [ ] Send thank you email with photos and recordings
- [ ] Publish session videos or transcripts
- [ ] Share attendee testimonials and highlights
- [ ] Activate social media mentions
- [ ] Thank speakers and sponsors publicly

### Short-Term Retention (Weeks 1-4)
- [ ] Send attendee survey and request feedback
- [ ] Facilitate introductions between new connections
- [ ] Create attendee community group (Discord, Slack, Facebook)
- [ ] Invite speakers and key attendees for next event
- [ ] Announce next event date and theme
- [ ] Feature standout presentations or speakers

### Long-Term Retention (Months 1-12)
- [ ] Monthly newsletter with community updates
- [ ] Host small monthly or quarterly gatherings
- [ ] Feature member spotlights and stories
- [ ] Create mentorship or peer groups
- [ ] Build tiered engagement (attendee → volunteer → speaker → organizer)
- [ ] Celebrate member achievements and milestones
- [ ] Surprise and delight loyal members

## Community Growth Tactics

### Viral Loop Design
1. **First Event**: Friend attends, has great experience
2. **Sharing**: Posts about event, tags friends, shares highlights
3. **Invitation**: Friends see post and ask for details
4. **Registration**: Friends register because friend recommended
5. **Repeat**: New friends have great experience, share, invite more friends

### Ambassador Program
- **Who**: Identify 10-20 most engaged community members
- **Responsibilities**:
  - Share event on social media and networks
  - Bring 2-3 new people to event
  - Help on day-of (host session, manage registration)
  - Provide feedback and ideas
- **Benefits**: Free tickets, recognition, first access to new events
- **Cadence**: Quarterly touchpoints and appreciation

### Referral Program
- Offer incentive for bringing 3+ friends (discount, free ticket, exclusive item)
- Create shareable unique referral link
- Track referrals and celebrate top referrers
- Make sharing easy (pre-written social posts, email templates)

### Partnership Development
- **Who**: Complementary communities, organizations, brands
- **Partnerships**: Co-host events, cross-promote, bundle memberships
- **Examples**:
  - Design meetup × Developer meetup = Design + Dev workshop
  - Local university × Community = Student speaker program
  - Corporate sponsor × Nonprofit = Free tickets for underrepresented groups

### Content & Social Strategy

**Content Calendar**
- Week 1-2: "Why attend?" messaging, speaker intro
- Week 3-4: Social proof (testimonials, past attendee quotes)
- Week 5-6: Session previews, speaker deep-dive
- Week 7-8: FOMO and final call, logistical reminders

**Social Posts**
- Mon: Speaker spotlight (bio, topic, why exciting)
- Wed: Attendee testimonial or past event highlight
- Fri: Social proof (registrations, trending topics)
- Sun: Reminder + direct registration link

**Email Sequences**
1. Welcome email (day 0)
2. "Here's what to expect" (1 week before)
3. "Speakers you'll meet" (4 days before)
4. "Last chance to register" (2 days before)
5. "Event details & logistics" (1 day before)
6. "See you tomorrow!" (morning of)
7. "Thank you + next event" (day after)

## Measuring Community Health

### Engagement Metrics
- Attendance rate (registered vs. actual)
- Session attendance and dropout rate
- Networking activity (business cards exchanged, social connections made)
- Social media mentions and shares
- Feedback survey completion rate
- NPS (Net Promoter Score)

### Retention Metrics
- Event recurrence rate (attended 1+ previous events)
- Churn rate (stopped attending)
- Referral rate (brought a friend)
- Volunteer rate (helped organize next event)
- Upgrade rate (became speaker, sponsor, organizer)

### Growth Metrics
- Month-over-month registration growth
- New vs. returning attendees
- Community list growth (email, social, app)
- Organic reach (not paid)
- Word-of-mouth referrals

### Community Satisfaction
- NPS: "How likely are you to recommend this event?" (0-10)
- Satisfaction: "How satisfied were you overall?" (scale)
- Content relevance: "Was content valuable?" (scale)
- Networking: "Did you make meaningful connections?" (yes/no)
- Would attend again: "Will you attend the next event?" (likely, maybe, unlikely)

## Building Inclusive Community

### Accessibility
- [ ] Event venue is fully wheelchair accessible
- [ ] Provide accessible parking or transit information
- [ ] Offer closed captioning for speakers
- [ ] Make registration form accessible (WCAG 2.1)
- [ ] Provide clear wayfinding and accessible restrooms
- [ ] Offer nursing/family rooms if applicable
- [ ] Ask accessibility needs in registration form

### Diversity & Inclusion
- [ ] Deliberately recruit diverse speakers and panel members
- [ ] Highlight diverse attendees and stories
- [ ] Enforce code of conduct and have trained volunteers
- [ ] Offer scholarships for underrepresented groups
- [ ] Recruit volunteers from underrepresented communities
- [ ] Partner with affinity groups and ERGs
- [ ] Review speaker lineup for diversity

### Newcomer Experience
- [ ] Have greeters at registration to welcome people
- [ ] Provide name badges and use pronouns
- [ ] Buddy system: pair new attendee with volunteer
- [ ] Host pre-event or first-time attendee mixer
- [ ] Create "first-timer" badge or recognition
- [ ] Provide orientation or map of venue
- [ ] Follow up personally with first-time attendees

## Hybrid or Virtual Community

### Hybrid Event (In-Person + Virtual)

**Design Principles**
- Virtual experience is equally valuable, not secondary
- Virtual attendees can interact with each other
- Virtual attendees can engage with in-person sessions
- Sessions designed for both audiences

**Virtual Engagement**
- [ ] Live Q&A from virtual attendees during sessions
- [ ] Breakout rooms for small group discussions
- [ ] Virtual networking rooms
- [ ] Chat moderators to surface questions
- [ ] Reaction emojis and polls for engagement
- [ ] Follow-up 1-1 meetings for connections

**Technology**
- Hopin, Luma, or Brella for platform
- Dual audio/video setup for seamless experience
- Multiple monitors for moderators/hosts
- Chat moderation and support team

### Virtual-First Community

**Building Connection Without Physical Space**
- Async community spaces (Discord, Slack)
- Monthly or quarterly live virtual events
- Small group video calls (office hours, cohorts)
- Shared documents and projects
- Member spotlights and interviews
- Leaderboards or badges for engagement

**Engagement Tactics**
- Weekly themed discussions
- Guest expert Q&A sessions
- Skill-sharing and teach-back groups
- Peer mentoring pairs
- Collaborative projects or challenges
- Member voting on topics and speakers
