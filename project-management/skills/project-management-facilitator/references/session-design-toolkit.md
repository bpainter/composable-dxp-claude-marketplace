# Session Design Toolkit: From Purpose to Payoff

## The 5 Ps of Workshop Design

Every session you design should address these five elements. They form the backbone of your planning.

### 1. Purpose (What Are We Actually Trying to Do?)
**Be specific. "Alignment" is not a purpose.**

**Vague**: "Team alignment workshop"
**Specific**: "Prioritize Q2 initiatives and align on resource trade-offs so we can move forward without ongoing conflict"

**Vague**: "Design critique"
**Specific**: "Give actionable feedback on the homepage redesign so the designer can revise with clear direction by Friday"

**Purpose defines everything**: outcomes, activities, invitation, timeframe

### 2. People (Who's in the Room and What's Their Context?)
Understand the dynamics:

**Who's attending?**
- Roles and functions (engineers, designers, PMs, stakeholders)
- Hierarchy (is the CEO attending? This changes everything)
- Perspectives (who agrees, who disagrees, who's on the fence?)
- Participation style (who talks easily, who's quiet, who dominates?)

**What's their context?**
- How much pressure are they under?
- What's their history with each other? (Trust level? Old conflicts?)
- What do they care about? (Deadlines, quality, being heard, belonging?)
- What are they afraid of? (Looking bad, wasting time, being overruled)

**Why this matters**:
- Agenda should be designed around *their* needs, not generic exercises
- You'll know how to manage dynamics (who to call on, where conflict might emerge, how to build safety)

### 3. Preparation (What Do People Need Before They Arrive?)
Great facilitation starts before people walk in.

**Participant prep** (send 1 week before):
- Purpose statement: "We're prioritizing Q2 work so we can move forward aligned"
- Pre-work: "Review the proposals and come with your top 3 priorities"
- Shared artifact: "Here's the budget and constraints. Come ready to discuss trade-offs"
- Time and format: "2 hours, in-person, bring your thinking"

**Facilitator prep** (day before):
- Review pre-work submissions (if any)
- Identify potential friction or misalignment early
- Prepare visual templates or agendas
- Test tech (Miro, FigJam, slides)
- Brief participants on agenda and time
- Identify who might dominate; plan how to invite quiet voices

**Room prep** (30 min before):
- Whiteboard / sticky notes visible
- Tech working
- Water, coffee
- Comfortable seating (not theatre style for discussion)

### 4. Process (How Will We Move Through Time?)
Structure the session with intentional sequencing.

**The pattern**:
1. **Open** (5 min): Purpose, norms, agenda
2. **Diverge** (15 min): Generate possibilities; silence space
3. **Share & Listen** (10 min): Bring ideas together; look for themes
4. **Converge** (20 min): Make trade-offs; prioritize
5. **Decide** (10 min): Land on recommendation; clarify owners
6. **Close** (5 min): Recap decisions; next steps

**Timing matters**:
- Provide more time for divergence than you think you need (people need silence to think)
- Convergence often takes longer than planned (it's where conflict emerges)
- Always leave time for decision clarity and ownership (don't rush the ending)
- Buffer 10-20% for tangents or unexpected themes

**Tools & Activities**:
- Silent generation (everyone writes ideas on sticky notes; no judgment yet)
- Share-out (round-robin; each person shares one idea)
- Themed grouping (cluster similar ideas; identify patterns)
- Voting (dot voting; each person votes on top priorities)
- Trade-off mapping (if we choose A, what do we sacrifice?)

### 5. Payoff (What Does Success Look Like?)
Define what people walk away with.

**Clarity on**:
- Decisions made (what did we agree on?)
- Owners (who's accountable for what?)
- Next steps (what happens next? When?)
- Timeline (when's the next checkpoint?)

**Document during the session**:
- Take photos of whiteboard / sticky notes
- Capture votes and decisions in real-time
- Assign owners publicly ("Sarah, you're leading the homepage redesign?")

**Send within 24 hours**:
- Decision record (what was decided, why, who's accountable)
- Action items (specific, owners, dates)
- Visual summary (photo of whiteboard or Miro board)
- Next meeting (if needed)

## Common Session Designs

### 30-Minute Status Alignment
**Purpose**: Quick check-in; no surprises before a larger meeting

**Structure**:
- Opening (1 min): "Here's where we're at"
- Updates (20 min): Each person shares top 3 things (2-3 min each)
- Blockers (5 min): "What do you need from me?"
- Close (2 min): Next meeting

### 90-Minute Strategy Session
**Purpose**: Prioritize initiatives, align on resource trade-offs

**Structure**:
- Open (5 min): Purpose, constraints (budget, timeline), decision rules
- Silent generation (10 min): Everyone writes top 3 initiatives on sticky notes
- Share-out (10 min): Post all ideas; theme them together
- Discussion (30 min): For top 3-5, discuss rationale, tradeoffs, risks
- Voting (15 min): Dot voting on final priorities
- Clarity (15 min): Assign owners, next steps, timeline
- Close (5 min): Recap decisions

### 2-Hour Co-Creation Workshop
**Purpose**: Design a new feature or service together; surface diverse perspectives

**Structure**:
- Open (10 min): Problem statement, design principles, constraints
- User research share (10 min): What do we know about users? What's the opportunity?
- Diverge: Sketching (20 min): Everyone sketches solutions silently
- Share (20 min): Each person shares their sketch (2 min); group identifies themes
- Discuss (30 min): What's working? What's risky? What's missing?
- Converge: Group prototype (20 min): Build one concept together
- Close (10 min): Next steps; who's prototyping; when's next iteration?

### 2-Hour Retro (Agile Team)
**Purpose**: Reflect on what went well and what needs to improve; identify experiments

**Structure**:
- Open (5 min): "We're reflecting so we can improve. All perspectives welcome."
- Well (15 min): What went well? (Sticky notes, share-out)
- Improvements (15 min): What could improve? (Sticky notes, share-out)
- Root cause (15 min): Why did good things happen? Why were there issues?
- Voting (10 min): Pick top 2-3 improvements for next sprint
- Commitment (10 min): "We will [experiment]. We'll measure [success]. Next retro, we'll see if it worked."
- Close (5 min): Recap experiments; appreciation for the team

## Facilitation Postures That Work

### Openness
- You don't know the answer; the group does
- Invite diverse perspectives
- Stay curious about different viewpoints

### Neutrality
- Don't use the session to advance your own agenda
- Give equal airtime to different perspectives
- If you have a strong opinion, share it as data, not as the answer

### Confidence
- You know the structure; you know how to guide it
- If silence emerges, stay calm; it's often where good thinking happens
- If conflict emerges, name it: "I notice we disagree on this. That's valuable data."

### Presence
- Bring energy without dominating
- Use body language to show you're listening (notes, eye contact)
- Ask questions because you want to understand, not to lead

## Reference: Agenda Language

### Opening Frame
"Here's what we're doing and why: [purpose]"
"We have [time]. Here's how we'll spend it: [agenda]"
"Here are some norms: Everyone talks, no one dominates. We're here to solve [problem], not win an argument."

### Divergence Prompt
"I want to open this up for ideas. Think big. We'll filter after we generate."
"Here's the question: [open-ended]. Take 10 minutes. Write ideas on sticky notes. No discussion yet."

### Convergence Prompt
"Let's look at what we have. What themes do you see?"
"Now let's get real. Which of these can we actually do? What are the trade-offs?"
"If we choose option A, what do we give up? Let's be honest about that."

### Decision Prompt
"We need to decide. Here are our options: A, B, C. Let's vote and see where we land."
"I'm hearing strong preferences. Help me understand what's most important to you."
"Okay, here's what I'm hearing as the direction. Are we aligned? [Thumbs up / sideways / down]"

### Ownership Prompt
"Who's going to take the lead on this?" (Wait for volunteer, not assignment)
"[Name], can you own this through the next stage?"
"Here's what needs to happen next. Who owns what?"

### Closing Prompt
"Here's what we decided. Here's who owns what. Here's our next meeting. Good work."
"Thank you for your thinking. This is progress. Next week, [owner] will have [update]."
